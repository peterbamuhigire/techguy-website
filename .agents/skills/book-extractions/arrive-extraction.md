# Devitt et al. - Arrive: A Design Innovation Framework

**Source:** *Arrive: A Design Innovation Framework to Deliver Breakthrough Services, Products and Experiences* (Devitt, Ryan et al., Routledge).

## Use this for

- Multi-dimensional innovation projects in established firms (not pure UI exploration).
- Discovery work where Strategic Suitability has historically killed otherwise-good projects.
- Compressing the full innovation arc into a 1–2 week studio engagement that hands off cleanly into Lean/Agile delivery.

## High-signal insights

1. **DVFS, not DVF.** Tim Brown's Desirability/Viability/Feasibility triple gets a fourth dimension: **Strategic Suitability** — the firm's portfolio fit, risk appetite, funding band, executive championship.
2. **Six stages.** Audit → Research → Reframe → Ideate → Validate → Execute. The two-R front end (Research separate from Reframe) is non-negotiable.
3. **Firm's Strategic Innovation Profile** is the load-bearing artefact. Capture strategic intent, risk appetite, investment band, capabilities, champions, time-to-deployment. Pin to the wall throughout the project.
4. **Reframe is the secret sauce.** Without a deliberate Reframe stage, research becomes a deck nobody reads. The Reframed Project Vision is the artefact that locks in scope.
5. **"Innovations cannot be all things to all people."** The frame must give a *compelling* solution to the *main* user, not an adequate solution to everyone.
6. **Concept Boards stay deliberately low-fi.** Near-realism makes users evaluate finish quality; low-fi makes them evaluate the idea.
7. **Assumption Map** = importance × evidence 2×2. Test the high-importance / low-evidence assumptions first.
8. **Validate ≠ Execute.** Validate de-risks assumptions; Execute proves *capacity to earn revenue*. Conflating them ships under-tested launches.
9. **FVS Sanity Index** at least monthly during Ideate. Desirability dominates, but obvious feasibility/viability/strategic-fit dealbreakers must not survive.
10. **Process is a de-risking tool, not a straitjacket.** Skipping stages is allowed but explicitly increases risk.

## Studio compression — 1–2 week version

Week 0: client homework (Initial Project Outline + Firm's Strategic Innovation Profile) + async desk research (PESTEL, Five Forces).

Week 1 (Audit + Research + Reframe): Mon Audit; Tue–Wed user interviews + live empathy/journey maps; Thu Affinity → Persona → Persona Needs Matrix → Reframed Vision; **Fri client sign-off on the new frame** (biggest gate).

Week 2 (Ideate + Validate + Execute-lite): Mon Ideate (diverge + converge to 2 concepts); Tue Concept Boards; Wed Lean Canvas + Pre-Mortem + Assumption Map; Thu Wizard-of-Oz / Paper prototype tests; Fri Concept Board (B), Business Case Dashboard, 2-page Execute Test Plan.
