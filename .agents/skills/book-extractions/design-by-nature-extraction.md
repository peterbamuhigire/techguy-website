# Maggie Macnab - Design by Nature

**Source:** Maggie Macnab, *Design by Nature: Using Universal Forms and Principles in Design* (New Riders).

## Use this for

- Choosing logo skeleton, layout grid, motion archetype, or icon family from the brief.
- Building brand systems with consistent DNA (one shape + one pattern + one symmetry + one ratio) propagated across logo, type, motion, illustration.
- Diagnosing why a design "feels off" without an obvious technical fault.

## High-signal insights

1. **Match form to message.** Corners = precision/authority/finance. Curves = flexibility/care/wellness. Mixing is intentional, not aesthetic.
2. **Five archetypal shapes — universal across cultures.** Circle (wholeness), Line/cross (relationship/precision), Triangle (aspiration/direction), Square (stability/durability), Spiral (creativity/regeneration).
3. **Six universal patterns.** Branching (delivery/networks), Meandering (ambient/exploration), Spiral (creative/lifecycle), Helix (binding/sync), Stacking (storage/finance), Radial burst (attention/celebration).
4. **Three symmetries.** Translation (rhythm), Reflection (settled/trust), Rotation (active/dynamic). Asymmetry is intentional choice, not imbalance.
5. **Black-and-white scaling test for logos.** If the form fails in pure black on white at 16px and 600px, no colour or effects will save it.
6. **Negative space is information.** Aim for roughly equal positive and negative space in any mark.
7. **Story before craft.** Every logo embeds one metaphor (Macnab's Agricultura: hand silhouette = "hand-grown," palm negative space = leaf, lifeline = stem).
8. **Phi is a sounding board, not a straitjacket.** Use the golden ratio to break ties; don't force every dimension into compliance.
9. **Self-similarity scales the brand.** Same DNA at favicon, app icon, business card, billboard. If you can't trace it through every collateral piece, the system isn't designed yet.
10. **Pattern recognition is pre-cognitive.** Civilization is 10,000 years old; species reflexes are millions. Aligned form = free comprehension; misaligned = user pays processing tax.

## Application matrix

| Brief signal | Shape | Pattern | Symmetry |
|---|---|---|---|
| Financial trust | Square | Stacking (hex) | Reflection |
| Tech-transfer / networking | Line | Branching | Translation/Rotation |
| Creative agency | Spiral | Spiral | Asymmetry/Rotation |
| Wellness / care | Circle | Meander | Reflection |
| Mobility / "we move you" | Triangle | Branching | Reflection |
| Editorial / magazine | (mixed) | Meander | Asymmetry |
