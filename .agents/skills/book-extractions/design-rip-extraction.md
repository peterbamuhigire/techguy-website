## High-signal insights

1. Typography, spacing, weight, and contrast create most of the perceived quality.
2. Dark is not black, and accessible does not have to look ugly.
3. Labels, placeholders, errors, and keyboard choice in forms are functional decisions, not cosmetic ones.
4. Buttons need explicit padding, hierarchy, shapes, destructive-state rules, and action-first labels.
5. Icon-only buttons must stay accessible with explicit labels.
6. Tables need readable spacing, alignment, sticky headers, and efficient filtering.
7. Selection controls, chips, and mobile navigation should reduce friction rather than mimic desktop widgets.
