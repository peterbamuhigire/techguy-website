## PART 1: THE CONTENT MARKETING FRAMEWORK

### Definition
**Content marketing** is a strategy for attracting, engaging, and acquiring customers. It entails creating and/or curating relevant and valuable content.

### Two Forms of Value Content Must Deliver
**a. Entertainment Value** — The audience finds the content funny, interesting, or entertaining
- Best example: Dollar Shave Club video — 13 million+ views because people enjoyed watching it; advertisement was secondary
- Side effect: improved brand perception ("they're cool")

**b. Educational Value** — The audience finds the content helpful; answers questions or solves problems
- Best example: Moz's Beginner's Guide to SEO — became the definitive resource; Moz's expertise displayed without being perceived as advertising
- Side effect: improved brand perception (helpful, expert)

### The Three-Stage Customer Acquisition Funnel
```
attract → engage → acquire
   ↑           ↑         ↑
"People"  "Audience"  "Customers"
```

- **Attract:** People see you, find you, become aware you exist → become your audience
- **Engage:** Audience gets to know you, start to like you, build relationship → become potential customers
- **Acquire:** Potential customers pay for your product → become customers

### Nine Ways Content Marketing Grows a Business

1. **Establishes authority** — Content displaying expertise improves perceived authority; a book is the strongest authority signal; a blog also works. People hire people who wrote the book.

2. **Builds relationships** — Readers on your blog are on your site, listening to you, potentially for extended periods. "I've met people who had been following my blog and as a result know a lot about me and feel like they know me." People buy from people they know, like, and trust.

3. **Reaches wider audiences** — People search for information, not just products. Per Google Keyword Planner: "productivity consultant" = 70 monthly searches; "how to be more productive" = 1,300. Content bridges that gap.

4. **Improves SEO** — (a) Google prefers recently updated sites; (b) content earns backlinks — people link to a useful blog post far more than to a sales page

5. **Leads to sales** — Sequence 1: Blog → newsletter signup → email nurture → product launch email → purchase (relationship built over 2 months). Sequence 2: Podcast → verbal product mention → listener clicks → becomes customer.

6. **Gets shared** — People share valuable content far more than they share product pages. Social sharing = free exposure. Word of mouth is still the primary recommendation mechanism.

7. **Is free or cheap** — Blogging costs almost nothing; only time. No up-front costs for most formats. Ideal for startups, consultants, authors on a budget.

8. **Is more targeted** — You can write "social media marketing for dentists" and reach dentists specifically. More precise than billboards or TV. Google Keyword Planner shows exactly who searches what.

9. **Builds an email list** — Email inbox = high-attention environment. Offer value in exchange for signup (ebook, white paper, or even just your posts by email). Email list compounds in value over time.

### The Two Most Important Content Marketing Rules
1. **Create content that's valuable to your audience.** If you do that, people will find you, share you, engage with you, return to you.
2. **Capture your audience.** Don't produce content just to produce content. Produce it to get results. Turn your audience into customers.

### The State of Marketing — Context
- E-commerce was only ~8% of total US retail in 2012 — massive room for growth
- Traditional advertising (billboards, TV, radio, cold calling): expensive, hard to measure, inefficiently targeted, increasingly ineffective
- People don't get sold anymore — they buy. Annoying people is not a good way to sell.
- Content marketing, social media marketing, and growth hacking are cheap, efficient, and effective

## PART 3: BLOGGING — COMPLETE STRATEGY

### 7 Reasons Blogging Drives Traffic
1. **Establishes authority** — Blogging on important topics in your space makes you an authority
2. **Customers like blogs** — People actively search for articles and blog posts
3. **Converts traffic** — Blog → leads → customers (more on this below)
4. **Is free** — Time only; no monetary cost
5. **Sustainable long-term channel** — Facebook ads come and go; blog posts stay indexed forever
6. **Warms up cold leads** — Someone who reads your blog before receiving your cold call/ad is more receptive
7. **Opens doors** — Speaking invitations, press mentions, partnership offers, LinkedIn group opportunities — all sourced from blog posts

### 3 Additional Reasons to Blog
- Writing forces you to articulate what you know and learn what you don't
- You become your own media company — tell your story your way without journalists
- Attracts professional contacts beyond customers: potential partners, hires, affiliates, journalists

### 2 Traffic Mechanisms from Blogging
1. **Search** — Every post is another indexed page; Google likes active sites; keyword matching drives organic discovery
2. **Social Shares** — People share interesting/useful posts; linked-to posts boost domain SEO

### 8 Ways to Get More Traffic from Blogging
1. **Be the answer to your customers' search queries** — Keyword research first, then write to match
2. **Write 500–1,250 word posts** — Customers and Google prefer this range; shorter = low value; longer = reader fatigue
3. **Write highly entertaining, valuable, or educational posts** — Know your audience; write "epic content" people bookmark, share, and link to
4. **Add images** — Posts with images stand out on Facebook and Twitter; they're visually appealing in the post itself
5. **Share on social channels and relevant networks** — LinkedIn for B2B; Hacker News for developers and entrepreneurs; find where your audience hangs out
6. **Add personality** — Be informative AND human; bleeds emotion onto the page; builds rapport
7. **Write consistently** — Minimum once per week to maintain habit and give returning visitors something new
8. **Use WordPress** — Easy to optimise link structures, meta descriptions, and URLs; Yoast plugin makes SEO management straightforward

### 2 Blogging Tactics to Convert Traffic into Customers
1. **Build a mailing list** — Someone might visit, not buy, and forget you. If they liked your blog they may subscribe. Email re-engagement weeks later converts at higher rates.
2. **Send blog posts instead of sales emails** — Use your blog content as sales enablement. Blog posts provide value, display expertise, and avoid "nagging sales email" fatigue.

### 12 Blog Topic Ideas
1. Write about your space, not your product (reach people earlier in the buying journey)
2. Tips and how-tos (actionable; high search frequency; clear value in the title)
3. Answers to common customer questions (if they asked you, others are searching for it)
4. Write for different customer segments ("social media marketing for startups", "…for dentists", "…for job seekers")
5. List the best resources in your space (those featured will likely share it)
6. Report on a conference you attended (valuable for non-attendees; signals industry involvement)
7. Summarise a book or research report ("# Big Takeaways From X")
8. Give your opinion on a recent news story or controversial topic (unique voice; opinions get engagement)
9. Browse Quora for questions in your field → write the answer as a blog post
10. Transcribe or summarise an interview you gave or received
11. Write a case study about a customer using your product successfully, or about a tactic in your industry
12. Interview an expert, customer, or potential customer (social proof + content creation; interviewee usually shares)

## PART 4: QUORA — TRAFFIC STRATEGY

### Why Quora Works
- Q&A site with extremely high content quality and active engaged users
- Acts as a search engine for many users — questions appear in Google results
- Answers are emailed to everyone following that question (email = high-attention inbox)
- Active topics include: Technology, Science, Business, Movies, Travel, Health & Wellness, Dating

### How to Drive Traffic with Quora
**Scout first:**
- Check question follower counts; questions with many followers = bigger potential audience
- Read the etiquette before answering

**Answer strategically:**
- Answer questions that your target audience is asking
- Answer questions whose answer delivers the same value your product/service provides
- Examples to look for: "What are the best resources to learn [area]?", "What are the best tools/apps for [niche]?", "How can I [benefit your offering]?"
- Do NOT shamelessly promote — deliver value; disclose affiliations per Quora's policy

**Optimise your profile:**
- Name and brief bio appear at top of your answer — edit the bio for the topic
- Link to your site in your profile
- Link to your site contextually within answers when appropriate (with disclosure)

**Use Quora credits:**
- Credits are earned by providing quality (upvotes, follow-ons to questions you asked others to answer)
- Spend credits to promote your content within Quora for more visibility

**Measure and iterate:**
- Check stats at quora.com/stats
- Performance builds over time as followers accumulate

**Quora Blogs:**
- Quora has a built-in blogging product; posts appear in followers' feeds
- WARNING: Republishing existing blog posts may trigger Google duplicate content penalties

## PART 5: SEO — CONTENT-DRIVEN STRATEGY

### How Google Ranks Content
Google's goal is to surface the most relevant and high-quality result for a searcher.

Ranking factors in order of importance:
1. **Keyword / Relevance matching** — Title, content, and meta must match search intent
2. **Endorsements / Links / Social activity** — Links = endorsements; Google +1s; Facebook shares; comments
3. **Popularity / Authority** — Page authority (individual URL) and domain authority (site-wide)

### 5 High-Impact SEO Tactics

**1. Authority Building**
- More views, Google+ "+1s", Facebook likes, comments, and shares → higher authority
- Authority has two levels: page authority and domain authority

**2. Link Building**
- Links = endorsements; a respected site linking to you signals value
- **Guest posting:** Write for blogs in your space; link back to your site contextually in the post body and in your bio
- **Press:** If you've achieved something newsworthy, pitch to media; most outlets have a news submission form
- **Create content worth linking to:** The most sustainable SEO strategy; as Google's algorithm improves, it will reward better content creators

**3. Keyword Research**
Using Google Keyword Planner:
- **Searches:** Higher Avg. Monthly Searches = more demand for that topic
- **Intent:** "buy [product]", "hire a [profession]", "[product] [size/spec]" = buying intent; "how to [activity]" = learning intent (great for books, courses)
- **Competition:** No competition = probably no money; too much competition = hard to rank; look for moderate competition from weak competitors (small niche blogs vs. NYT)

**4. Keyword Optimisation**
- Include target keyword in: headline, meta description, title tag, and anchor text of inbound links
- Match your domain name to your primary keyword if possible
- .com, .net, .org rank higher than other TLDs
- Country-specific TLDs (.us) rank higher in their country
- Domains with numbers are penalised; shorter domains rank higher than longer ones

**5. WordPress as SEO Platform**
- Easy to edit link structures, meta descriptions, and URL slugs
- Yoast SEO plugin: most important free SEO tool for WordPress users

### Intent-Based Keyword Framework
| Intent Signal | Example Search | What it Means |
|--------------|----------------|---------------|
| Research / awareness | "productivity" | Early stage; reaching them with content builds relationship |
| Learning | "how to be more productive" | Ready to consume educational content |
| Comparative | "best productivity tools" | Near-decision; comparison content converts |
| Buying | "buy productivity planner", "hire productivity consultant" | Ready to purchase |

People search for "how to be more productive" (1,300/month) far more than "productivity consultant" (70/month) — this is the content opportunity.

## PART 6: SLIDESHARE — PRESENTATION STRATEGY

### Key Statistics
- 50M+ unique monthly visitors
- 10M+ presentations uploaded
- Popular categories: business, technology, health, travel, education
- Mary Meeker's 2013 Internet Trends: 57% of 1 million views came from embeds in the first week alone

### Why SlideShare is Powerful for Marketers
1. **Internal search engine** — Users search directly within SlideShare for topics; keyword-optimised presentations get discovered
2. **Browsability** — "Related presentations" sidebar drives discovery from other content
3. **Shareability** — Built-in share button; viewers can embed on their blogs; embeds = massive reach
4. **Google ranking** — SlideShare presentations rank fairly high on Google search — additional entry points for your content
5. **Featured presentations** — Homepage features most popular presentations; promoted content gets additional platform exposure

### How to Get Traffic from SlideShare
Include links to your website in:
1. Your SlideShare profile
2. At the end of presentations (last slide with CTA + URL)
3. Within presentations (contextual slides)
4. In the presentation description

### 6 Ways to Get More Value from SlideShare
1. Publish slides rich with valuable, actionable content
2. Reformat other content (blog post, YouTube video) into presentation format — same knowledge, new audience
3. Make slides visually appealing — the format rewards visual design
4. Use the right keywords in title, slides, and description for internal and Google search
5. Promote on Twitter, Facebook, LinkedIn; embed in blog posts
6. Engage with other content (comment, follow) to increase your profile visibility

## PART 7: YOUTUBE — VIDEO STRATEGY

### Key Statistics
- 2nd largest search engine in the world
- 3rd most visited website globally
- 1 billion+ unique monthly visitors
- 500 tweets per minute contain a YouTube link

### YouTube's Three Structural Advantages for Marketers
1. **Search** — People search YouTube directly; your videos are like mini websites indexed within YouTube
2. **Google indexing** — Google owns YouTube; YouTube videos rank in Google searches
3. **Channels and subscribers** — Subscriber count affects ranking; you can private-message subscribers; subscriber growth compounds visibility

### 5 Tips for YouTube Traffic

**1. Know your audience**
- Does your audience prefer video over text or audio? Check how competitor videos are performing
- What kind of content does your audience want? Educational how-tos? Entertainment? Answers to specific questions?

**2. Produce amazing content**
- Valuable to your audience; high audio AND video quality
- Three video types that work:
  - **Presentation:** Record your screen while walking through a slide deck
  - **Demo/Tutorial:** Screen recording of step-by-step process (e.g. "how to get traffic from Quora")
  - **Record Yourself:** Camera facing you; great scenery adds interest

**3. Target the right keywords**
- Research using Google Keyword Planner; include target keyword in title, description, and tags

**4. Boost your search ranking**
- On-page factors: title, description, tags
- Channel authority factors: number of subscribers, views, likes, backlinks, comments

**5. Include links and CTAs in three places:**
1. In the video description (use full https:// URL to make it clickable)
2. At the end of the video (verbal instruction + text/image overlay showing URL)
3. Throughout the video (text overlay with web address; verbal mentions)

## PART 8: AMAZON SELF-PUBLISHING — AUTHORITY AND TRAFFIC STRATEGY

### Why Amazon Self-Publishing Works
1. **Authority** — A book is the strongest authority signal available; almost nothing else tells people you're an expert
2. **Money** — 25% of the top 100 Kindle bestsellers in 2012 were self-published; passive income after writing + promotion
3. **Exposure** — 100 million+ registered Amazon users actively searching for books
4. **SEO** — Amazon pages rank highly on Google; a book often outranks blog posts on the same topic

### Traffic Path: Amazon → Your Website
Include links in three places:
1. **Book description** — Viewed by more people than the book itself (the conversion funnel: views → readers); include URL and CTA
2. **Within the book** — Contextual links when relevant; author bio at beginning or end with site link; final chapter directing readers to your website
3. **Author profile** — Author page on Amazon; link to your site and other products

### How to Write the Book Faster
1. **Start blogging** — Break the book into small blog posts; get feedback iteratively; build audience before launch; email subscribers for launch day
2. **Combine blog posts** — Existing blog content can become book chapters (with significant editing and elaboration)
3. **Repurpose other content** — Transcribe Udemy courses, YouTube videos, podcasts to text
4. **Edit and elaborate** — Readers expect more than collected blog posts; add context, stories, examples; Amazon may penalise duplicate web content

### How to Sell More Books and Drive Traffic

**Within Amazon:**
1. Optimise keywords in title and description (Amazon is a search engine for shopping)
2. Get reviews — books with more reviews appear higher in search and are cross-referenced more; ask friends, family, colleagues
3. Run free promotion days — Amazon lets authors set book to free for limited days; free = more orders = better ranking; list on free book sites during promotion; social + email blast during free days

**Outside Amazon:**
1. Keyword optimise for Google (Amazon pages rank highly)
2. Guest blog with link to Amazon page
3. Content marketing: Udemy courses, YouTube videos, Quora answers, SlideShare, Podcasts — all linking back to the book
4. Public speaking: conferences, events, Meetups, university guest lectures
5. Get interviewed: bloggers (text interviews) and podcasters in your niche
6. Share on all social channels; email contacts with personalised emails (personalised > BCC)
7. Paid ads: Facebook or Twitter ads to the Amazon page for specific demographic targeting

## PART 9: PODCASTING — AUDIO CONTENT STRATEGY

### What Makes Podcasting a Great Marketing Channel

**1. Mobile** — Podcasts are consumed on mobile while commuting, exercising, or otherwise away from a computer; no other content format reaches people in those moments

**2. Separate search index** — People searching iTunes Podcast marketplace will not find your blog posts; Podcasting lets you reach an audience that prefers audio — a completely separate audience segment

**3. Less competition** — Far fewer podcasts exist than blogs; easier to rank and be discovered

**4. Deeper engagement** — Listening to someone's voice is more personal than reading text; builds stronger relationships with potential customers than blog posts do

### How to Start Podcasting Quickly
Equipment: computer + internet connection + microphone (minimum)

**4 content kickstart methods:**
1. **Video to audio:** Extract audio from Udemy or YouTube videos → publish as episodes
2. **Text to audio:** Use services like VoiceBunny to have your blog posts read professionally
3. **Reformat other content:** Talk about topics you've already written about
4. **Interview people:** Someone else does the talking; interviewee likely shares with their audience; great networking opportunity; fast way to learn from experts

### How to Promote Your Podcast
1. Content marketing on other platforms with links/mentions to the podcast
2. Mention in blog posts when covering related topics
3. Produce excellent podcasts (best promotion = quality)
4. Mention when speaking publicly (instead of Twitter handle or blog URL)
5. Ask friends, colleagues, and family to share on social channels

## PART 10: CONTENT DISTRIBUTION AND PROMOTION

### 9 Ways to Get More Views on Your Content

1. **Niche social networks — go where your customers go:**
   - Photographers → Instagram
   - Business professionals → LinkedIn, oHours
   - Apartment renters → AirBnb, Craigslist
   - Developers → StackOverflow, GitHub
   - Find industry-specific forums and discussion boards

2. **Share on major social networks:**
   - **Twitter:** "[Post title] [link] [relevant hashtags]"; mention people featured in the post; tag influencers who would benefit from reading
   - **LinkedIn:** Status updates (seen by connections and their connections on "like"); Groups (search for relevant industry groups, join, post with personalised commentary)
   - **Google+:** Status updates + Communities; Google +1s are the **second most influential factor** for search ranking (per Moz's Search Ranking Factors at the time)
   - **Facebook:** Personal profile + business page; posts with images stand out significantly; check Facebook groups for your audience's interest areas

3. **Distribution networks:**
   - Reddit: social news voting; most upvoted articles reach front page for massive exposure; ask friends to upvote
   - Hacker News, Quibb, Growth Hackers: startup/tech niche content networks
   - Find industry-specific link-sharing communities

4. **SEO:** Improve search ranking for sustainable long-term traffic (see SEO section)

5. **Produce on multiple platforms:** Blogs are not the only way to reach people; six platforms each with their own search index (Udemy, Podcasts, Quora, SlideShare, YouTube, Amazon)

6. **Gchat/Google Chat status:** Put your latest blog URL as your Gchat status — contacts see it under your name

7. **Email signature:** Update your email signature with every new blog post; Gmail hides repeated signatures — changing it makes it visible again to every recipient

8. **Email newsletter:** Opt-in for blog post delivery by email; display prominently on blog; mention at end of posts; every post publication = an email audience to notify. Tools: Mailchimp, Aweber

9. **Call to action at end of every piece of content:**
   - Check out related posts on similar topics
   - Sign up for the email newsletter
   - Buy your product or service
   - Schedule a free consultation call

## PART 12: THE CONTENT MARKETING ECOSYSTEM — REPURPOSING FRAMEWORK

### The Content Multiplication Principle
One piece of content → multiple formats → multiple platforms → multiple audiences

```
Blog post → SlideShare presentation → YouTube video → Podcast episode → Amazon book chapter → Quora answer
```

**Repurposing tactics:**
- Transcribe a YouTube video → blog post
- Extract audio from YouTube/Udemy → podcast episode
- Combine blog posts → book chapter
- Turn blog post bullet points → SlideShare slide deck
- Read a blog post aloud → podcast
- Use VoiceBunny to professionally read your blog post as audio

### Building an Audience Pre-Launch
1. Start blogging before your product launches
2. Give people the option to receive posts and updates by email
3. Build up following over months; when the product launches you have a captive email audience

## PART 13: METRICS AND MEASUREMENT THINKING

### Traffic is Just the Top of the Funnel
Traffic is the first step, not the goal. You also need:
- Optimised conversion funnels
- Strong copywriting
- Good design and UX
- A product people actually want

### Customer Lifetime Value vs. Customer Acquisition Cost
Every channel must be evaluated against: **what is my CLV, and what does it cost me to acquire a customer through this channel?**

Test multiple channels; iterate; find what works at a profitable CAC.

### Product/Market Fit First
Traffic won't convert without product/market fit. Signs of PMF:
- High referral rates
- Organic virality
- Users returning without re-engagement effort

### What Indicates High-Quality Content Performance
- Bookmarks / saves
- Social shares (especially to non-followers)
- Email newsletter signups
- Backlinks from other content creators
- Comments and engagement
- Return visitors

## PART 14: CONTENT MARKETING — KEY TAKEAWAYS SUMMARY

1. Traditional marketing and advertising methods are proving inefficient and increasingly ineffective
2. Content marketing is a great way to attract, engage, and acquire customers — start by understanding who your audience is and where they "hang out"
3. Produce high-quality content that is entertaining and/or educational, tailored to your target audience
4. Tap into platforms with their own search indexes: Quora, Udemy, Amazon, Podcasts, YouTube, and SlideShare to massively expand reach
5. Build an email list alongside every content platform — email is the most reliable re-engagement tool
6. Repurpose content across formats to multiply reach without multiplying work
7. Guest blogging is one of the most effective ways to build both traffic and backlinks simultaneously
8. Authority built through content compounds — books > courses > blog posts in terms of authority signal strength
9. Consistency beats intensity — weekly output over time outperforms sporadic spikes
10. Every piece of content must end with a clear call to action; don't let visitors just read and leave
