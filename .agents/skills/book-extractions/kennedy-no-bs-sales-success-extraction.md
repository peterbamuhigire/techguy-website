# Book Extraction: Dan Kennedy — No B.S. Sales Success

**Source:** Kennedy, Dan. *No B.S. Sales Success: The Ultimate No Holds Barred, Kick Butt, Take No Prisoners, Tough and Spirited Guide* (Entrepreneur Press, 3rd ed. 2004 / original 1994). 266 pages. Foreword by Tom Hopkins.

**Why this matters:** Kennedy's synthesis of 25+ years working with ambitious salespeople is the most direct, pragmatic inventory of *what actually works in selling* when stripped of academic theory and motivational fluff. The book's structure (23 Strategies + 6-Step Process + Takeaway Selling + Positioning-Not-Prospecting) is the backbone of Kennedy's GKIC (Glazer-Kennedy Insider's Circle) sales training.

> "This is simply a straightforward, relentlessly pragmatic, 'no b.s.' presentation of what REALLY works in selling. Not what should work. Not academic theories. What REALLY works." — Kennedy

## PART 1 — THE 23 STRATEGIES FOR EXCEPTIONAL SUCCESS

### Strategy 1: Ignoring the Word "No"

> "Nos turned into yeses — that's what master salespeople do."

A "no" is rarely a final verdict. Most firsts "nos" mean:
- "Not yet" (timing)
- "Not in this form" (offer mismatch)
- "Not you, not my fit" (positioning)
- "I don't know enough" (information gap)
- "I'm not the decider" (wrong person)

The discipline: keep the relationship alive. Fall-back prospects get systematic revisit every ~3 months.

### Strategy 2: The Positive Power of Negative Preparation

Patton's doctrine: rehearse everything that can go wrong. Before any presentation, list every possible objection, technical snag, competitive counter, personality clash. Prepare explicit responses. Confidence in the call comes from having already handled the bad cases in your head.

### Strategy 3: Use Listening to Influence People

> "How to read anyone's mind: listen hard enough."

Master sellers listen 70% and talk 30%. The prospect who corrects you is giving you gold. Specific techniques:
- **Active mirroring** — restate in their words
- **Strategic silence** — after they finish, pause 2–3 seconds; they'll keep talking and reveal more
- **Note-taking** — forces you to listen and flatters them
- **"Tell me more about that…"** — the most powerful 5 words in selling

### Strategy 4: Avoid Contamination

Beware the "grizzled old pro" who teaches you to cheat on expense accounts, shortcut discovery, pre-judge prospects, and assume what customers want. Office cynicism is contagious. Seek uncontaminated mentors.

### Strategy 5: The Process of Personal Packaging

Dress, grooming, car, business card, office, email signature, profile photo — all are sales copy about *you*. Every touchpoint either adds or subtracts credibility. Budget for your packaging as you would for advertising.

> "A well-dressed banker" — the original bankers' lesson: dress as your customer expects the trustworthy version of your role to dress.

### Strategy 6: Remembering Why You're There

You're not there to be liked. Not there to educate. Not there to "build the relationship." **You are there to close the sale.** Every other activity is either serving that goal or sabotaging it.

### Strategy 7: Do Expectations Govern Results?

Yes. Go into every call expecting the sale. Anxiety leaks through voice, body language, email tone. Prepare well enough that you have no reason to be anxious — then act accordingly.

### Strategy 8: Proof — The Most Important Tool

Forms of proof, in rough order of power:

1. **Testimonials** — specific, named, with outcomes and timeframes
2. **Pictures / before-after** — visual proof is worth 1,000 words of copy
3. **Statistical proof** — "847 clients, average result $X, over Y years"
4. **Third-party validation** — media coverage, industry awards, celebrity endorsement
5. **Demonstration** — live or recorded, Billy-Mays style
6. **Guarantee** — proof-by-putting-risk-on-self
7. **Client logos / association**

**How much proof is enough?** Slightly more than feels awkward. Under-proof kills far more sales than over-proof. **How much is too much?** When it stops being specific and becomes generic clutter.

### Strategy 9: The K.I.S.S. Principle (Fred Herman)

> "You can get rich making the complicated simple."

Jargon, complexity, and "salesperson-speak" kill sales. Close the Sales Prevention Department:
- No acronyms the prospect doesn't use
- No feature-dumps
- No tour of your process — tour of their outcome
- Yes-or-no questions > essay questions

### Strategy 10: Sell Money at a Discount

Reframe every dollar the buyer spends as a discount on money they'll get, save, or earn back. "$5,000 today to save $47,000 this year." This is the **Added-Value** sale:
- Added Value from Thin Air — bonuses, exclusivity, community, status
- Added Value at Nominal Cost — templates, checklists, audits, one-off consulting calls

### Strategy 11: Always Compare Apples to Oranges

Never let the buyer compare you to a cheaper competitor. Change the frame. Different bundle. Different guarantee. Different timeline. Different outcome measure.

> "Most of the advice you get about dealing with price resistance is wrong." — Kennedy

The right answer to price resistance is never to cut price. It is to re-sell the value, re-frame the comparison, add a bonus, or walk away.

### Strategy 12: In Search of the Free Lunch (Premiums)

A **premium** (a valuable bonus given with purchase) is one of the most under-used closers. Premiums can:
- Close the difficult sale
- Strengthen retention and referrals
- Enable "big ticket selling" at prices above market
- Be funded by the vendor/supplier (manufacturer co-op) — free to you

**Remarkable strategy of "extra added value"** — don't mention the premium in your initial pitch; introduce it at the last moment as an "since you're deciding today" bonus.

### Strategy 13: The Magic of Mystique

> "Perception is reality. The story is the magic ingredient."

Build mystique around you, your method, your process, your lineage. Techniques:
- Proprietary method with a name
- Signature process with a step count
- Lineage story ("I trained under…", "I developed this after…")
- Technology that looks impressive
- Magical demonstration — a single visual moment of "wow"
- **How to unmask and still create mystique** — reveal the method, but keep the artistry

### Strategy 14: Dumb and Persistent Beats Smart and Impatient

The "dumb frog" rookie who keeps hopping in outsells the veteran genius who gives up after two calls. Persistence compounds.

> "Resiliency is better than persistence." — Kennedy's refinement

Persistence says "keep pushing the same way." Resilience says "keep the outcome, change the approach."

### Strategy 15: Long Distance Is Nowhere Near as Good as Being There

In-person beats phone, phone beats video, video beats email, email beats text for closing ratios. Work up the stack as the deal gets closer.

## PART 2 — POSITIONING, NOT PROSPECTING (Strategies 16–17)

### The Paradigm Shift

Most salespeople chase prospects. Top earners **are chased by prospects.** The shift happens through positioning.

### Four Pillars of Positioning

1. **Writing** — articles, books, reports, newsletters in your industry's media
2. **Public Speaking** — conferences, associations, webinars, podcasts
3. **Publicity** — press releases, media appearances, interviews
4. **The Final Component — Expert Authority** — becoming the go-to in a narrow niche

> "Are you just another…?" If the prospect sees you as "another rep, another agent, another consultant," you lose. Positioning makes you "the person who wrote the book on X."

### Strategy 17: Lead Generation Advertising — The "Welcome Guest Process"

Shift from cold calling (pest) to lead-generation marketing (welcome guest).

**The Welcome Guest Process:**
1. Run a lead-generation ad (never a product ad)
2. Offer valuable information for free — book, report, checklist, recording
3. The prospect raises their hand for the information
4. You deliver it + follow-up sequence
5. The follow-up sequence pre-qualifies them and positions you
6. When they're ready, they call *you*

**Where to run lead-gen ads:**
- Trade journals
- Industry association lists
- Postcard direct mail (high open rate, low production)
- Online: Google, Facebook, LinkedIn ads → landing page → lead magnet
- **Headlines matter most** — 80% of the ad's success

**Force-info-give strategy:** design the ad so the prospect must provide phone + company + role to get the lead magnet. This qualifies them and hands you control of the subsequent sales process.

## PART 3 — THE 6-STEP NO B.S. SALES PROCESS (Strategy 18)

After all the setup, the actual sale follows 6 steps:

### Step 1: Permission to Sell

Don't start selling until the prospect has explicitly authorised the conversation. Signals of permission:
- "Yes, tell me more"
- "Sure, I'll take 15 minutes"
- "Okay, let's see what you have"

If you sell without permission, you're a pest. Ask for permission; if denied, gracefully retreat and re-position.

### Step 2: The Offer

Present the offer — specific, complete, with exact price, terms, timeline, deliverables. Most salespeople hint at the offer. Top closers *present* it with confidence.

### Step 3: The Presentation

Tailored to what discovery uncovered. Covers:
- How this specifically addresses *their* stated problem
- The process / deliverables
- Proof (testimonials, case studies, statistics)
- Hidden benefits (Strategy 8)

### Step 4: Emotional Logic

> "People buy on emotion and justify with logic."

Emotional hooks:
- Fear of loss (loss aversion >> gain)
- Desire for status
- Relief from frustration
- Hope for transformation
- Pride / legacy

Then layer logical justification — ROI, guarantees, comparison — so they can defend the decision to themselves and others.

### Step 5: Closing the Sale

Ask for the order. Not "what do you think?" but "when would you like to start?" or "shall I put you down for the X package?" The close is a question with a commitment built in.

Classic closes that still work:
- **Assumptive close** — "When should we deliver?"
- **Alternative close** — "A or B?"
- **Takeaway close** — "You may not qualify — let's see…" (Strategy 21)
- **"Does this make sense?" close** — Schiffman's version; humble and effective

### Step 6: The Morning After

Buyer's remorse is real. Within 24 hours of a sale:
- Send a welcome letter / email
- Confirm the decision with a third-party endorsement
- Re-state the benefits
- Introduce the next step ("here's what happens next")
- Ask for a referral OR a testimonial

Preventing remorse = preventing refunds = preventing lost referrals.

## PART 4 — WHAT SABOTAGES SALES (Strategies 19–20)

### B.S. That Sales Managers Shovel

- "Make 100 cold calls a day" (volume over quality)
- "Never leave without a yes or no" (forces premature pressure)
- "Stick to the script" (kills adaptation)
- "Discount to close" (trains buyers to resist)

### The Six Dumbest Things Salespeople Do

1. Product dump / feature-dump before discovery
2. Apologise for price
3. Talk instead of listen
4. Chase wrong-fit prospects out of desperation
5. Trade margin for volume without math
6. Quit follow-up too early

## PART 5 — TAKEAWAY SELLING (Strategy 21)

> "The most important single technique I've ever developed." — Kennedy

**The principle:** whatever you chase flees; whatever you pull away is chased. Deliberately **take the offer away** when the prospect wavers.

Mechanisms:
- **Disqualification criteria** — "This isn't for everyone. You only qualify if…"
- **Limited availability** — "I only take 10 new clients per quarter"
- **Walk-away** — "It sounds like we're not a fit. Let me refund your deposit."
- **"Are you sure?"** — after the yes, ask them to confirm they meet the criteria

### Case Examples

- **Starving dog trainer** — moved from chasing every prospect to a rigorous pre-qualification process; income rose, workload fell.
- **Real estate agent** — "I only represent buyers willing to commit to seeing at least 10 properties. Is that you?"
- **Kennedy's own practice** — "I don't accept new clients except under these conditions…"

### Power of Disqualification

Paradox: the more you disqualify, the more prospects want in. Exclusivity creates demand. **A Fundamental Choice**: you can be busy with bad clients or selective with good ones. There is no middle.

## PART 6 — SALES TOOLS AND TECHNOLOGY (Strategies 22–23)

### Tele-Selling (Strategy 22)

- Conference calls for group presentations
- 1:1 dialing for dollars (still effective, still despised, still works)
- Automated dialing / voicemail broadcasts
- Fax (historical but still legal notice-style in B2B)
- **Legal notice format** — pattern-interrupt envelope/email/fax that *looks* like official communication

### Techno-Selling (Strategy 23)

- Websites — most are product-brochure (fail); winning ones are long-form sales letters
- Email — treat subject line as envelope, first 2 lines as Johnson Box
- Automated online salesforces — cart, follow-up sequence, webinar replay
- Laptop / tablet selling in the field (demo-ready always)
- Contact management (CRM) — mandatory for follow-up discipline

## BONUS: HOW TO READ ANYONE'S MIND

Reduces to four practices:
1. Listen 70% / talk 30%
2. Ask questions deeper than they expect ("what would have to be true for this to work?")
3. Watch for micro-signals (eye shift, lean, word choice)
4. Silence after a pivotal question — let the answer expand

## SYNTHESIS — THE KENNEDY SALES PLAYBOOK

1. Stop cold-prospecting; start positioning (writing / speaking / publicity)
2. Build lead-generation machines that pull the right prospects
3. Refuse wrong-fit prospects via disqualification criteria
4. Land on prospects in order: in-person > phone > video > email
5. Follow the 6-step process every time (Permission → Offer → Presentation → Emotional Logic → Close → Morning After)
6. Stack proof; never apologise for price
7. Use takeaway selling, especially when prospect is wavering
8. Sequence follow-up until conclusion (20–50% response lift)
9. Build mystique around your method — name it, proprietise it
10. Prevent buyer's remorse the day after every close

## Uganda / East Africa application notes

- **Relationship-based market fit.** The "welcome guest" / lead-generation / positioning pillars align perfectly with Uganda's relationship-economy. Writing a book, speaking at KACITA or Rotary, appearing on NBS/NTV, being quoted in Daily Monitor — all create the positioning that collapses sales resistance.
- **Takeaway selling translates.** In a market where many buyers expect to negotiate downward, a clear disqualification stance ("we only accept clients who…") repositions you as preeminent.
- **Morning-after protocol** reduces chargebacks and refund requests especially on mobile-money impulse buys.
- **Lead-generation ads on Facebook/Instagram** (landing page → WhatsApp opt-in → 3-message sequence) outperform direct-sale ads 3–10× in UGX cost-per-acquired-customer.
- **Mystique** — Uganda responds strongly to signature methods with names. "The X-Step System," "The Y Method," "The Z Protocol" outperform generic service descriptions.
