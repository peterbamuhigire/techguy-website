# Kennedy And Wiebe Conversion Upgrade 2026

Internal synthesis of the reviewed EPUBs for `website-skills`.
Use this as an audit trail and implementation source, not for redistribution.

## Sources reviewed

- Dan Kennedy, *Speak To Sell*
- Dan Kennedy, *Magnetic Marketing*
- Joanna Wiebe, *Where Stellar Messages Come From*
- Joanna Wiebe, *Headlines, Subheads & Value Propositions*
- Joanna Wiebe, *Buttons*
- Joanna Wiebe, *The Great Value Proposition Test*

## Durable principles worth encoding

### 1. Start with market, message, and offer

- Kennedy's most durable contribution here is systems thinking.
- Website pages should not start as layout exercises.
- They should start by choosing the market, the message that matters most, and the offer being advanced.

### 2. Voice-of-customer is the real draft

- Wiebe is strongest when she forces the writer back into interviews, surveys, reviews, support logs, and testimonials.
- The most persuasive page language usually already exists in buyer speech.

### 3. Value propositions must be clear before they are clever

- A strong value proposition is unique, desirable, specific, succinct, and sticky.
- It should say what the offer is early, not hide behind brand theatre.

### 4. CTA copy must sell the next step

- Buttons should not describe interface mechanics.
- They should clarify the reward, lower anxiety, and reveal what happens after the click.
- Generic CTA labels should be treated as a defect, not a style choice.

### 5. Offers need the right page treatment

- Lead magnet pages should mostly sell the free resource.
- Consultation pages should mostly sell the quality and relevance of the conversation.
- Direct purchase pages should sell the outcome, terms, proof, and risk-reversal.

### 6. Objections belong in the page architecture

- Repeated objections should be handled before the user has to ask.
- Place proof and reassurance next to the claim or CTA that creates the doubt.

### 7. Authority should be taught, not merely claimed

- *Speak To Sell* reinforces a durable website lesson: authority rises when the brand teaches, diagnoses, and frames the problem better than competitors.
- Thought leadership should make the sales page easier to believe.

## What to reject or qualify

- artificial urgency
- empty slogan-style positioning
- vague CTA labels
- overclaiming without proof
- trying to talk to every possible buyer at once

## Repository changes driven by this synthesis

- harden message-mining requirements
- harden value proposition and CTA guidance
- push page planning toward market-message-offer logic
- keep contact and consultation pages outcome-led, not admin-led
