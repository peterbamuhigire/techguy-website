## Homepage Hero Copy Formula

Structure for the hero section above the fold. Fill in the bracketed fields for the specific firm.

```
Headline (H1):
[Specific problem or situation] in [City/Region]?
[What the attorney does about it.]

Sub-headline (P):
[Firm name] has [X years / handled X cases] helping [client type] in [geographic area].
[One Great Thing differentiator].

Primary CTA button:
Get Your Free Consultation

Secondary trust signal (beneath CTA):
[Rating / Years / Clients served / No fee unless we win]
```

## Practice Area Page Structure

Each practice area must have its own dedicated page. Do not combine practice areas.

### Required Sections (in order)

**1. Page Header**
- H1: [Specific Practice Sub-Area] Attorney in [City, State]
- Brief intro (2–3 sentences): who this page serves, what situation they are in, what the attorney does
- Word count target: 40–60 words

**2. "We Handle These Cases" section**
- Bulleted list of specific case types within this practice area
- Use client search language, not legal classifications
- Word count target: 80–120 words

**3. What Clients Typically Face**
- Acknowledge the emotional reality: what they fear, what they are dealing with
- 1–2 paragraphs in second person ("You may be worried about...")
- Word count target: 100–150 words

**4. What We Do for You**
- The legal process in plain English
- What happens from first contact through resolution
- No unexplained jargon; translate any legal terms immediately
- Word count target: 150–250 words

**5. Why Choose [Firm Name]**
- One Great Thing differentiator specific to this practice area
- 2–3 supporting trust signals relevant to this practice type
- Word count target: 80–120 words

**6. Frequently Asked Questions**
- 4–6 questions drawn from actual client questions for this practice area
- Each answer: 50–100 words, plain English
- See FAQ templates below by practice area
- Word count target: 300–500 words total for this section

**7. Practice Area CTA**
- Specific to the practice area — not generic "Contact Us"
- Example for PI: "Talk to a Personal Injury Attorney — Free Case Review"
- Example for family: "Schedule Your Confidential Family Law Consultation"
- Include phone number, tap-to-call on mobile

**Total page word count target**: 800–1,200 words per practice area page

### Keyword Placement Guide

- H1: [practice area] + attorney/lawyer + [city, state]
- First paragraph: practice area keyword in first 50 words
- Subheadings (H2, H3): include practice-specific terms and city once each
- FAQ questions: use exact phrasing from Google Autocomplete for this practice area
- Meta title: [Practice Area] Attorney | [City, State] | [Firm Name]
- Meta description: use the formula below

## Attorney Bio Template

Use this structure for every attorney profile page. Write in first person unless the attorney explicitly requests third person.

```
[Opening — 2–3 sentences]
I represent [client type] facing [specific situation].
[What the attorney focuses on and why it matters to the client].

[Why this area of law — 1 paragraph]
[Personal narrative: why the attorney chose this practice; a specific moment or case type that defines their commitment]

[Experience in plain English — 1–2 paragraphs]
[Not a case list. What the attorney has done for clients like the reader. Use specific outcomes where ethics permits, always with disclaimer.]

[Credentials — structured list]
- Bar admissions: [states]
- Law school: [name, year]
- Certifications: [list only bar-approved certifications]
- Peer recognition: [Martindale-Hubbell rating, Super Lawyers, Best Lawyers — current year only]

[Personal note — 3–5 sentences]
[Community involvement, outside interests, family — brief; humanises without oversharing]

[Direct CTA]
I welcome you to call or email me directly.
[Phone] | [Email] | [Schedule a call link]
```

**Ethics notes for bios**:
- Do not use "expert" or "specialist" without bar certification
- Case outcomes: include disclaimer "Past results do not guarantee a similar outcome. Every case is different."
- Bar admissions: list only jurisdictions where the attorney is currently admitted

## Testimonial and Review Format

### Named Client Testimonial Format

```
"[Testimonial text — client's words, unedited except for length]"

— [First Name Last Name], [Case Type or Situation]
[City, optional]
[Year, optional]

Disclaimer (required near all testimonials):
Client testimonials do not guarantee, warrant, or predict results in your case.
```

**What makes a testimonial convert**:
- Full name (never anonymous)
- Specific situation, not generic praise
- Mentions the attorney by name
- References the process, not just the outcome
- 50–150 words optimal

**What to avoid**:
- "They were great!" — too vague
- Anonymous or initialled names — low credibility
- Outcome-specific testimonials in jurisdictions that restrict them
- Composite or edited testimonials that change the client's meaning

### Composite Testimonial Rule

Do not create composite testimonials (combining feedback from multiple clients into one fictional quote). Many jurisdictions prohibit this and it creates ethics exposure. Use only direct quotes from actual clients who have provided written permission.

## FAQ Page Structure by Practice Area

### Personal Injury FAQs

1. How long do I have to file a personal injury claim in [state]?
2. What does a personal injury attorney cost?
3. What should I do immediately after an accident?
4. How long does a personal injury case take?
5. What if I was partially at fault for the accident?
6. Do I have to go to court?

### Family Law FAQs

1. How long does a divorce take in [state]?
2. How is child custody decided?
3. How is child support calculated?
4. What is the difference between legal separation and divorce?
5. Can I modify a custody order after it is finalized?
6. Do I need a lawyer if we agree on everything?

### Criminal Defense FAQs

1. What should I do if I am arrested?
2. Should I speak to the police without an attorney?
3. What is the difference between a misdemeanor and a felony?
4. Can a criminal record be expunged?
5. How do bail and bond work?
6. What does a criminal defense attorney do at each stage?

### Estate Planning FAQs

1. Do I need a will if I do not have many assets?
2. What is the difference between a will and a living trust?
3. What happens if I die without a will in [state]?
4. What is a power of attorney and do I need one?
5. How often should I update my estate plan?
6. How much does estate planning cost?

### Immigration FAQs

1. How long does the green card process take?
2. What is the difference between a visa and a green card?
3. Can I work in the US while my application is pending?
4. What happens if my visa application is denied?
5. How can a criminal record affect my immigration case?
6. Do I need a lawyer or can I file on my own?

## Meta Title and Description Formulas

### Meta Title Formula

`[Practice Area] Attorney | [City, State] | [Firm Name]`

- 45–65 characters maximum
- Front-load the practice area keyword, not the firm name
- Do not use pipes within the practice area description

**Examples**:
- `Car Accident Attorney | Chicago, IL | Smith Law Group` (52 chars)
- `Divorce Lawyer | Austin, TX | Garcia Family Law` (47 chars)
- `Criminal Defense Attorney | Miami, FL | Rivera & Assoc.` (56 chars)

### Meta Description Formula

`[Who you help] in [city] + [What you do] + [One differentiator] + [CTA with action].`

- Up to 155 characters for the primary display version
- Include the geographic area
- Include the primary CTA
- Write for click-through rate, not keyword density — it does not directly affect rankings

**Examples**:

Personal Injury:
> Injured in a Chicago accident? Smith Law Group has recovered $50M+ for injury victims. Free case review — no fee unless we win. Call today.

Family Law:
> Chicago family law attorney helping parents navigate divorce and custody with clarity. Free 30-minute consultation. We respond same day.

Criminal Defense:
> Facing criminal charges in Miami? Former prosecutor now defending clients 24/7. Free case evaluation. Call Rivera & Associates now.
