# Deal Structure Reference

## Pricing Architecture Overview

### The Four-Tier Offer Stack

Design your service offerings as a clear ladder. Every client should see exactly what they get at each level and the natural progression upward.

| Tier | Name | Price | What It Includes |
|------|------|-------|-----------------|
| Entry | Audit + Roadmap | $1,500 one-time | 47-point diagnostic, 12-month strategic roadmap, prioritised action list |
| Foundation | Website Build | $8,000–$15,000 | Strategy, design, development, SEO setup, launch |
| Growth | Website + SEO Retainer | $15,000 build + $1,500/mo | Foundation + ongoing SEO, monthly reporting, quarterly strategy |
| Premium | Full Partnership | $20,000+ build + $3,000–$5,000/mo | Growth + content strategy, paid ads management, weekly calls |

**The Audit as a Gateway:**
The $1,500 audit is the lowest-risk entry point. It:
- Generates immediate revenue
- Proves your diagnostic expertise before the client commits to a full project
- Produces a roadmap that the client either implements themselves (and struggles) or hires you to implement
- Converts 40-60% of audit clients into full project clients

### Proposal Structure

Every proposal follows this order:

**1. Situation Summary (½ page)**
"Based on our discovery call, here is our understanding of your current situation..."
- Their problem in their words
- The cost of that problem (quantified if possible)
- What success looks like

**2. Recommended Approach (1 page)**
"Here is what we recommend and why..."
- The specific strategy (not a list of deliverables)
- Why this approach addresses their specific situation
- What we will NOT do and why (shows discipline)

**3. Scope and Deliverables (1 page)**
"Here is exactly what is included..."
- Phase breakdown with timeline
- Explicit list of deliverables
- What is NOT included (scope boundaries)

**4. Investment (½ page)**
"Here is what this costs..."
- Project fee
- Ongoing retainer recommendation
- Payment terms
- What happens if they want to add scope

**5. Why Us (½ page)**
"Here is why we are the right partner..."
- One relevant case study
- One specific differentiator
- One risk-reversal (guarantee or performance clause)

**6. Next Steps**
"Here is what happens when you say yes..."
- Sign agreement by [date]
- Invoice sent [date]
- Kickoff call scheduled [date]
- Project begins [date]

## Contract Essentials

Minimum clauses for every agency contract:

**Payment terms:**
- 50% deposit before work begins, non-refundable
- 50% on launch (or split: 25% at design approval, 25% at launch)
- Retainers billed monthly in advance

**Scope:**
- All deliverables listed explicitly
- Change order process defined (written request → written approval → updated invoice)
- Number of revision rounds specified per phase

**Timelines:**
- Project timeline contingent on client providing content/feedback within [X] business days
- Timeline restarts if client delays exceed [X] days (clock-stopping clause)

**Intellectual property:**
- Full ownership transferred to client upon final payment
- Agency retains right to use work in portfolio (with client approval for sensitive industries)

**Termination:**
- 30-day written notice required for retainer cancellation
- Deposit non-refundable regardless of cancellation reason
- Project work completed to date billed at hourly rate if project cancelled mid-build

## Performance Deal Calculation Worksheet

Before offering a performance deal, calculate whether it is financially viable:

**Step 1: Determine client's average lead value**
- Average transaction value: $[X]
- Close rate on leads: [Y]%
- Lead value = $X × Y% = $[Z]

**Step 2: Project monthly lead volume**
- Current leads/month: [A]
- Expected increase from redesign: [B]%
- New leads/month: A × (1 + B%) = [C]
- Incremental leads/month: C - A = [D]

**Step 3: Calculate performance fee**
- Incremental revenue/month: D × $Z = $[E]
- Agency commission rate: [F]% (typically 20-50%)
- Agency monthly revenue from deal: $E × F% = $[G]

**Step 4: Break-even calculation**
- Cost to deliver (design + development + monthly): $[H]
- Months to break even: $H / $G = [I] months

If break-even is beyond 18 months: the deal is not viable. Negotiate a higher commission rate, a setup fee, or move to a retainer model.

## Upsell Conversation Framework

The right time to upsell is during the Seed the Vision quarterly review.

**Never say:** "We'd like to add another service to your package." (Sounds like a sales pitch)

**Instead say:** "To hit the goal you told me about in month 1 — [their specific goal] — we've identified that [X] is the limiting factor. Here's what adding [service] would do for that goal, and what it would cost."

**The upsell formula:**
- Reference their original goal
- Show evidence that a gap exists
- Present the solution as the logical next step
- Quantify the expected outcome
- Give a price

**Common upsell triggers:**
- Organic traffic growing but conversion rate flat → CRO audit + redesign
- Website performing but leads not converting → CRM integration + email automation
- Content published but not generating traffic → SEO content strategy upgrade
- Seasonal traffic spikes with no lead capture → Landing page and offer development
- Multiple services promoted on one site → Microsite strategy for highest-value service

## Churn Prevention Checklist

Run this checklist at the end of every month for every client:

**Green flags (client is healthy):**
- [ ] Monthly report sent on schedule
- [ ] Monthly call held (not cancelled or rescheduled more than once)
- [ ] Client responded to last 3 messages within 24 hours
- [ ] Results are tracking toward the 12-month goal
- [ ] Client mentioned the agency positively in conversation

**Red flags (intervention needed):**
- [ ] Client has not responded to last 2 messages
- [ ] Monthly call cancelled twice in a row
- [ ] Results have been flat or declining for 2 consecutive months
- [ ] Client mentioned budget review or company restructure
- [ ] Client asked to "pause" any part of the service

**If 2+ red flags:** Call (not email) the client within 48 hours. Ask: "How are you feeling about where things are? Is there anything we should be doing differently?" Listen without defending. Fix the problem they name, even if it's uncomfortable.
