# Expansion Offers Matrix

The catalogue of fixed-fee expansion offers the agency presents at post-launch
review milestones (per `post-launch-review-cadence.md`). Each offer is a
productized engagement — clear scope, fixed fee, fixed timeline — that grows
client lifetime value without random upselling.

These are not retainers (those live in `retainer-package-catalog.md`). These
are *bounded projects* sold to existing clients. Most are 4 to 12 weeks. Each
fits naturally as the answer to a specific question the client raises at the
30-day, 90-day, quarterly, or annual review.

Tzuo's *Subscribed* benchmark: in mature subscription businesses, ~20 per
cent of revenue comes from upsells and cross-sells. Without an explicit
expansion offer set, the agency cannot reach that number — every expansion
gets re-negotiated from scratch, and most never happen.

## Universal expansion-offer rules

These rules apply to every offer in the matrix.

### Pricing and term

- **All expansion offers are fixed-fee.** No hourly billing, no
  time-and-materials.
- **50% deposit at signature, 50% on delivery.** No exceptions for existing
  clients.
- **Fees are public** on the agency's services pages. No "special pricing
  for retainer clients" — the relationship is the discount mechanism (we
  prioritise retainer clients in scheduling, not in price).
- **Fees do not stack with retainer fees.** Each is a separate commercial
  decision.

### Scope and timeline

- **Each offer states what is included and what is not.**
- **Each has a fixed timeline.** Anything beyond the timeline is treated
  as a new engagement.
- **Two rounds of revision** included for design or content offers; further
  rounds quoted at $750/round.
- **No "as long as it takes."** Every offer ships when the timeline ends.
  Anything not done by then is documented for follow-up but the engagement
  closes.

### Where the conversation happens

- **30-day review:** offers 6 (Analytics) only; the foundation for everything
  else.
- **90-day review:** offers 1, 2, 3, 4 (the validation-driven expansions).
- **Quarterly review:** offers 5, 6, 7 (the data-driven expansions).
- **Annual review:** offers 2, 8, 9 (the strategic/transformative
  expansions).

### When to refuse the sale

The agency declines an expansion when:

- The client has not yet absorbed the value of the previous engagement (less
  than 60 days post-launch with no measurable signals).
- The client's stated goal cannot be reasonably achieved by the offer.
- The client is asking for the expansion to "give us something to do
  together" rather than to solve a specific problem.
- The client is in an underperforming retainer — fix the retainer first;
  expansion comes after.

## Offer 1 — Landing Page System

**Fee:** $4,500–$7,500 (1 page = $4,500; 3 pages = $7,500)
**Duration:** 4 weeks
**Best fit:** Client running paid traffic, planning a launch campaign, or
needing a campaign-specific destination page that doesn't fit the main site
information architecture.

### What's included

- Discovery call (60 min) — the campaign goal, the audience, the offer.
- Buyer-language research — 30 min interviewing 1 to 3 of the client's
  recent best-fit customers.
- Single landing page (or 3 pages) — long-form, conversion-engineered,
  designed bespoke around the offer.
- Hero, problem, solution, proof, offer, FAQ, single CTA — full long-form
  pattern.
- 1 form integrated to the client's CRM or email tool.
- Mobile-optimised, accessibility-checked, performance-tuned.
- Conversion-tracking event setup (pairs with Offer 6 if not already
  installed).
- 30-day post-launch optimisation: 1 hypothesis tested, 1 round of changes
  shipped based on data.

### Excluded

- Paid-traffic media buying (not an agency service).
- Copy/content for offer assets the page links to (e.g. PDF download).
- A/B testing platform fees.

### Conversion logic

A client who runs successful paid traffic typically returns 3 to 6 months
later for a second landing page system or graduates to a CRO retainer.

## Offer 2 — Multilingual Rollout

**Fee:** $6,000 (1 additional language) up to $15,000 (3 additional
languages)
**Duration:** 6 to 10 weeks depending on scope
**Best fit:** Established site, client expanding into a new geography or
serving an existing multilingual audience.

### What's included

- Translation workflow setup using the i18n architecture from
  `i18n/SKILL.md`.
- Language-specific content review by a native-speaker reviewer (per
  `language-standards/SKILL.md`).
- Hreflang tags, language-specific sitemaps, locale tags (per `seo/`).
- Language-aware Nginx routing.
- All cornerstone pages translated (home, services, about, top 5 articles).
- Multi-language navigation and language switcher.
- Per-language Search Console verification.

### Excluded

- Translation of the full blog archive (priced per article: $200 per piece
  in scope).
- Cultural adaptation beyond translation (priced as separate engagement if
  needed).
- Net-new language-specific content (use Content Growth retainer with a
  language scope).

### Conversion logic

A client expanding internationally usually returns within 6 months for the
language they didn't add the first time, or for content-engine localisation.

## Offer 3 — Content Engine Activation

**Fee:** $5,500 setup + ongoing Content Growth retainer ($3,000/month)
**Duration:** 4 weeks setup, then ongoing
**Best fit:** 90-day review where the client confirms commitment to
long-term thought leadership but doesn't yet have the editorial machinery.

### What's included (setup)

- 12-month editorial calendar built around the client's Big Domino (per
  `they-ask-you-answer/`).
- Cornerstone-and-cluster architecture mapped out — the 4 cornerstones the
  client commits to over the year, each with 6 to 8 cluster articles.
- SME interview process designed and run for the first cornerstone (the
  30-minute interview-to-draft workflow).
- First cornerstone article published.
- Editorial style guide for the client's voice.
- Author profiles set up.
- RSS, email subscription, and OG/schema infrastructure.

### Then transitions to

- Content Growth retainer ($3,000/month) for ongoing publication (per
  `retainer-package-catalog.md`).

### Excluded

- Net-new keyword research (included in retainer).
- Original photography/video.
- Translation.

### Conversion logic

The setup engagement is gateway to a 12+ month content retainer. Setup-only
clients typically return within 6 to 12 months frustrated by the absence of
publishing rhythm — the retainer is the right answer.

## Offer 4 — Lead Magnet Funnel

**Fee:** $4,500
**Duration:** 4 weeks
**Best fit:** Client with traffic but weak email list growth, or client
launching a thought-leadership asset (research report, guide).

### What's included

- Lead magnet asset design and production (1 PDF, ebook, guide, or
  benchmark report — content provided by client; we design and produce).
- Landing page for the lead magnet (uses Offer 1 patterns).

  ConvertKit, etc.).
- 5-email Soap Opera onboarding sequence (per
  `agency-positioning/references/expert-authority-framework.md` section 9)
  — written by us based on client's voice.

- 30-day post-launch reporting on subscribers acquired and engagement.

### Excluded

- Paid amplification of the lead magnet.
- Original research underlying the asset (priced separately).
- Long-term email-list management (use Content Growth retainer or refer to
  email specialist).

### Conversion logic

A successful lead magnet funnel typically catalyses an Authority tier
re-engagement focused on the broader content strategy. Or a Content
Growth retainer.

## Offer 5 — CRO Sprint

**Fee:** $6,500
**Duration:** 6 weeks
**Best fit:** Quarterly review when client surfaces a specific conversion
problem (low form completion, high cart abandonment, low signup-to-trial
conversion).

### What's included

- Initial conversion audit (week 1) — funnel analysis, drop-off
  identification, micro-conversion mapping (per `cro-audit/`).
- 8-question buyer-language research with 5 recent visitors who didn't
  convert (when reachable).
- 12 hypotheses generated and ICE-scored.
- Top 3 hypotheses designed as experiments.
- Experiments shipped and run for at least 3 weeks (or to statistical
  significance).
- Weekly experiment progress emails.
- Post-sprint report — what was tested, what shipped permanently, what
  was killed, what to test next quarter.

### Excluded

- Net-new page builds (use Offer 1 if needed).
- Backend/transactional changes outside UX layer.
- Ongoing CRO operations (use CRO retainer).

### Conversion logic

A CRO sprint that produces a measurable lift (typical: 8% to 25% on the
target metric) usually leads to a CRO retainer ($2,500/month) for ongoing
experimentation.

## Offer 6 — Analytics & Attribution Refinement

**Fee:** $3,500
**Duration:** 3 weeks
**Best fit:** 30-day review when the client realises they cannot answer
"which marketing efforts produced this lead?" or quarterly review when they
admit their reports are noise.

### What's included

- Audit of current GA4, Search Console, CRM, and email-tool
  instrumentation.
- Event taxonomy redesign (per `seo/references/analytics-event-map.md`).
- UTM convention documented and rolled out to active campaigns.
- First-touch + last-touch attribution model implemented.
- "How did you hear about us?" self-report field added to all forms.
- Custom GA4 reports for the client's monthly report needs.
- Looker Studio dashboard with 6 to 8 panels relevant to the client's
  business.
- 60-minute training session for the client's team on the dashboard.

### Excluded

- CRM implementation or migration (separate scope).
- Server-side tracking (separate engagement; rare).
- Ongoing analytics maintenance (covered under Care or SEO retainers).

### Conversion logic

This is often the offer that *enables* every other expansion — without good
attribution, retainer reports are unconvincing. We frequently sell this as
the gateway expansion at the 30-day review, then sell the bigger expansions
at 90 days once the data is trustworthy.

**Duration:** 4 weeks per sector
**Best fit:** Quarterly or annual review when the client has succeeded with

## Offer 8 — Authority Talk + Recording

**Fee:** $4,500
**Duration:** 4 weeks
**Best fit:** Annual review when the client is ready to move from publishing
to speaking — a senior partner or founder who has built a market presence
and now needs a flagship talk.

### What's included

- 90-minute interview with the speaker about the talk's central argument.
- Talk structured using the signature talk skeleton (per

- Speaker notes and Q&A prep.
- 1 rehearsal run-through with feedback.
- Professional recording session (we coordinate; client pays the
  videographer directly — typically $1,000 to $2,500).

- Article draft adapted from the talk.

### Excluded

- Speaker training beyond 1 rehearsal (refer to specialist).
- PR outreach for speaking invitations.
- Booking actual speaking slots.

### Conversion logic

A successful authority talk typically catalyses an annual retainer cycle of
4 to 6 talks with derivative content per talk — moves the client toward
Authority retainer tier.

## Offer 9 — Re-engagement at Higher Tier

**Fee:** $18,000 to $45,000 (Growth or Authority tier per
`agency-positioning/references/premium-website-offer-playbook.md`)
**Duration:** 10 to 14 weeks
**Best fit:** Annual review when the client's business has materially
evolved (new sectors, new offers, leadership change, scale milestone, M&A)
and the existing site no longer represents the firm.

### What's included

- A full new engagement at the appropriate tier (Foundation, Growth, or
  Authority — most common is one tier above the original engagement).
- See engagement-tier scope in
  `agency-positioning/references/scope-matrix.md`.

### Special handling for re-engagement

- 10% loyalty credit applied to the engagement fee for clients on retainer
  for 12+ months at the time of re-engagement (this is the only published
  discount in the agency's pricing).
- Existing retainer pauses during the re-engagement and resumes at launch.
- Existing analytics, content library, and proof assets are preserved
  where appropriate.
- Strategic frame is rewritten from scratch — re-engagement is not "site
  v2," it is "system v2."

### Conversion logic

A successful re-engagement is the highest-LTV outcome the agency can
produce — it resets the client's lifetime value to zero and starts a new
cycle of retainer + expansion offers. Aim for 1 in 4 annual reviews to
produce a re-engagement (Authority tier clients) or 1 in 6 (Growth tier
clients).

## Decision matrix — when to recommend which expansion

| Client signal at review | Recommended offer | Why |
|-------------------------|-------------------|-----|
| "We can't tell what's working" | 6. Analytics & Attribution | Data foundation precedes everything else |
| "We're running ads but the conversions are weak" | 1. Landing Page System | Targeted destination engineered for the campaign |
| "Our blog isn't producing leads" | 3. Content Engine Activation + retainer | Editorial discipline, not more posts, is the answer |
| "Our French/Swahili audience is underserved" | 2. Multilingual Rollout | Bounded project; clear deliverable |
| "Our list isn't growing" | 4. Lead Magnet Funnel | Specific asset + funnel + sequence |
| "Our conversion rate dropped" | 5. CRO Sprint | Hypothesis-driven cycle, not "redesign" |
| "We want to enter [adjacent sector]" | 7. Sector Page Expansion | Test the market before full re-engagement |
| "I want to do more public speaking" | 8. Authority Talk | Signature asset that generates derivatives |
| "Our business has fundamentally changed" | 9. Re-engagement | The site is no longer the right system |

A reviewer who cannot find the client's situation in this matrix should not
recommend an expansion at that review. Wait for the next milestone.

## Pricing logic across the matrix

All expansion fees follow the same value-based logic as engagement and
retainer pricing:

- Each offer's fee is sized for at least 60% gross margin.
- Each offer is priced at a value ratio of at least 5:1.
- Offers are public on the website's services pages.
- No offer is priced as a "trial" or a "loss leader."
- The 10% loyalty credit on re-engagements is the only published discount
  mechanic.

## How expansion offers feed retainer growth

Per Tzuo's PADRE model (see `subscription-economy-model.md`), expansion is
a structural function of a healthy subscription business — not an
opportunistic add-on. Each expansion offer is designed to either:

- **Catalyse a new retainer** (Offers 3, 5).
- **Strengthen an existing retainer's outcomes** (Offers 6, 4).
- **Expand the relationship's surface area** (Offers 1, 2, 7).
- **Reset the relationship at higher value** (Offers 8, 9).

Track for each expansion: (1) did it catalyse a retainer change within 90
days? (2) Did it improve the existing retainer's measured outcomes? (3) Did
it produce a referral within 6 months?

If 3 to 5 expansions in a row produce none of those three outcomes, the
expansion offer is not earning its place — pause and diagnose.

## Anti-patterns

- **Bundling expansion into the next retainer fee.** Destroys the
  commercial conversation; trains the client to expect free expansion.
- **Custom-quoted expansions for each client.** Defeats productisation;
  consumes founder time; introduces pricing inconsistency.
- **Recommending expansion to a struggling client.** Fix the existing
  retainer first.
- **Stacking 3+ expansions in a year.** Exhausts the client's internal
  capacity; degrades attention on each.
- **"Trial" or "discounted first" expansions.** Same problems as
  discounted retainers; signals weakness.
- **Vague success criteria.** Each expansion should have a stated
  expected outcome at proposal time, validated 60 days post-completion.
- **Forgetting the 10% loyalty credit on re-engagement.** Damages goodwill
  with long-term retained clients.
