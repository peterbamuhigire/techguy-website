# Agency Client Retention Skill

The difference between a fragile agency and a sustainable one is not the quality of the work — it is the quality of the systems. Every client relationship needs a defined structure: how it starts, how it's delivered, how results are communicated, and how the client is grown.

## The Rule of Five Ones (Nelson)

The foundational unit of a sustainable agency:

**5 clients × minimum retainer = monthly recurring revenue base**

This is your economic anchor. Before pursuing $20,000 projects, build the recurring base that makes you financially stable enough to take on premium work without panic.

| Stage | Target | Monthly Recurring Revenue |
|-------|--------|--------------------------|
| Survival | 5 clients @ $1,000/mo | $5,000/mo |
| Stability | 10 clients @ $1,500/mo | $15,000/mo |
| Growth | 20 clients @ $2,000/mo | $40,000/mo |
| Premium | 10 clients @ $5,000/mo | $50,000/mo |
| Seven Figure | 80 clients @ $1,000–$5,000/mo average | $960,000/year |

**The key insight:** A $20,000 website project is a one-time event. A $20,000 website + $1,500/month SEO and maintenance retainer is a $38,000/year client relationship. Always pair project work with ongoing retainers.

### Minimum Viable Retainer Design

Every service package must be deliverable profitably by your team at the stated price. Design serviceability before setting price.

**What a $1,500/month retainer includes (example):**
- Monthly SEO reporting and optimisation (4 hours)
- 2 blog articles per month (outsourceable)
- Monthly analytics review and client call (1 hour)
- Technical maintenance and updates (1 hour)
- Quarterly strategy review (Seed the Vision)

**What a $3,000/month retainer adds:**
- 4 blog articles per month
- Google Ads management
- Social media content calendar
- Bi-weekly check-in calls

**What a $5,000/month retainer adds:**
- Full content strategy
- 8 blog articles per month
- Monthly video content
- Weekly performance reporting
- Monthly competitor analysis

## The Land-Deliver-Retain Model (Nelson)

Every client relationship has three phases. Each phase requires different skills and systems.

### Phase 1: Land

Getting the right client at the right price requires positioning authority, not sales pressure.

**The 4-Step Discovery Call Process:**

**Step 1 — Connect (5 minutes)**
Build rapport. Ask one personal question. Find common ground. This is a partnership — it starts with a human connection, not a pitch.

**Step 2 — Diagnose (20 minutes)**
Ask questions. Listen. Take notes. Never present solutions before you understand the problem.
- "Walk me through what your current website does and doesn't do for your business."
- "What does your ideal client look like? How do they typically find you?"
- "What have you tried before? What worked, what didn't?"
- "What does success look like in 12 months? Give me a specific number."

**Step 3 — Prescribe (15 minutes)**
Based on what you heard, present your recommendation. Frame it as a diagnosis followed by a prescription.
- "Based on what you've told me, the core problem is [X]."
- "Here's what I'd recommend and why..."
- "This approach will specifically address [their stated problem] by [mechanism]."

**Step 4 — Propose (10 minutes)**
Present pricing as the natural next step, not a negotiation.
- "For what you've described, our approach would be [X] at [price]."
- Handle objections with content (see they-ask-you-answer skill).
- Close by asking: "Does this feel like the right approach?"

**What disqualifies a prospect:**
- Budget below your minimum (refer, don't discount)
- Unclear success metrics (no "what does success look like?" answer)
- History of 3+ agency relationships that "all disappointed" them
- Resistance to the process or timeline
- Expecting you to copy a competitor's website exactly

### Phase 2: Deliver

Results are the product. The delivery system must produce them consistently.

**Project Delivery Framework:**

**Week 1-2: Discovery and Strategy**
- Stakeholder interviews (understand the business, not just the brief)
- Competitor analysis
- Keyword research (for SEO integration)
- Conversion goal mapping (what should each page make the visitor do?)
- Deliver: Strategy document, wireframe outlines, content brief

**Week 3-6: Design**
- Present 2 design directions (not 3 — too many choices causes paralysis)
- One round of feedback
- Finalise design with brand standards applied
- Deliver: Approved design mockups (desktop + mobile)

**Week 7-10: Development**
- Build on CMS
- SEO technical implementation (schema, sitemaps, meta, speed)
- Content integration
- Cross-browser and cross-device testing
- Deliver: Staging site for client review

**Week 11-12: Review and Launch**
- Client review period (maximum 2 rounds of amends)
- Performance testing (Lighthouse score: 90+ target)
- Launch
- 30-day post-launch monitoring
- Deliver: Launched site + onboarding documentation

**Scope Management Rule:** Every change request outside the original brief goes through a change order. No exceptions. Scope creep kills project profitability and timelines.

### Phase 3: Retain

Retention is the most profitable phase of the client relationship. It costs 5x more to acquire a new client than to retain an existing one.

**The Three Retention Drivers:**

1. **Results** — The work must be producing measurable outcomes. Track and report them.
2. **Communication** — Clients who don't hear from you assume nothing is happening. Fill the silence.
3. **Vision** — Clients who see a clear future with you don't look for alternatives.

## Monthly Communication Rhythm

The #1 reason clients leave agencies is not poor results — it is poor communication. A client who doesn't hear from you assumes nothing is happening. A client who receives a monthly report and call stays even through difficult periods.

### The Monthly Report Structure

**Delivery:** Email on the same day each month, followed by a 30-minute call.

**Report sections:**
1. **Executive Summary (3 bullets)**
   - What went up this month
   - What we're watching
   - What we're doing about it

2. **Traffic Overview**
   - Total sessions vs. last month vs. last year
   - Top traffic sources (organic, direct, referral, social, paid)
   - New vs. returning visitors

3. **Conversion Performance**
   - Leads/enquiries generated (number and source)
   - Conversion rate vs. previous month
   - Revenue attributed to website (if trackable)

4. **SEO Progress**
   - Keyword ranking movements (top 10 target keywords)
   - New content published and its early performance
   - Technical issues identified and resolved

5. **What We Did This Month**
   - Specific actions taken (not hours logged — actions and reasons)
   - "We rewrote the Services page headline. The old version had a 68% bounce rate. The new version addresses the visitor's primary concern directly. We're measuring the change."

6. **Next Month Plan**
   - 3-5 specific planned actions with expected outcomes
   - Any client input needed

**Report length:** 1-2 pages. Not a data wall. Every number needs a "so what."

### The Monthly Call Agenda (30 minutes)

- 5 min: How is the business? (Non-website conversation — builds relationship)
- 10 min: Review the report highlights (not a read-through — discuss)
- 10 min: What we're planning next month and why
- 5 min: Any concerns, questions, or opportunities from the client

**The golden rule:** The client should leave every call feeling informed, confident, and looked after. Not sold to.

## Seed the Vision (Nelson)

The quarterly strategy session is the most powerful retention tool in the agency toolkit. It transforms a vendor relationship into a strategic partnership.

### Format (90-minute meeting, once per quarter)

**Part 1: Results Review (30 minutes)**
- Show the past 3 months of data
- Frame it against the original 12-month goal: "In month 1, you were here. You're now here. The goal is here."
- Show how their results compare to industry benchmarks: "The average conversion rate for [industry] sites in [region] is [X]%. You're at [Y]%."
- Let the data do the selling — your commentary should be explanatory, not promotional

**Part 2: Vision for Next Quarter (30 minutes)**
- Present 2-3 strategic initiatives for the next 3 months
- Each initiative must connect to a business outcome: "This content series will target [keyword cluster], which our data shows your ideal clients are actively searching. We expect [X] new organic leads per month by month 3."
- Show the 12-month roadmap — where are they now, where will they be in 3 months, 6 months, 12 months?

**Part 3: Alignment (30 minutes)**
- "Which of these initiatives feels most important to you and why?"
- Surface any business changes that affect the strategy (new product, new market, new competitor)
- Get explicit buy-in: "Are you aligned with this direction for next quarter?"
- Discuss upsell naturally: "To hit [goal], we'd need to add [service]. Would you like us to include that in next quarter's scope?"

**Why this retains clients:**
- Clients who see a clear trajectory do not leave
- Clients who feel heard and involved in strategy become advocates
- The quarterly review creates a natural upsell conversation — never a sales pitch
- Clients who have just approved a 3-month plan are not calling other agencies

## Performance-Based Deal Structures (Wardrope)

For premium clients who resist the upfront price of a $20,000 website, performance-based structures remove the barrier to entry.

### The Five Deal Models

**Model 1: Zero Risk Performance Basis**
- Agency: Gets paid only on results generated
- Client: Zero upfront cost
- Structure: 30-50% of revenue generated by leads from the website
- Best for: Businesses with proven unit economics (they know their lead value)
- Risk to agency: High (client must be able to convert leads)

**Model 2: Deposit + Commission**
- Agency: Receives a setup fee ($3,000-$5,000) + 20-30% commission on results
- Client: Reduced upfront risk
- Structure: Deposit covers design/development; commission covers ongoing value
- Best for: Mid-tier clients with budget but who want risk-sharing

**Model 3: Frontend + Backend**
- Agency: Setup fee at market rate + reduced monthly retainer + revenue share
- Client: Pays market rate for the build, gets reduced ongoing fee
- Structure: $X build + $500/mo + 15% of leads generated
- Best for: Clients who believe in the project but want skin-in-the-game alignment

**Model 4: Straight Retainer (Premium)**
- Agency: Full market rate upfront and monthly
- Client: Pays full price for the full service
- Structure: $15,000-$25,000 build + $2,000-$5,000/mo retainer
- Best for: Established businesses with proven marketing budgets
- This is the target model for a 7-figure agency

**Model 5: Deductible Deposit**
- Agency: Receives $5,000-$10,000 deposit, deductible from final price
- Client: Can walk away having only lost the deposit
- Structure: Deposit covers strategy phase; client decides whether to proceed to build
- Best for: Risk-averse clients who need to validate the relationship before committing

### When to Use Performance Deals

Use performance deals to:
- Land a case study client in a new sector (worth more than the fee)
- Close a high-value client who has budget concerns but strong business
- Differentiate from agencies who only take fixed fees
- Accelerate growth by removing the price objection on premium projects

Do NOT use performance deals for:
- Clients with no proven ability to convert leads
- Businesses in decline (you cannot rescue a failing business with a website)
- Clients with unrealistic revenue expectations
- Short-term engagements (performance models need 6-12 months to generate returns)

## Client Health Scorecard

Rate each client monthly on these five dimensions (1-5 scale). Any client scoring below 15/25 needs a retention intervention.

| Dimension | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| Results | Declining | Flat | Growing |
| Communication | No responses | Slow responses | Engaged |
| Budget security | Mentioned cutting | Stable | Increasing |
| Relationship | Distant | Cordial | Strong |
| Upsell readiness | None | Potential | Active |

**Below 15:** Schedule a retention call this week. Ask: "How are you feeling about what we're doing together? Is there anything we should be doing differently?"

**Above 20:** This client is an ambassador. Ask for a referral or testimonial video.
