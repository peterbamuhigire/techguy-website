# Post-Launch Review Cadence

The fixed cadence of structured reviews after a website ships. This is the
client-success discipline that converts a one-time engagement into a multi-year
relationship — and the early-warning system for retainer renewal and expansion
opportunities.

Without this cadence, the agency drifts into reactive mode: the client only
hears from us when there's a bug or an invoice. With this cadence, the agency
appears proactive, strategic, and indispensable — which is exactly what a
$3,000/month retainer requires.

This file pairs with `retainer-package-catalog.md` (what we sell post-launch),
`monthly-report-template.md` (what we deliver monthly), and
`expansion-offers-matrix.md` (what we recommend at each review).

---

## The five reviews at a glance

| Review | Timing | Length | Owner | Goal |
|--------|--------|--------|-------|------|
| Launch Day | Day 0 | 30 min | Founder + client lead | Celebrate, hand over, set expectations |
| 30-Day Review | Launch + 30 days | 60 min | Founder + client decision-maker | Confirm health; sell first retainer |
| 90-Day Review | Launch + 90 days | 90 min | Founder + client team | Validate outcomes; recommend expansion |
| Quarterly Roadmap Review | Every 90 days | 90 min | Account lead + client team | Set next quarter's priorities |
| Annual Renewal & Expansion | Launch + 365 days, annually | 120 min | Founder + client decision-maker | Renew retainer; seed next-tier engagement |

Each review has a fixed agenda, a fixed deliverable, and a fixed
post-meeting follow-up. None of these reviews are optional or moveable
without explicit written agreement.

---

## Review 1 — Launch Day handoff

### Timing

Day of launch (the day the new site goes live in production).

### Length

30 minutes. Held the same day, no later.

### Attendees

- Agency: founder + project lead.
- Client: primary decision-maker + day-to-day contact.

### Agenda

1. Confirm launch is complete (5 min) — site is live, redirects work,
   analytics is recording, search console is verified, all forms are
   submitting, all integrations are working.
2. Walk through the post-launch documentation handover (10 min) — admin
   credentials, hosting access, content management process, who to call
   for what, the client wiki/notion page.
3. Confirm 30-day review date and time (now scheduled in the calendar).
4. Confirm Care & Maintenance retainer start date if signed; if not signed,
   schedule a separate conversation in week 2.
5. Celebrate (5 min) — explicitly. Acknowledge the work the client did to
   get here. Send a small handwritten note within 48 hours.

### Deliverable to client (within 24 hours)

- Launch confirmation email — single page, what is now live, what to expect
  in the first 30 days, who to contact for what, schedule of upcoming
  reviews.
- Post-launch documentation pack (PDF) — admin credentials, content update
  process, contact directory, escalation procedures.
- Calendar invite for 30-day review.

### What to avoid

- Treating launch day as the end of the engagement. It is the beginning of
  the relationship.
- Leaving documentation incomplete because "we'll send it tomorrow."
  Tomorrow it doesn't get sent.
- Asking for a testimonial on launch day. Too early; the client hasn't
  experienced the value yet.

## Review 2 — 30-Day Review (the retainer-sale moment)

### Timing

Launch + 30 calendar days, give or take 3 business days. Schedule the meeting
on launch day; do not let it drift.

### Length

60 minutes.

### Attendees

- Agency: founder + senior strategist.
- Client: primary decision-maker (non-negotiable; if they cannot attend,
  reschedule).

### Agenda

1. **Health check (15 min).** What we have observed in the first 30 days —
   uptime, performance, traffic patterns, any issues identified and resolved,
   anything pending.
2. **Early signals (15 min).** Traffic, leads, conversions if measurable
   yet. Compare to baseline (pre-launch site) where data exists. Honest
   framing: 30 days is too early for definitive outcomes; we are looking
   for *patterns*, not verdicts.
3. **Client side update (10 min).** What the client has noticed internally
   — has the team been using it, have any clients/customers commented, has
   the sales team adopted the new content/pages.
4. **The next-90-day plan (15 min).** What we recommend the client focus on,
   what we recommend the agency does in support — including the **first
   retainer recommendation** (always Care & Maintenance; possibly one
   other based on stated goals at engagement). Use the decision logic in
   `retainer-package-catalog.md` section "When to recommend which
   retainer."
5. **Confirm next review and any action items (5 min).**

### Deliverable to client (within 3 business days)

- Written 30-day report (5 to 7 pages) — same structure as monthly report
  template, but expanded to cover the launch-to-30-day arc.
- Retainer proposal (one-page) for the recommended retainer(s), with start
  date and signed contract waiting on the client's side.
- Calendar invite for 90-day review.

### The retainer conversion goal

By the end of the 30-day review, the Care & Maintenance retainer should
be signed. Conversion rates from this conversation should be:

- Care & Maintenance: 90%+ of clients sign.
- A second retainer (SEO, Content, CRO, or Tech Support): 30%–50% sign at
  this stage.

If conversion rates fall below these thresholds, the diagnosis is one of:

- The retainer recommendation isn't matched to the client's stated goals
  (over- or under-recommending).
- The client did not understand the value of post-launch work during
  engagement (positioning failure earlier in the cycle).
- The 30-day report did not surface compelling early signals.

Each missed conversion should be logged in `project-log/` with the cause.
Patterns become evident across 5 to 8 clients.

### What to avoid

- Selling retainers cold without first establishing what the report
  surfaced. The report is the case for the retainer.
- Recommending more than 2 retainers at this stage. Overwhelms the client
  and dilutes commitment to the most important one.
- Discounting the retainer to "get the conversation moving." Pricing is
  fixed (see `retainer-package-catalog.md`).

## Review 3 — 90-Day Review (the validation moment)

### Timing

Launch + 90 calendar days. Most-watched review of the year.

### Length

90 minutes.

### Attendees

- Agency: founder + relevant specialist (SEO lead, content manager, CRO
  lead — depending on signed retainers).
- Client: decision-maker + 1 to 2 operational leaders (e.g. marketing
  manager, sales lead).

### Agenda

1. **Outcome validation (30 min).** The first definitive look at outcomes
   versus the success criteria agreed at engagement (see
   `agency-positioning/references/case-study-template.md` section 4 for the
   format of these criteria). Did the work do what we said it would?
2. **What worked / what surprised (15 min).** Both directions — what we got
   right, what we got wrong, what the client experienced that we didn't
   predict.
3. **Strategic commentary (15 min).** The agency's expert read on what the
   client should now do — informed by 90 days of live data and the original
   strategic frame.
4. **Expansion recommendation (15 min).** Based on the validation: which
   expansion offer (per `expansion-offers-matrix.md`) is the highest-leverage
   next step. Always one specific recommendation, never "here are five
   options."
5. **Forward roadmap and confirmation of quarterly cadence (15 min).**

### Deliverable to client (within 5 business days)

- Written 90-day strategic report (10 to 15 pages) — outcomes against
  success criteria, strategic commentary, expansion recommendation.
- Case study draft (if the client is willing) — see
  `agency-positioning/references/case-study-template.md` section "Case
  study production process."
- Expansion proposal (one-page) for the recommended next-step engagement.
- Calendar invites for the recurring quarterly roadmap reviews.

### The expansion conversion goal

By the end of the 90-day review window, 30%–50% of clients should commit
to one expansion offer (typically a second retainer or a focused
project — landing pages, multilingual, content engine, etc.).

This is the single highest-leverage commercial moment in the post-launch
cycle. Skipping or postponing the 90-day review is the most common cause
of stalled client lifetime value.

### What to avoid

- Treating the 90-day review as a "check-in" rather than a strategic
  conversation. It is the strategic conversation of the year.
- Failing to write the strategic report because "the client didn't ask for
  it." The report is the case for the expansion. Without it, the
  recommendation reads as upselling.
- Recommending expansion that doesn't align with the validated outcomes.
  If outcomes are weak, fix the existing retainer first; expansion comes
  next quarter.

## Review 4 — Quarterly Roadmap Review (recurring)

### Timing

Every 90 days after the 90-day review. Anchor to a fixed date (e.g. the
1st Wednesday of the relevant month).

### Length

90 minutes.

### Attendees

- Agency: account lead + relevant specialists.
- Client: decision-maker + operational leader.

### Agenda

1. **Last quarter's outcomes (20 min).** Run through the previous quarter's
   monthly reports as a series. What trended up, what trended down, what
   was learned.
2. **Retainer-by-retainer review (20 min).** Each active retainer reviewed
   on its own:
   - Was the contracted scope delivered?
   - Did the included quantities (hours, articles, experiments) get used?
   - What outcome did the retainer produce?
   - Is the retainer earning its fee?
3. **Strategic theme for the following quarter (20 min).** One stated theme that
   organises the following quarter's work (e.g. "convert the blog readers we
   built up last quarter into qualified leads"). One theme, not five.
4. **Resource and capacity confirmation (10 min).** Does the existing
   retainer set support the theme, or do we need to flex (expand, swap,
   or re-scope)?
5. **Action items and the following quarter's monthly cadence (10 min).**
6. **Open conversation (10 min).** Anything the client has not yet
   surfaced — competitive moves, internal changes, leadership shifts.

### Deliverable to client (within 5 business days)

- Quarterly roadmap document (3 to 5 pages) — last quarter's outcomes, this
  quarter's theme, this quarter's specific objectives by retainer, KPIs to
  watch.
- Updated retainer agreements if scope or fees are changing.

### The retention conversion goal

Quarterly reviews are the recurring renewal moment for retainers. A retainer
that survives 4 quarterly reviews has crossed the 12-month threshold and
typically becomes evergreen. Goal: 80%+ of retainers reach quarter 4.

## Review 5 — Annual Renewal and Expansion

### Timing

Launch + 365 days, then annually. Ideally aligned to the client's fiscal-
year planning cycle.

### Length

120 minutes. Often held in person or as a half-day if geography allows.

### Attendees

- Agency: founder + senior strategist + relevant specialists.
- Client: decision-maker + the leadership team (3 to 6 people).

### Agenda

1. **Year in review (30 min).** The full 12-month outcome story — traffic
   trajectory, lead trajectory, conversion improvements, content library
   growth, search position gains, revenue attributable.
2. **What we learned about the business (20 min).** The agency's
   accumulated insight from a year of working in the client's market.
   Often the most valuable section — the agency now knows things about the
   client's customers the client may not have surfaced internally.
3. **Strategic recommendation for next year (30 min).** What the agency
   recommends the client invest in next year. This is the moment for major
   expansion offers: a re-engagement at a higher tier, a multilingual
   rollout, an annual benchmark report, a sector expansion.
4. **Renewal of all retainers (20 min).** Each retainer's terms confirmed
   for the next 12 months — fees may step up here (annual review of fees
   per inflation, scope evolution, or value uplift).
5. **Annual case study capture (15 min).** Schedule the case study
   interview, testimonial recording, and any photography needed for the
   updated case study (per
   `agency-positioning/references/case-study-template.md`).
6. **Referral conversation (5 min).** Direct ask: "Who else do you know
   who could benefit from work like this?"

### Deliverable to client (within 10 business days)

- Annual review document (15 to 25 pages) — full year-in-review, learnings,
  recommendations, renewed retainer proposals, expansion proposals.
- Updated case study (published within 30 days).
- Renewed contracts for all retainers.

### The renewal and expansion conversion goal

- Retainer renewal: 90%+ of clients in year 1 renew for year 2.
- Net retention (revenue from existing clients including expansion): 110%+
  per Tzuo's *Subscribed* benchmark for healthy SaaS — applied to agency
  retainers.
- At least 1 expansion offer accepted at the annual review for 50%+ of
  clients.
- Referral generated for 30%+ of clients within 90 days of the annual
  review.

## What sits between reviews — the monthly rhythm

In addition to the five structured reviews, every retained client receives:

- Monthly written report by the 5th business day of the month (template:
  `monthly-report-template.md`).
- Monthly call (30 minutes) walking through the report, scheduled in the
  first 2 weeks of the month.
- Slack/email channel for ad-hoc requests within agreed SLAs (see
  `retainer-package-catalog.md`).

The monthly cadence is operational. The reviews are strategic. Both must
happen for the relationship to compound.

## Documentation and process discipline

Every review produces written output filed in `project-log/` (per
`templates/project-log-template.md`):

- Pre-review brief (1 page) — what we'll cover, what outcomes we expect,
  what decisions we need from the client.
- Post-review notes (1 page) — what was decided, what action items emerged,
  who owns each.
- Quarterly roll-up in `project-log/monthly/YYYY-MM.md` (the source for
  the `monthly-report` skill).

If a review happens but is not documented in `project-log/`, it didn't
happen. Documentation discipline is the single most-cited cause of
relationship breakdown when accounts churn after staff transitions.

## Renewal early-warning signals

Watch for these signals between scheduled reviews. Each is a churn
indicator:

- **Skipped or postponed monthly call.** Once is fine; twice is a signal;
  three times is an active churn risk.
- **Slow response to monthly report.** Report sent, no acknowledgement
  within 5 business days. Suggests the client is no longer reading.
- **Client absent from quarterly review.** Decision-maker sends a delegate
  or postpones twice. Re-establish direct contact within 2 weeks.
- **Client begins asking about contract terms or termination clauses
  unexpectedly.** Direct address required within 1 week.
- **Internal restructure** — new CMO, new finance director, agency
  consolidation initiative. Always reach out within 1 week of any internal
  leadership change at the client.
- **Sudden surge of out-of-scope requests.** Often indicates the client
  is testing whether the relationship has slack, in advance of a budget
  conversation.
- **Decreased SME participation in editorial interviews** (if Content
  retainer). The client has stopped investing time. Renewal is at risk.
- **Drop in the client's monthly enthusiasm.** Hard to quantify; note it
  in `project-log/`. If three months in a row feel quieter, schedule a
  founder-to-founder call outside the regular cadence.

Each early-warning signal triggers a written entry in `project-log/` and
an active recovery action — a phone call, a re-scoping conversation, a
strategic re-set. Churn that surprises the agency is churn the agency
ignored signals about.

## When to fire a retainer client

Per `agency-positioning/references/qualification-guide.md`, the agency
fires misfits — including retainer clients. The triggers:

- **Repeated payment failures** beyond the cure period.
- **Repeated scope abuse** after one written warning.
- **Disrespect of team members** — non-negotiable, immediate offboarding.
- **Strategic misalignment** — the client has pivoted to a position
  inconsistent with the work we do (e.g. moved to a sector we don't serve).
- **Net negative margin for 3 consecutive months** — usually a sign of
  scope drift; either re-scope or end.
- **The client requests work that violates our principles** (per
  `agency-positioning/references/expert-authority-framework.md` section 11)
  and won't accept the rationale.

When firing a retainer client:

- Give 60-day notice in writing.
- Provide a clean handover document including credentials, processes, and a
  recommended successor (where ethical to do so).
- Offer 1 transition call with the next supplier.
- Pay forward goodwill — the firing should be professional and the client
  should leave with the systems intact.

## Anti-patterns

- **Skipping the 30-day review** because the client "seems happy." This is
  the most common, most expensive omission.
- **Letting the 90-day review become a friendly catch-up.** It must be a
  strategic outcome conversation with a written deliverable.
- **Compressing the annual review into 30 minutes** because everyone is
  busy. The annual is the renewal-and-expansion moment; it warrants 2
  hours.
- **Reporting outputs (work delivered) instead of outcomes (what changed
  for the client).** See `monthly-report-template.md` — every section
  must explain what changed for the client's business, not what we did.
- **Recommending expansion based on what the agency wants to sell** rather
  than what the client's data suggests they need next. The client smells
  this immediately.
- **Failing to get the case study captured at the annual review.** Twelve
  months in, the data is rich and the client has perspective. Six months
  later, both have faded.
- **Treating quarterly reviews as the primary commercial moment.** They
  are renewal moments, not expansion moments. The 30-day, 90-day, and
  annual are the expansion moments.
