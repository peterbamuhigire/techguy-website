# Retainer Package Catalog

The five productized retainer packages the agency offers after a website launch.
Every Foundation/Growth/Authority engagement (see
`agency-positioning/references/premium-website-offer-playbook.md`) is followed by
a retainer recommendation. The retainer is not optional pricing theatre — it is
how the agency earns recurring revenue and how the client compounds the value of
the build.

This catalog is the agency's commercial answer to the question: *"What happens
after launch?"* If we cannot answer that with a productized offer, every client
defaults to "ad-hoc fix when something breaks" — the lowest-margin, highest-
churn relationship there is.

The pricing in this file aligns with the agency narrative ($12k floor, value-
based pricing, no hourly billing; see `agency-positioning/references/`).

## The five retainers at a glance

| # | Retainer | Monthly fee | Min term | Best fit | Primary outcome |
|---|----------|-------------|----------|----------|-----------------|
| 1 | Care & Maintenance | $750 | 6 months | Every Foundation+ client | Site stays healthy, secure, fast |
| 2 | SEO Foundation + Reporting | $1,800 | 12 months | Growth+ clients with search demand | Rankings + qualified organic traffic |
| 3 | Content Growth | $3,000 | 12 months | Authority clients building authority | Cornerstone + cluster publishing rhythm |
| 4 | Conversion Improvement (CRO) | $2,500 | 6 months | Sites with measurable traffic and weak conversion | Higher conversion rate, more leads |
| 5 | Technical Support & Enhancements | $1,500 | 6 months | Clients with active feature requests | Iteration without re-engagement fees |

A single client can carry one retainer or several. The most common stack:
**Care & Maintenance + one of (SEO, Content, CRO)**. The agency does not sell
all five to one client — that is unfocused and expensive without producing more
outcome.

Annual prepay receives 1 month free (12 months for the price of 11). Quarterly
prepay receives no discount — quarterly is administrative, not commercial.

## Universal retainer rules (apply to all 5 packages)

These rules govern every retainer. They are stated in writing in every retainer
agreement.

### Pricing and term

- **Monthly fee paid in advance, on the 1st of the month.** No invoicing
  delays.
- **Minimum term** as stated per package (6 or 12 months). After the minimum
  term, retainer becomes month-to-month with 60-day written notice to cancel.
- **Annual prepay discount:** 1 month free (8.3% effective discount).
- **No partial months.** Engagements start on the 1st of the month after
  contract signing.
- **No paused months.** A retainer is not pausable. Cancel and re-onboard if
  needed (re-onboarding is billed at $750).

### Scope and exclusions

- **Each retainer states what is included and what is not.** Anything not
  listed in the "Includes" section is out of scope.
- **No "unlimited" anything.** Unlimited support, unlimited revisions,
  unlimited requests — all forbidden. Specific quantities (5 hours, 3
  articles, 2 experiments) only.
- **Out-of-scope work** is quoted separately as a fixed-fee project or as an
  expansion offer (see `expansion-offers-matrix.md`).
- **Carryover of unused work:** None. If the client doesn't use the included
  hours/articles/experiments in a month, they expire. Retainers are
  capacity reservations, not banks.

### Communication

- **Monthly report** delivered by the 5th business day of the following
  month (see `monthly-report-template.md`).
- **Monthly call** (30 minutes) scheduled within the first 2 weeks of each
  month, walking through the report.
- **Slack/email response window:** 1 business day for non-urgent requests; 4
  business hours for issues blocking client business.
- **No after-hours support** unless explicitly contracted (Care &
  Maintenance Plus tier, +$500/month).
- **Quarterly strategy review** (90 minutes) every 3 months — see
  `post-launch-review-cadence.md`.

### What kills a retainer

The agency reserves the right to terminate any retainer for:

- Non-payment (after 14-day cure period).
- Repeated scope-violation requests after one written warning.
- Disrespect of team members.
- Material change in the client's business that makes the retainer no longer
  the right service.

The client may terminate at any time with 60-day written notice (after the
minimum term). The agency works the notice period in good faith and provides
a clean handover.

### What we never do in a retainer

- We do not sell a retainer to a client who didn't pay full engagement fee.
  Retainers compound value built in the engagement; without that foundation
  there is nothing to retain.
- We do not bundle retainers into the engagement fee. The retainer is a
  separate commercial decision made at the right post-launch moment (the
  30-day review).
- We do not do "trial months." Either the retainer is the right service or
  it isn't.
- We do not pro-rate retainer hours that the client didn't use.
- We do not allow clients to "switch" retainers monthly. A retainer change
  requires a new contract and 30 days' notice.

## Retainer 1 — Care & Maintenance

**Fee:** $750/month
**Minimum term:** 6 months
**Best fit:** Every site we ship, regardless of tier.

### What it does

Keeps the site healthy, secure, fast, and current. Prevents the slow decay
that turns a launch-day-perfect site into a 6-month-later embarrassment.

### What's included

- Hosting management (where we host) or hosting liaison (where the client
  hosts) — including SSL renewal, DNS management, uptime monitoring.
- Software updates (CMS, plugins, dependencies) every month, applied in a
  staging environment first.
- Security monitoring — daily backups (retained 30 days), malware scans,
  WAF rules where applicable.
- Performance monitoring — monthly Lighthouse + Core Web Vitals report,
  alerts when scores drop below threshold.
- Up to **2 hours of small content edits per month** (text changes, image
  swaps, contact-info updates). Not new pages, not feature additions.
- Monthly health report (1 page, sent by email).

### SLA

- Uptime monitoring: every 5 minutes.
- Critical security patch: applied within 24 hours of vendor release.
- Backup recovery: site restored within 4 business hours of request.
- Small content edit: turnaround within 2 business days.
- Site-down incident: response within 4 business hours; resolution effort
  begins immediately.

### Communication cadence

- Monthly health report.
- Quarterly strategy check-in (15 minutes — short for this retainer).
- Slack/email channel for ad-hoc requests.

### Exclusions

- New pages, new sections, new features (use Technical Support retainer or
  expansion offer).
- Content writing or strategy (use Content Growth retainer).
- Marketing, SEO work, analytics analysis (use SEO retainer).
- After-hours emergency response (available as +$500/month add-on).
- Custom development of new functionality (project work).

### Upsell path

After 60 days on Care & Maintenance, present the relevant additional
retainer based on the client's stated goals:

- Client wants more traffic → SEO Foundation retainer.
- Client wants to publish authority content → Content Growth retainer.
- Client mentions specific conversion goals → CRO retainer.
- Client is iterating product/services → Technical Support retainer.

## Retainer 2 — SEO Foundation + Reporting

**Fee:** $1,800/month
**Minimum term:** 12 months
**Best fit:** Growth and Authority tier clients in sectors with meaningful
search demand.

### What it does

Establishes and maintains the technical and content foundations for organic
search visibility, then reports on what is working and what needs to change.

### What's included

- Monthly technical SEO audit (crawl, indexation, schema validation, Core
  Web Vitals, internal link health).
- Monthly keyword position monitoring on the agreed target query set
  (typically 30 to 60 queries).
- Backlink monitoring and disavow management.
- Schema markup updates as new pages launch or as Google's schema
  recommendations evolve.
- Monthly content recommendations — 3 to 5 specific article briefs based on
  query gap analysis.
- Quarterly cornerstone refresh — 1 cornerstone article updated per quarter
  to reclaim or hold position.
- Sitemap and robots.txt management.
- Search Console + GA4 monitoring; alerts on traffic anomalies.
- Monthly written report walking through what changed, why it changed, and
  what we are doing about it.
- Monthly strategy call (30 minutes).

### SLA

- Monthly report by the 5th business day of each month.
- Critical SEO issue (de-indexation, ranking drop >50% on a target query):
  diagnostic within 1 business day, recovery plan within 3 business days.
- Article brief turnaround: 5 business days from request.

### Communication cadence

- Monthly report and call.
- Quarterly strategy review (90 minutes).
- Slack/email channel for ad-hoc questions.

### Exclusions

- Content writing (use Content Growth retainer or commission per article).
- Paid search management (separate retainer / not currently offered).
- Local SEO citation building (available as one-time project).
- Penalty recovery from past black-hat work (requires audit + project).
- Translation/multilingual SEO maintenance (available as multilingual
  rollout expansion offer).

### Upsell path

- Client wants to act on the article briefs → Content Growth retainer.
- Client wants to add new sectors → multilingual or sector expansion offer.
- Client wants paid search → refer to a paid-search specialist partner.

## Retainer 3 — Content Growth

**Fee:** $3,000/month
**Minimum term:** 12 months
**Best fit:** Authority tier clients pursuing long-term sector authority.

### What it does

Executes the cornerstone-and-cluster publishing rhythm (per
`they-ask-you-answer/references/agency-content-plan.md` and
`editorial-calendar-and-rhythm.md`) on the client's behalf.

### What's included

- 1 cornerstone article per quarter (3,000 to 6,000 words, with custom
  illustrations and embedded video where relevant).
- 4 cluster articles per month (1,200 to 2,500 words each).
- All articles include: SME interview (the 30-minute interview-to-draft
  workflow), draft, edit, fact-check, OG image, schema markup, internal
  linking.
- Editorial calendar maintained 60 days ahead.
- Monthly content performance report — what published, what is ranking,
  what is converting.
- Quarterly content strategy session (90 minutes).
- One signature talk recording or refresh per year (Authority tier only).

### SLA

- Editorial calendar refreshed monthly.
- Article brief approval needed from client SME within 5 business days
  (otherwise we proceed with our brief).
- Article first draft within 10 business days of brief approval.
- Article published within 20 business days of brief approval.
- Cornerstone first draft within 30 business days; published within 45.

### Communication cadence

- Weekly editorial standup (15 minutes — for the SME interview slot).
- Monthly content performance report and call.
- Quarterly content strategy review.

### Exclusions

- Original photography or video production (commissioned separately when
  needed).
- Distribution beyond the client's owned channels — paid amplification, PR
  outreach, podcast guesting (separate offers).
- Translation of articles into additional languages (multilingual rollout
  expansion offer).
- Articles requiring more than 1 hour of SME interview time per piece (the
  workflow assumes the 30-min interview baseline).
- Articles in technical specialisms outside the agency's core competence
  (declined, not bundled).

### Upsell path

- Client demand for translated content → multilingual expansion offer.
- Client wants distribution help → paid LinkedIn or email-list growth
  retainer (Authority tier add-on).
- Client wants to repurpose into video at scale → in-house video expansion
  offer.

## Retainer 4 — Conversion Improvement (CRO)

**Fee:** $2,500/month
**Minimum term:** 6 months
**Best fit:** Sites with at least 2,000 monthly sessions and an articulated
conversion goal that is currently underperforming.

### What it does

Runs a structured experimentation cycle to improve conversion rate on the
pages and journeys that matter most. Replaces "guessing what to change" with
hypothesis-driven testing.

### What's included

- Initial CRO baseline assessment (first month) — funnel analysis, drop-off
  identification, micro-conversion mapping.
- 1 prioritised hypothesis backlog maintained ongoingly (ICE-scored — see
  `cro-audit/`).
- 2 experiments designed and shipped per month — copy changes, layout
  changes, form changes, CTA changes, page additions.
- Each experiment runs to statistical significance or to 30 days, whichever
  comes first.
- Heatmap and session-recording analysis (Hotjar/Microsoft Clarity).
- Monthly experiment report — what we tested, results, what we are
  shipping permanently, what we are killing.
- Quarterly conversion roadmap review.

### SLA

- Monthly experiment report by the 5th business day of the month.
- Experiment design within 5 business days of hypothesis prioritisation.
- Experiment build and ship within 10 business days of design approval.

### Communication cadence

- Monthly report and call.
- Quarterly conversion roadmap review (90 minutes).

### Exclusions

- Net-new page builds (counts as expansion offer).
- Backend/infrastructure changes affecting checkout or transactional
  systems beyond UX layer.
- Paid-traffic landing pages (use landing page expansion offer).
- A/B test platform fees (Google Optimize is gone — we use Convert.com,
  client pays the platform fee directly).

### Upsell path

- Client wants to test new offers → landing page expansion.
- Client wants email/lifecycle CRO → email lifecycle retainer (custom).
- Major UX overhaul indicated by experiments → re-engagement at Growth tier.

## Retainer 5 — Technical Support & Enhancements

**Fee:** $1,500/month
**Minimum term:** 6 months
**Best fit:** Clients in active iteration — adding features, integrating
tools, evolving the site month over month.

### What it does

Reserves a fixed block of senior development capacity for small-to-medium
improvements, integrations, and bug fixes — without re-engagement fees for
each change.

### What's included

- **8 hours per month** of senior development time, applied to whatever the
  client most needs (within scope).
- Bug fixes (functional issues found post-launch, not covered under the
  90-day post-launch warranty).
- Small feature additions (new sections, new form fields, new component
  variants, new landing pages within the existing system).
- Tool integrations (CRM, email, analytics, scheduling tools, payment).
- Performance optimisation cycles.
- Accessibility patches.
- Monthly summary of work delivered.

### SLA

- Bug response within 1 business day.
- Standard request response within 2 business days; estimate provided.
- Standard request shipped within 10 business days of approval (for work
  that fits in 8-hour budget).

### Communication cadence

- Bi-weekly check-in (30 minutes) on backlog.
- Monthly summary email.
- Slack/email channel.

### Exclusions

- Net-new pages requiring strategy or design (counts as project, not
  enhancement).
- Anything that changes the brand identity, messaging, or positioning.
- Mobile app development.
- Major version migrations (e.g. CMS upgrades requiring re-architecture).
- Work consuming more than 8 hours in a month — billed at $200/hour
  overage if approved in advance, or rolled to the next month.

### Upsell path

- Client wants major features → Growth tier re-engagement.
- Client wants ongoing feature shipping > 8 hr/month → Plus tier ($2,500
  for 16 hours).
- Client wants a redesign → re-engagement at original tier or higher.

## Pricing logic

The retainer fees follow the same value-based logic as engagement pricing
(see `agency-positioning/references/pricing-rules.md`):

- Each retainer's fee is sized so that the agency's gross margin per client
  is at minimum 60%, ideally 70%+.
- Each retainer's fee anchors at a value ratio of at least 5:1 — the
  outcome the client receives is worth 5x the fee.
- No hourly billing, no time-and-materials. Fixed-fee productised offers
  only.
- No "junior" retainer pricing. Senior strategist always involved.

The retainer fees are public — published on the website's services pages.
Pricing transparency (per Sheridan's CarMax Effect, see
`they-ask-you-answer/references/legacy-guidance.md`) is non-negotiable.

## Capacity planning

Each retainer consumes a fixed amount of senior team capacity:

| Retainer | Senior strategist hrs/month | Specialist hrs/month |
|----------|----------------------------|----------------------|
| Care & Maintenance | 1 | 4 (developer) |
| SEO Foundation | 2 | 6 (SEO specialist) |
| Content Growth | 3 | 24 (content manager + designer) |
| CRO | 4 | 12 (CRO specialist + dev) |
| Technical Support | 1 | 8 (developer) |

A founder-led 5-person agency can carry approximately:

- 30 to 40 Care & Maintenance retainers (passive revenue).
- 8 to 12 SEO retainers.
- 4 to 6 Content Growth retainers.
- 4 to 6 CRO retainers.
- 6 to 10 Technical Support retainers.

A healthy agency mix: 20 Care + 6 SEO + 4 Content + 3 CRO + 5 Tech =
$87,300/month recurring (~$1M ARR), comfortably supportable by a 5-person
team with margin to spare.

Past those numbers, the agency must hire — see
`hybrid-agency-operating-model.md` (forthcoming) for the team scaling
playbook.

## When to recommend which retainer

Decision logic for the 30-day review (see `post-launch-review-cadence.md`):

1. **Always recommend Care & Maintenance.** If the client refuses, document
   the risk and require them to sign a "no maintenance" acknowledgement.
2. **Recommend SEO Foundation if** the client's sector has measurable
   organic search demand and the client articulated SEO as a goal at
   discovery.
3. **Recommend Content Growth if** the client engaged at Authority tier and
   committed to a long-term thought-leadership posture.
4. **Recommend CRO if** the client has measurable traffic and stated
   conversion goals at engagement, and is past 60 days post-launch.
5. **Recommend Technical Support if** the client is in active iteration
   mode (adding features, integrations) within the first 90 days.

Never recommend more than 2 retainers at the 30-day review. Recommend the
single highest-leverage retainer first; introduce others at 90 or 180-day
reviews.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- **Vague deliverables** — "ongoing SEO support" with no specifics. Causes
  scope expansion and client dissatisfaction. Each retainer must list
  specific deliverables in countable units.
- **Unlimited support** — never. Always cap.
- **Hourly retainers** — they create the wrong incentive (the client
  watches the clock instead of the outcome).
- **Bundling retainer into engagement fee** — destroys the post-launch
  commercial conversation and trains the client to expect ongoing work for
  free.
- **Selling all 5 retainers to one client** — unfocused; the client doesn't
  know what they bought; renewal conversations become impossible.
- **Discounting** — never below the listed rate. Annual prepay is the only
  discount mechanic.
- **Skipping the 30-day review** — the moment retainers are sold; missing
  this window means the client falls out of the agency's commercial sight
  line.
