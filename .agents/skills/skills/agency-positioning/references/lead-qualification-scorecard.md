# Lead Qualification Scorecard

Use this before deep discovery, proposal writing, or any fixed-price scoping.
The purpose is to reject weak-fit projects early and protect premium delivery time.

This scorecard combines:

- selective-fit logic from *Book Yourself Solid*
- diagnostic discipline from *SPIN Selling*
- service quality and client-management principles from *Managing the Professional Service Firm*

## How To Use

1. Score each category from `0` to `3`.
2. Mark any hard-stop failure.
3. Add the total score.
4. Route the lead using the decision rules below.

## Scoring Scale

- `0` = unacceptable or unknown after first conversation
- `1` = weak fit; material risk exists
- `2` = workable but needs active management
- `3` = strong fit for a premium website engagement

## Qualification Categories

| Category | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Budget suitability | No realistic budget or asks for pricing before problem definition | Budget below minimum build threshold | Budget can support a narrower scope | Budget clearly supports the recommended engagement |
| Decision-maker access | Gatekeeper only; buyer absent | Buyer is indirect or unavailable | Buyer attends some key calls | Final decision-maker is present and responsive |
| Urgency level | No deadline or false urgency | Timeline exists but is vague | Clear timeline with moderate pressure | Genuine business reason for timing and fast approvals |
| Business maturity | Offer, market, or operations are unstable | Early business with weak traction | Operating business with clear offer | Established business with proof, traction, and clear economics |
| Content readiness | No content, no owner, no willingness to help | Major gaps with weak accountability | Partial assets and someone responsible | Strong source material and accountable contributors |
| Trust and compliance demands | High-risk regulated context with no review discipline | Significant legal/compliance needs but unclear owner | Clear compliance needs and reviewer exists | Compliance owner and approval path are defined |
| Growth intent | Website seen as a cosmetic expense | Wants a website but not strategic change | Wants measurable improvement | Treats website as revenue and authority infrastructure |

## Hard Stops

If any of the following are true, do not advance to a premium website proposal:

- No decision-maker access after the first qualification call
- Refusal to complete discovery or provide source material
- Unrealistic budget combined with premium expectations
- Scope pressure that bypasses strategy, approvals, or QA
- Ethical, legal, or compliance risk with no accountable approver
- Repeated attempts to make chat threads the source of truth

## Minimum Thresholds

- Minimum acceptable score for paid discovery: `11/21`
- Minimum acceptable score for a premium website proposal: `15/21`
- Minimum acceptable score for a fixed-price build recommendation: `17/21`

Also require:

- no hard-stop failures
- at least `2` on budget suitability
- at least `2` on decision-maker access
- at least `2` on content readiness or an approved paid-discovery plan to close the gap

## Routing Decisions

### Route A - Premium Website Build

Use when:

- score is `15+`
- no hard stops
- discovery inputs can realistically be completed

Next step:

- move to discovery and proposal

### Route B - Paid Strategy / Discovery First

Use when:

- score is `11-14`
- the client has potential but major unknowns remain
- the real need is diagnosis, not quoting

Offer:

- paid strategy workshop
- research and messaging brief
- sitemap and scope recommendation
- implementation proposal after strategy approval

### Route C - Lower-Tier Offer

Use when:

- budget is real but insufficient for a premium build
- the client needs a narrower deliverable

Offer examples:

- audit and roadmap
- conversion review
- SEO triage
- landing page sprint

### Route D - Referral / Decline

Use when:

- lead score is below `11`
- trust is weak
- the project is not commercially or operationally viable

Response principle:

- decline clearly
- protect the relationship
- refer where appropriate
