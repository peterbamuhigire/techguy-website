# Positioning Language Reference

## The Language Transformation Matrix

### Service → Outcome Rewrites

**Website Design:**
- Weak: "Custom website design and development"
- Strong: "A website engineered to convert your ideal clients — not just look good"
- Strongest: "We build websites that generate leads while you sleep. Every design decision is tested against one question: does this move a visitor closer to contacting you?"

**SEO:**
- Weak: "Search engine optimisation services"
- Strong: "Get found by clients who are actively looking for what you offer"
- Strongest: "We put you in front of clients at the exact moment they're searching for your service — before they find your competitor"

**Brand Identity:**
- Weak: "Logo design and brand guidelines"
- Strong: "A visual identity that makes your premium pricing feel obvious"
- Strongest: "We build brands that make the right clients say 'this is exactly who I want to work with' before they've read a word of your copy"

**Content Strategy:**
- Weak: "Blog writing and content creation"
- Strong: "Educational content that answers your clients' questions before the sales call"
- Strongest: "We turn your expertise into content that pre-sells your services — clients arrive at the sales call already convinced"

**UX/Conversion Optimisation:**
- Weak: "User experience design and CRO"
- Strong: "Removing the friction between a website visitor and a paying client"
- Strongest: "We identify exactly where your website is losing you money and fix it — most clients see 30-60% more conversions within 90 days"

## The Three Prospect Pain Categories (Schulkind)

Address these pains explicitly in all agency copy:

**1. Opportunity Cost Pain**
"Every month your website isn't converting, you're paying for traffic that leaves without enquiring. That's money you've already spent, producing nothing."

**2. Personal Risk Pain**
"The last agency promised results but delivered a beautiful website that nobody found. We understand why you're cautious — and we'll show you exactly why this time is different."

**3. Sunk Cost Pain**
"You've already invested in a website that isn't working. The question isn't whether to fix it — it's how much longer you can afford to lose leads to a site that doesn't convert."

## Objection Handling Scripts

**"Why does it cost so much?"**
"Let's anchor this differently. What is your average client worth to you over 12 months? [Listen.] And how many clients per month is your current website bringing you? [Listen.] So the question isn't whether $20,000 is a lot — the question is: if this website brings you 3 extra clients per month for the next 3 years, what's that worth? Our job is to make that happen."

**"Can't AI just build my website?"**
"AI can generate layouts and text. What it can't do is diagnose why your specific business is losing clients online, set a strategy that accounts for your market position and competitors, or explain to you each month what changed and why it worked. That's what you're paying for — strategy, accountability, and results."

**"We had an agency before and it didn't work."**
"That's the most important thing you could tell us. Can you walk me through what happened? [Listen carefully.] Based on what you've described, the issue was [X]. Here's specifically how our process prevents that from happening. And here's what we do if results aren't tracking where they should be after 90 days."

**"Can you show me your portfolio?"**
"Absolutely. But more useful than looking at designs is looking at results. [Show case study.] This client had [problem]. We did [approach]. The outcome was [specific metric]. That's the standard we hold ourselves to."

## Competitive Differentiation Script

When asked "how are you different from other agencies?":

"Most agencies sell you a project and disappear. You get a beautiful website, and then three months later you're wondering why nothing has changed. We work differently on three levels:

First, we start with a diagnostic — not a proposal. We spend time understanding why your current situation isn't working before we propose anything. Most agencies skip this step.

Second, we treat every engagement as a 12-month partnership, not a one-time build. Your website is a living asset — it needs monthly attention, testing, and improvement.

Third, every month you get a plain-language report that tells you exactly what changed, why, and what result it produced. You'll always know exactly what you're getting for your investment."
