# Premium Audit Offers

Paid, fixed-fee audit engagements that function as the top of the agency's sales
funnel. Each audit is a real commercial product — not a free consultation, not a
teaser, not a trojan horse disguised as "discovery."

A premium audit has four jobs:

1. **Deliver genuine value on its own** — the audit is useful even if the client
   never buys anything else.
2. **Qualify the buyer** — they paid, which proves intent and budget.
3. **Demonstrate strategic rigour** — the audit is the most detailed proof of how
   the agency thinks.
4. **Earn the right to recommend a premium engagement** — after a paid audit, a
   $22k Growth proposal lands on prepared ground.

The rule: *free consulting is a trap.* Paid audits are the cure.

---

## Why paid audits instead of free audits

| Free "audit" | Paid audit |
|--------------|------------|
| Prospect with no skin in the game | Prospect with verified intent |
| Generic checklist output | Custom strategic assessment |
| 30 minutes of the founder's time, given away | 6–12 hours of team work, fairly priced |
| Creates "free consulting" dependency | Creates commercial relationship |
| Positions agency as vendor | Positions agency as expert |
| Converts at 5–10% | Converts at 40–70% |
| Cannot be marketed publicly | IS marketing material |

Free audits train prospects to expect free work. Paid audits train prospects to
expect to pay for expertise. Choose once.

## The four standard audit offers

The agency sells four audits. Each targets a different buyer pain, produces a
different deliverable, and sits at a different fee point. Any can lead to a
Foundation, Growth, or Authority engagement.

### 1. Website Authority Audit — $2,500

**The promise.** In two weeks, we will tell you whether your current website
positions you as the category leader in your sector, and exactly what it would
take to get there.

**Who it is for.** An established business ($2M+ revenue or 8+ years operating)
whose leadership knows the current site is not punching above its weight, but who
cannot articulate what is missing. Typical buyer: a founder or MD whose
competitors are quietly eating them in search and proposals.

**What we deliver.**

- A 30-page written audit covering:
  - Category positioning analysis — where they sit versus the 5 nearest competitors.
  - Message hierarchy diagnosis — are they communicating expertise or commodity.
  - Authority asset inventory — content, case studies, speaking, press.
  - Trust signal audit — testimonials, credentials, social proof quality.
  - Buyer journey audit — does the site lead a prospect toward a qualified
    conversation.
  - Gap analysis against the Authority Website System standard.
- A 90-minute findings call with the founder/leadership team.
- A prioritised 12-month roadmap — what to fix first, what to fix next, what to
  ignore.
- A decision memo: whether a full engagement with us is (or is not) the right
  next step. We say no when no is the honest answer.

**What we do not deliver.** Design work, copy, implementation, changes to the
site. The audit is diagnosis, not treatment.

**Timeline.** 2 weeks from deposit. Day 1 kickoff call. Days 2–10 audit work.
Day 11 draft review with the agency team. Day 12 written audit delivered. Day
13–14 findings call scheduled.

**Deposit and payment.** 100% upfront. Non-refundable. Clients who want to "see
what you find first" are not buyers.

**Conversion pathway.** The audit's decision memo either recommends one of:

- A Growth or Authority engagement with us (the audit fee is credited toward the
  engagement fee — see `pricing-rules.md`).
- A Foundation engagement with us (fee is credited).
- No engagement with us at this time (we refund nothing, but we also refuse to
  sell something the client does not need).

Expected conversion rate: 50–65% to a recommended engagement.

### 2. Trust and Conversion Review — $1,800

**The promise.** In 10 business days, we will tell you exactly why your current
website is losing qualified visitors and give you 12 to 20 specific fixes ranked
by impact and effort.

**Who it is for.** A business with an existing site generating traffic (ideally
1,000+ monthly visitors) and some leads, but below the leadership's expectations.
Typical buyer: a marketing director or a founder who has run ads against a
converting landing page and knows the numbers aren't good enough.

**What we deliver.**

- A 20-page written review covering:
  - Trust signal audit — credentials, testimonials, case studies, named team,
    address, response time guarantees.
  - Conversion mechanic audit — CTA placement, form design, friction points,
    objection handling on key pages.
  - Message-market fit — does the headline promise what the buyer is looking for.
  - Analytics sanity check — are conversions being measured, which pages are
    leaking, funnel drop-offs.
  - Mobile experience review — 375px pass/fail on the 5 highest-traffic pages.
  - Competitor benchmarking — how 3 comparable sites handle the same conversion
    moments.
- A prioritised fix list (12–20 items) with:
  - Expected impact (high/medium/low).
  - Effort estimate (hours).
  - Whether the agency would implement (scope for follow-on work) or whether the
    client's internal team can handle it.
- A 60-minute findings call.

**Timeline.** 10 business days from deposit.

**Deposit and payment.** 100% upfront.

**Conversion pathway.** The Trust and Conversion Review is designed to lead into:

- A conversion-focused Foundation or Growth rebuild ($12k–$22k).
- A retainer with monthly CRO experiments ($2.5k/month).

Expected conversion rate: 40–55%.

### 3. SEO Foundation Review — $1,500

**The promise.** In 7 business days, we will tell you whether your site has the
technical and structural foundation to rank for the queries that matter to your
business — and what must change if it doesn't.

**Who it is for.** A business whose leadership believes search should be driving
qualified leads but currently isn't, or a business that has paid for SEO services
with no visible outcome.

**What we deliver.**

- A 15-page written review covering:
  - Technical foundation — indexation, crawlability, Core Web Vitals, schema,
    internal linking.
  - Keyword opportunity mapping — the 15 highest-value queries for this business,
    current ranking, estimated effort to move.
  - Content gap analysis against the top 3 organic competitors.
  - Local SEO audit (where relevant) — GMB, citations, review velocity.
  - Hreflang and language-version SEO audit (for multilingual sites).
  - Off-page signals — link profile health, brand search trend, mentions.
  - A 6-month SEO improvement plan with milestones.
- A 60-minute findings call.

**Timeline.** 7 business days.

**Deposit and payment.** 100% upfront.

**Conversion pathway.** Leads into:

- A content engine retainer ($3k–$5k/month — see `they-ask-you-answer/`).
- A Growth engagement where the rebuild is structured around the SEO gaps found.

Expected conversion rate: 45–60%.

### 4. Messaging Clarity Review — $1,200

**The promise.** In 5 business days, we will tell you whether your website
explains — in the first 7 seconds — what you do, who you do it for, and why they
should care. And we will rewrite your hero section if it doesn't.

**Who it is for.** A business whose website reads as generic "we do great X" and
who knows it but can't see past their own familiarity with the brand. Typical
buyer: a founder who has tried 3 rounds of copywriter edits and is still not
satisfied.

**What we deliver.**

- A 10-page written review covering:
  - Current message audit — what the homepage says in the first screen.
  - The 7-second test — results of showing the site to 5 people cold.
  - Buyer-language analysis — what your ideal buyer Googles, says in meetings, and
    writes in RFPs. Does your site use their language or yours.
  - Category positioning — are you competing in a category or fighting for
    oxygen in a commodity market.
  - Proof stack — are the right trust signals visible above the fold.
  - Three rewritten headline options plus a short rationale for each.
  - A new hero section (headline, subhead, primary CTA) ready to paste into the
    site.
- A 45-minute findings call.

**Timeline.** 5 business days.

**Deposit and payment.** 100% upfront.

**Conversion pathway.** Leads into:

- A positioning and messaging engagement ($8k–$15k — see `brand-strategy`).
- A Growth engagement where the new messaging drives the rebuild.

Expected conversion rate: 55–70% — this audit has the highest conversion because
the gap it surfaces is usually painful and immediately actionable.

## How to sell a paid audit

### On the agency's website

Each audit has its own landing page (not a sub-section of the services page). Each
landing page follows a standard structure:

1. Hero: the outcome and the fee, not the process.
2. Who it's for / who it's not for.
3. The 3 symptoms that mean you need this.
4. What you get — specific deliverables with samples.
5. Timeline.
6. Testimonial from a previous audit client.
7. The process week by week.
8. Pricing and what's included.
9. What happens next if you buy an engagement afterward.
10. FAQ (8–12 questions, including price objections).
11. A single CTA: a short booking form that qualifies before a call.

### In a discovery call

When a prospect arrives inbound but is not yet ready for a $22k engagement:

> "Based on what you've told me, there are three things that need to be true before
> I'd recommend a Growth engagement. Before we know whether those three things are
> true, I'd recommend a Website Authority Audit. It's $2,500, takes two weeks, and
> produces a written decision memo on whether a full engagement is the right move —
> including me telling you if it isn't. If you decide to go ahead with a full
> engagement with us, the $2,500 comes off the fee. If you decide not to, you keep
> the audit and the decision. Fair?"

Never frame the audit as a lesser service. It is a qualification product; that's
the value.

### When referred by an existing client

When a previous client refers a new prospect, the audit is often skipped — the
referred prospect already trusts the agency. Use the audit only if:

- The referred prospect is not yet certain of their own needs.
- The referrer is a weak referral (casual acquaintance, not warm endorsement).
- The prospect's stated budget is below the agency floor ($12k), and the audit is
  a way to prove the floor is justified.

### When inbound from content

For prospects who arrive via the agency's authority content (blog, teardowns,
talks), the audit is the natural next step. Include an audit CTA at the bottom of
every cornerstone article:

> "This article explains the principles. If you want this applied to your
> specific business, the Website Authority Audit does exactly that. $2,500, two
> weeks, written decision memo."

## What not to do

- **Do not discount the audit fee.** The fee is the qualification mechanism. A
  discounted audit is a free audit.
- **Do not deliver the audit in under the quoted timeline.** Fast delivery
  signals the work was shallow. The timeline is the audit's weight.
- **Do not roll audit work into the engagement silently.** The audit fee is
  credited explicitly on the engagement invoice so the client sees the commercial
  mechanic.
- **Do not spec a free "version of" the audit.** One fee, one deliverable, one
  level of rigour.
- **Do not pitch the engagement during the audit.** The decision memo pitches it.
  During the audit, the agency's job is to diagnose honestly. Clients smell a
  sales pitch disguised as analysis.
- **Do not leave the audit deliverable undated and unsigned.** Every audit has the
  lead auditor's name, date, and version number.
- **Do not use the audit to extract free discovery for a proposal.** If the
  prospect has declined to pay for the audit but wants "a few recommendations,"
  decline. Recommendations without assessment are malpractice.

## Audit pricing logic

The audits are priced at 8–20% of the entry-tier engagement fee. Rationale:

- Low enough that a qualified buyer with real intent will not balk.
- High enough that unqualified buyers self-select out.
- High enough to cover 6–12 hours of real work by a senior auditor plus review.
- Transparent and public — the fee is on the website, not negotiated per call.

If the audit fee is being negotiated, the prospect is not the right buyer. Let
them go.

## Audit delivery team

Each audit is delivered by the senior strategist, not a junior. The audit is the
highest-leverage, lowest-revenue part of the engagement sequence — it must be
excellent because it is the prospect's first experience of the agency's work
product. If juniors deliver audits, conversion rates collapse.

A single senior strategist can deliver 3 to 5 audits per month without eroding
engagement delivery time. Plan capacity accordingly.

## Measuring the audit offer

Track these monthly, per audit type:

- Audit enquiries received.
- Audit proposals sent (if any — most are self-serve buy-now).
- Audits sold.
- Audit conversion rate to full engagement.
- Average fee of the engagement sold (to verify audits are leading to premium
  work, not downsell).
- Audit-to-engagement cycle length.
- Clients who bought audit but no engagement (reason captured for every case).

The audit line is profitable on its own if conversion to engagement is above
40%. Below 40%, either the audit is not diagnosing properly, or the engagement
offer is not the right next step after the audit — investigate before repricing.
