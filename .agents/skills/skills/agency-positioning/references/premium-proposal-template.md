# Premium Website Proposal Template

Use this template after qualification and discovery. The proposal should prove that
the recommendation follows from the client's business problem, not from a generic
bundle of deliverables.

This structure aligns with:

- diagnosis-first selling from *SPIN Selling*
- selective-fit and trust-based selling from *Book Yourself Solid*
- service quality discipline from *Managing the Professional Service Firm*

## Proposal Metadata

- Client:
- Proposal date:
- Prepared by:
- Valid until:
- Proposal version:

## 1. Executive Summary

Summarise:

- the business problem
- the commercial impact of leaving it unresolved
- the recommended strategic direction

Prompt:

`Based on discovery, our view is that the current website is limiting growth because...`

## 2. Current Situation

Capture the facts in the client's language where possible.

Include:

- current website and commercial context
- target audience and buying context
- current conversion or positioning problems
- known content, proof, SEO, and technical issues

## 3. Strategic Recommendation

Describe the recommended approach as a business solution.

Include:

- what we recommend
- why this approach fits the evidence
- what it is intended to change
- what we are deliberately not recommending

## 4. Scope Definition

List what is included and keep it concrete.

Suggested structure:

- strategy and discovery outputs
- sitemap and page planning
- design system work
- copy/content production support
- website build and implementation
- technical SEO setup
- QA and launch support

## 5. Delivery Method

Describe how the work will run.

Include:

- project stages
- review points
- client responsibilities
- feedback windows
- approval rules

## 6. Timeline

State timing as conditional on client responsiveness.

Include:

- estimated project phases
- expected approval windows
- known dependencies
- what causes the timeline to pause or reset

## 7. Investment

Include:

- project fee
- payment schedule
- optional add-ons
- post-launch retainer recommendation if relevant
- re-scope/change-order rule

## 8. Assumptions

Make assumptions visible before contract signature.

Examples:

- client will provide approvals within `X` business days
- client will supply named content inputs by date
- compliance review will be handled by the client's designated approver
- third-party tools are licensed by the client unless stated otherwise

## 9. Exclusions

This section is mandatory.

Examples:

- ongoing ad management
- full CRM implementation
- custom software outside named scope
- untranslated secondary languages
- photography or video production unless listed
- legal review and compliance sign-off

## 10. Approval Model

Define how decisions are made.

Include:

- primary approver
- backup approver
- approval channels accepted
- what happens if feedback conflicts or arrives late

## 11. Risks And Dependencies

State the main project risks plainly.

Examples:

- missing content
- delayed approvals
- poor analytics access
- compliance bottlenecks
- hidden technical debt on the current site

## 12. Next Steps

Keep this operational, not vague.

1. Proposal accepted in writing by:
2. Deposit or paid-discovery invoice paid by:
3. Kickoff scheduled for:
4. Required client inputs due by:
5. Project start target:

## Proposal Writing Rules

- Lead with the problem and the strategic recommendation, not a feature list.
- Use the client's language from discovery where useful.
- Make the scope boundary obvious.
- Do not hide assumptions in paragraphs.
- Do not quote a fixed build if critical discovery facts are still unknown.

## Proposal Skeleton

```md
# Proposal: [Client Name]

## Executive Summary

## Current Situation

## Strategic Recommendation

## Scope Definition

## Delivery Method

## Timeline

## Investment

## Assumptions

## Exclusions

## Approval Model

## Risks And Dependencies

## Next Steps
```
