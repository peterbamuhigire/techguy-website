# Channel Economics

Use this reference to decide how an authority offer should acquire attention and how
to measure whether the path is commercially working.

## Traffic-source fit

| Source | Best For | Watch Out For |
|---|---|---|
| Organic search | High-intent educational demand | Slow ramp, weak if offer is too abstract |
| Social organic | Repetition, authority, proof snippets | Reach without capture is not a business asset |
| Paid social | Fast list growth, webinar registration, retargeting | Weak economics if the offer is vague or the CTA is high-friction |
| Email | Nurture, reactivation, ascension | Requires segmentation by offer and intent |
| WhatsApp / sales follow-up | Warm leads, reminders, application nudges | Must feel contextual, not spammy |

## Direct-response measurement

Track:

- Cost per lead or registration
- Lead-to-call or lead-to-sale rate
- Show-up rate for webinars or workshops
- Audit-to-project conversion
- Email click-through to next-step CTA
- Time-to-conversion by traffic source

Do not stop at impressions, likes, or reach.

## Retargeting posture

Retarget only after the initial promise is proven:

1. Traffic lands on one focused offer page.
2. Visitors who engage but do not convert enter a retargeting audience.
3. Retargeting creative restates the promise, adds proof, and removes one objection.
4. Send the click back to the same conversion step or the immediate next step.

## List-building rule

Owned audience is an asset. Every authority campaign should answer:

- What email segment does this build?
- What proof can be collected from this cohort?
- What is the next offer after this opt-in or purchase?

If the answer is "none," the offer is a content artifact, not a growth asset.
