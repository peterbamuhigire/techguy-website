# Authority Offer Ladder

Use this reference when packaging expertise into a measurable offer.

## Core rule

Match the offer to the buyer's temperature and risk tolerance. Cold audiences need
clarity and a low-friction exchange. Warm audiences can accept diagnosis, workshops,
or paid strategic work. Do not ask for a large commitment before trust exists.

## Offer types

| Offer Type | Best Use | Typical Friction | What Must Be True |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Lead magnet | Build list and pixel pool | Low | Solves one narrow problem quickly |
| Email course | Nurture and qualify | Low-Medium | Each lesson leads toward one commercial next step |
| Webinar / workshop | Educate and convert | Medium | Topic is urgent, outcome is concrete, CTA is singular |
| Paid audit / diagnostic | Qualify and monetize insight | Medium | The audit has a clear rubric, outputs, and path to implementation |
| Playbook / toolkit | Productize repeatable knowledge | Medium | The framework is proven and easily applied |
| Strategy sprint | High-value warm-lead offer | High | The buyer already trusts the operator's expertise |

## Selection rules

1. Start with the business goal.
   - Need top-of-funnel volume: lead magnet, checklist, calculator, or template.
   - Need better qualification: paid audit, scorecard, or application-first webinar.
   - Need revenue before full-service sale: workshop, toolkit, paid sprint.
2. Start with one promised outcome.
   - "Reduce cart abandonment."
   - "Clarify homepage messaging."
   - "Audit mobile trust blockers."
3. Remove anything that dilutes the promise.
   - One audience.
   - One problem.
   - One CTA.

## Offer construction

Every offer brief should specify:

1. Audience and situation.
2. Problem stated in buyer language.
3. Desired outcome.
4. Mechanism or method.
5. Proof available today.
6. CTA and follow-up sequence.
7. Ascension path to the next offer.

## Proof requirements

Use at least two of these before launch:

- Case study or metric
- Demonstrated framework
- Before/after comparison
- Named deliverable
- Risk-reversal or clear boundary
- Operator credibility signal

## Anti-hype filter

- Replace "secret" with mechanism.
- Replace "instant" with time-to-value.
- Replace "bonus stack" with deliverables.
- Replace vague urgency with a real deadline, capacity cap, or event date.
