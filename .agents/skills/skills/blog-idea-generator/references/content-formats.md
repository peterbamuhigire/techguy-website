# Blog Content Formats — 20 Types

Every topic can be expressed through different formats. Choose the format that best serves the reader's needs and the content's purpose. The blog-idea-generator specifies a format for each idea; the blog-writer uses format-specific structural templates from `blog-writer/references/writing-craft.md`.

Sources: Danny Iny (Blog Post Ideas), Rebecca Livermore (Blogger's Quick Guide), Eddy (Blog It Right), Kothand (One Hour Content Plan).

## Format Library

### 1. How-To / Tutorial
**When:** Audience needs to learn a process. Most searched format.
**Structure:** Problem → Why it matters → Step-by-step → Common mistakes → Result
**Title patterns:** "How to [Goal] in [Timeframe]", "[N] Steps to [Outcome]"
**Evergreen potential:** High — ranks for years.

### 2. Case Study
**When:** You have specific client results or project outcomes to share.
**Structure:** Situation → Challenge → What was done → Results (with numbers) → Lessons
**Title patterns:** "How [Company] [Achieved Result]", "From [Problem] to [Solution]: A [Industry] Case Study"
**Trust factor:** High — concrete proof builds credibility.

### 3. List / Listicle
**When:** Content is naturally enumerable. Scannable, shareable.
**Structure:** Hook → Items (varied depth, not all equal) → Pattern/insight → CTA
**Title patterns:** "[N] [Things] Every [Audience] Should Know", "The [N] Best [Items] for [Context]"
**Rules:** Odd numbers outperform even. Don't make all items equal length — vary depth.

### 4. Opinion / Contrarian
**When:** You disagree with conventional wisdom and have evidence.
**Structure:** Bold claim → Evidence → Counter-argument → Rebuttal → Conclusion
**Title patterns:** "Why [Common Advice] Is Wrong", "Stop [Doing Thing] — Here's What Works Instead"
**Voice:** Requires strong authorial position. Best for Tier 3 thought leadership.

### 5. Ultimate Guide / Pillar
**When:** Core topic that establishes authority. Long-form (2,500+ words).
**Structure:** Overview → Sections by subtopic (each with examples) → Summary → Next steps
**Title patterns:** "The Complete Guide to [Topic]", "Everything You Need to Know About [Topic]"
**SEO value:** Very high — attracts links and long-tail traffic.

### 6. Story / Personal Narrative
**When:** Author has a relevant experience that illustrates a lesson.
**Structure:** Scene → Challenge → Action → Result → Lesson → Bridge to reader
**Title patterns:** "What [Experience] Taught Me About [Topic]", "The Day I [Event] (And What I Learned)"
**Human factor:** Highest — creates deep reader connection.

### 7. Comparison / Versus
**When:** Audience is choosing between options.
**Structure:** Context → Criteria → Item-by-item analysis → Verdict → Who should choose what
**Title patterns:** "[A] vs [B]: Which Is Right for [Audience]?", "[N] [Options] Compared for [Context]"
**Decision stage:** Best for Solution-aware and Decision-ready readers.

### 8. Interview / Expert Feature
**When:** You can feature a client, industry expert, or partner.
**Structure:** Brief intro → Q&A or narrative → Key takeaways → Reader action
**Title patterns:** "[Expert Name] on [Topic]", "[N] Experts Answer: [Question]"
**Two formats:** Deep dive (one person) or compilation (several experts, same question).

### 9. Curated Roundup
**When:** Industry has notable resources, tools, or content worth compiling.
**Structure:** Intro (why this roundup) → Items with commentary → Pattern/insight → CTA
**Title patterns:** "[N] Best [Resources] for [Audience] in [Year]", "The [Audience]'s Resource Guide to [Topic]"
**Rules:** Always add your own commentary (1-3 sentences per item). Never a bare link list.

### 10. FAQ / Question Post
**When:** Audience has specific, recurring questions.
**Structure:** Question → Clear answer → Context/nuance → Related questions → CTA
**Title patterns:** "[N] Questions About [Topic] Answered", "What You Need to Know About [Topic]"
**SEO value:** High — matches "People Also Ask" and voice search queries.

### 11. Myth-Busting
**When:** Widespread misconceptions exist in your industry.
**Structure:** State the myth → Why people believe it → The reality (with proof) → What to do instead
**Title patterns:** "[N] [Topic] Myths That Are Costing You [Consequence]", "The Truth About [Topic]"
**Engagement:** High — readers share contrarian content.

### 12. Prediction / Trends
**When:** Industry is evolving. Seasonal (start of year, quarter, after major event).
**Structure:** Context → Prediction 1 (with evidence) → Prediction 2 → ... → What readers should do now
**Title patterns:** "[N] [Industry] Trends for [Year]", "What's Coming Next in [Topic]"
**Follow-up potential:** Revisit predictions later for a second article.

### 13. Product / Service Review
**When:** Audience is evaluating tools, services, or approaches.
**Structure:** What it is → Who it's for → Pros → Cons → Verdict → Alternatives
**Title patterns:** "[Product] Review: [Verdict in 5 Words]", "Is [Product] Worth It for [Audience]?"
**Honesty required:** Balanced reviews build more trust than pure praise.

### 14. Problem/Solution
**When:** Audience faces a specific, defined problem.
**Structure:** Problem description → Why it happens → Failed approaches → The solution → Implementation → Result
**Title patterns:** "How to Fix [Problem] Once and For All", "The [Problem] Problem (And How to Solve It)"
**Pain point formula:** Demonstrate understanding before offering solutions.

### 15. Behind the Scenes / Day in the Life
**When:** Audience is curious about how things work. Humanises the brand.
**Structure:** Set the scene → Walk through the day/process → Surprising moments → Takeaways
**Title patterns:** "A Day in the Life of a [Role]", "Behind the Scenes: How We [Process]"
**Authenticity:** Must feel real — include messy details, not just highlights.

### 16. Checklist / Template
**When:** Audience needs a practical tool they can use immediately.
**Structure:** Why this checklist → The checklist (actionable items) → How to use it → Download/save CTA
**Title patterns:** "The [Topic] Checklist for [Audience]", "[N]-Point [Topic] Template You Can Use Today"
**Value exchange:** High — readers bookmark and return. Great for lead magnets.

### 17. Data / Research
**When:** You have original data, survey results, or can compile industry statistics.
**Structure:** Key finding → Methodology → Data presentation → Analysis → Implications → Action
**Title patterns:** "We Analysed [N] [Things] — Here's What We Found", "[N] [Industry] Statistics for [Year]"
**Authority factor:** Very high — original data attracts links and citations.

### 18. Glossary / Jargon Explainer
**When:** Technical niche with newcomer audience.
**Structure:** Term → Plain-language definition → Example → Why it matters → Related terms
**Title patterns:** "[Industry] Terms Explained", "A Beginner's Guide to [Niche] Terminology"
**SEO value:** High — beginners search for definitions. Can be one master post or individual posts.

### 19. Mistakes / Lessons Learned
**When:** Author or clients have made instructive mistakes.
**Structure:** The mistake → What happened → Why it happened → The fix → Prevention
**Title patterns:** "[N] [Topic] Mistakes I Made (So You Don't Have To)", "What I Wish I'd Known About [Topic]"
**Trust factor:** Admitting mistakes builds credibility and reader connection.

### 20. Manifesto / Declaration
**When:** Brand wants to stake a position on an industry issue.
**Structure:** State of affairs → What's wrong → What should change → Your commitment → Reader invitation
**Title patterns:** "Why We Believe [Bold Statement]", "Our [Topic] Manifesto"
**Brand building:** Strongest format for attracting aligned customers and repelling misaligned ones.

## Format Selection Guide

| Reader Need | Best Formats |
|-------------|-------------|
| "How do I do this?" | How-to, Checklist, Template |
| "Which should I choose?" | Comparison, Review, List |
| "Is this a good idea?" | Opinion, Case study, Data |
| "What's happening in my industry?" | Trends, Roundup, News |
| "Can I trust this company?" | Story, Behind-the-scenes, Case study |
| "I need to understand this concept" | Guide, Glossary, FAQ |
| "Convince me to act" | Problem/Solution, Mistakes, Myth-busting |

## Content Calendar Mix

For a healthy blog, rotate through format types:
- **Monthly must-haves:** 1 How-to/Guide (Tier 1) + 1 Authority piece (Tier 2)
- **Quarterly:** 1 Case study + 1 Opinion/Prediction (Tier 3)
- **Annually:** 1 Ultimate Guide / Pillar post + 1 Year-in-review / Predictions
