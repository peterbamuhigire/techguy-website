# Idea Sources And Series Engine

Use this reference when generating blog ideas for a real business that needs an editorial pipeline, not a one-off brainstorm.

Primary sources:

- Danny Iny et al., *Blog Post Ideas*
- *BLOGS CONTENTS* (used critically, low authority)
- Mike Fishbein, *Growth Hacking with Content Marketing* synthesis already in `book-extractions/`

## Core rule

Generate ideas from source buckets, then group them into clusters and series.
Do not return a random list of disconnected titles.

## Seven high-yield source buckets

1. audience questions
2. objections and hesitations
3. proof and case studies
4. market commentary
5. curated resources
6. seasonal or event hooks
7. internal frameworks, checklists, and templates

Each content plan should use at least four buckets.

## Reliable idea triggers

- Ask the audience directly.
- Turn one good question into three angles:
  - explainer
  - how-to
  - comparison
- Borrow momentum from current media or news only when the link is real.
- Use guest perspectives, interviews, or roundups to borrow expertise and widen reach.
- Write follow-ups from successful posts instead of always starting from zero.

## Build series, not isolated posts

For every pillar topic, produce:

1. one flagship guide
2. two practical how-to posts
3. one comparison or objection post
4. one case study or example post
5. one curated resource or tools post

That gives the site depth, internal-linking logic, and repeated trust signals.

## Critical use of `BLOGS CONTENTS`

The file is useful only for:

- keyword-aware topic scaffolding
- follow-up angles
- outline prompts

Do not copy its tone, structure, or generic phrasing into the repository.

## Output standard

Every idea batch should identify:

- the source bucket
- the buyer stage
- the target keyword or intent
- the likely CTA posture
- the cluster or series it belongs to
