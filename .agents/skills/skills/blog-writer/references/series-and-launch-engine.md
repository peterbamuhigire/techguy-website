# Series And Launch Engine

Use this reference when a blog article must do more than rank or inform.
Apply it when the article needs to support:

- a campaign window
- a launch
- a waitlist
- a service push
- authority-building around a commercial offer

Primary sources:

- Ann Handley, *Everybody Writes*
- Danny Iny et al., *Blog Post Ideas*
- Jeff Walker, *Launch*

## Core rule

Treat strong blog content as part of a sequence.
A single article should prepare the reader for the next right action.

## Three article roles

### 1. Attract

Purpose:

- rank
- earn attention
- frame the problem

Best CTA posture:

- guide
- related article
- newsletter

### 2. Warm

Purpose:

- deepen understanding
- handle objections
- demonstrate expertise

Best CTA posture:

- diagnostic
- checklist
- case study
- webinar or event registration

### 3. Convert

Purpose:

- support an active offer
- move readers to a clear decision

Best CTA posture:

- consult
- application
- pricing
- waitlist

## Launch-aware content sequence

When a business has a timed offer, event, cohort, enrolment window, or announcement, plan blog content in this order:

1. problem agitation and market education
2. framework or solution explanation
3. proof or case-study article
4. objection-handling article
5. deadline or launch article

## Repurposing rule

Every substantial article should leave behind at least two follow-on assets:

- email summary
- LinkedIn post
- WhatsApp teaser
- FAQ snippet
- sales-page proof block

## Handley discipline

Do not let launch pressure ruin the writing.
Keep:

- reader-first openings
- strong structure
- plain language
- one big idea per piece
- one primary next step

## Output standard

When writing a blog article tied to a campaign, note:

- which role the article plays
- what next action it is meant to earn
- what follow-on assets should be repurposed from it
