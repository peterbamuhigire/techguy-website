# Trust Architecture Checklist

Use this checklist for premium websites where trust is part of the buying decision.
This is especially important for legal, consulting, healthcare, education,
financial, and other high-stakes service categories.

Trust should not be patched in after design. It should be planned as an explicit system.

## 1. Credentials And Legitimacy

- [ ] Relevant founder or team credentials are visible
- [ ] Certifications, licenses, or memberships are listed
- [ ] Company age or operating history is stated where useful
- [ ] Physical location or service-area legitimacy is shown
- [ ] Regulated-sector disclaimers are present where required

## 2. Social Proof

- [ ] Testimonials exist and are specific
- [ ] Testimonials match the target buyer profile
- [ ] Case studies exist for the main offer
- [ ] Client or partner logos are used appropriately
- [ ] Results claims are supported, not vague

## 3. Process Transparency

- [ ] The process is explained simply
- [ ] The buyer can see what happens next
- [ ] Timelines or phases are made visible where relevant
- [ ] Expectations and responsibilities are stated
- [ ] Hidden complexity is reduced through explanation

## 4. Team Proof

- [ ] Real people are shown
- [ ] Relevant expertise is attached to those people
- [ ] Roles are clear
- [ ] Generic stock imagery does not replace essential team proof

## 5. Risk Reduction

- [ ] Guarantees or assurances are stated honestly
- [ ] Objections are addressed directly
- [ ] Pricing ambiguity is reduced where commercially appropriate
- [ ] "What if we are not a fit?" is answered
- [ ] Contact and response expectations are clear

## 6. Compliance And Safety Signals

- [ ] Privacy, legal, or regulatory requirements are surfaced
- [ ] Claims are compatible with the sector's compliance rules
- [ ] Form and intake friction reflect the risk level appropriately
- [ ] Sensitive sectors avoid unsupported superlatives

## 7. Locality And Reality Cues

- [ ] Real office, location, or service-area evidence is present
- [ ] Contact details are complete
- [ ] Named humans, not only generic brand language, are present
- [ ] The site feels like a real business, not a landing-page shell

## 8. Page-Level Proof Burden

For each priority page:

- [ ] One proof burden is defined
- [ ] The required proof is present or flagged as missing
- [ ] The CTA is not stronger than the proof on the page can support
