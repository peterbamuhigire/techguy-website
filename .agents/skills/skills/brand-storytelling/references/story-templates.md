## 2. Hero's Journey Mapped to Website Pages

The customer is always the hero. The brand is always the mentor. Apply this map before writing any page that involves a customer transformation.

| Journey Stage | Website Page | What to Write |
|---------------|-------------|---------------|
| Ordinary World | Services page intro | The customer's current situation — the before state |
| Call to Adventure | Problem statement | The moment they realised the status quo was no longer acceptable |
| Refusal of the Call | Objection handling | The reasons they hesitated, delayed, or doubted |
| Meeting the Mentor | About / Why Us | How the brand enters the story as guide, not hero |
| Crossing the Threshold | CTA / Enquiry | The decision to act; the first step with the brand |
| Tests, Allies, Enemies | Case study Act 2 | The obstacles encountered during the engagement |
| The Ordeal | Case study Act 3 | The hardest moment; the thing that almost didn't work |
| The Reward | Case study Act 4 | The breakthrough; the first evidence of transformation |
| The Road Back | Case study Act 5 | Consolidation; the new capability or state |
| Resurrection | Testimonial | The moment the transformation proved permanent |
| Return with Elixir | Referral / review | The hero shares their experience so others can find the same guide |

## 3. Story Circle Applied to Case Studies (Dan Harmon)

Eight-stage condensed version for case study and testimonial pages. Each stage is one section of the case study. Keep each stage to one to three sentences in a testimonial context; expand to a paragraph in a full case study.

1. **You** — the client is introduced; their situation, role, and context before the problem
2. **Need** — the specific problem or gap they were living with; why it mattered
3. **Go** — the moment they decided to seek a solution; what made them reach out
4. **Search** — what they tried or considered before finding this brand; due diligence they did
5. **Find** — the engagement begins; why they chose this brand; the first promising signal
6. **Take** — the core work; what was delivered; what the client received
7. **Return** — the client applies the result in their business or life
8. **Change** — the measurable transformation; how the client is different now; what they can do that they could not before

**Story Circle Template (Case Study Format):**

> Before working with [Brand], [Client Name] was dealing with _______________________________________________. They had tried _______________________________________________. When they found [Brand], _______________________________________________ . During the engagement, _______________________________________________. The result was _______________________________________________, and today [Client Name] _______________________________________________.
