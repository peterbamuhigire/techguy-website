### The 10 Logo Don'ts

1. **Never stretch or distort the logo.** Disproportionate scaling breaks the visual balance.
2. **Never rotate the logo.** The logo reads horizontally in all approved contexts.
3. **Never apply a drop shadow, glow, or outer effect.** Effects add noise and reduce impact.
4. **Never apply a gradient to the logo.** The logo uses flat, solid approved colours only.
5. **Never place the logo on a busy photographic background.** Legibility cannot be guaranteed.
6. **Never change the logo colours** outside the approved variants. Off-brand colours break recognition.
7. **Never rearrange logo elements** — the mark and wordmark must maintain their approved relationship.
8. **Never add text, taglines, or graphics** to the logo lockup. Use the approved lockup files only.
9. **Never use low-resolution logo files** for digital or print. Always use SVG or provided print files.
10. **Never use outdated logo versions.** Contact [agency/brand manager] if you are unsure which version is current.

| PNG (transparent background) | Presentations, documents, email signatures |
| PDF (vector) | Print production, large format, external agencies |
| JPG | Last resort only — use when transparency is not supported |

| File format | JPG for photographs, maximum 500KB after compression |
| File naming | [Descriptive, lowercase, hyphenated — e.g., team-sarah-portrait.jpg] |

### The 10 Photography Rules

**Do:**
1. Use authentic, candid moments that show real people in genuine situations.
2. Feature people who resemble the target customer — similarity builds trust and connection.
3. Maintain a consistent colour grade across all images in the same layout or page.
4. Use natural or well-controlled artificial lighting — avoid harsh flash.
5. Include real team members and real customers wherever possible.

**Do not:**
6. Never use generic stock photography showing posed models in artificial business scenarios. Research confirms this destroys trust faster than having no photography.
7. Never apply heavy filters or effects that conflict with the established colour palette.
8. Never use photos with visible watermarks, including stock library watermarks.
9. Never mix photographs with wildly different colour temperatures on the same page.
10. Never use photos below the minimum resolution — blurry photography signals low quality.

### Stock Photo Policy

[State clearly: Approved with conditions / Restricted / Prohibited.] If approved: stock photography must appear authentic and non-posed; subjects must reflect the target customer demographic; obvious stock clichés (handshakes, floating heads, generic office scenes) are prohibited.

### AI-Generated Image Policy

AI-generated images are not approved for client-facing materials without explicit client consent and written disclosure to audiences. This policy protects brand authenticity and customer trust.

## Section 9: Usage Rules — Always and Never

A tight set of always/never rules makes the brand feel reliable to customers and gives everyone who creates content clear, unambiguous guidance. (Drew/Williams, Brand Strategy 101.)

### Always

1. **Always use the approved logo files.** Never recreate the logo from scratch.
2. **Always maintain the minimum clear space around the logo.** Nothing enters the exclusion zone.
3. **Always use the approved colour palette.** Introduce no new colours without a formal brand update.
4. **Always use the approved typefaces.** Install fonts from the provided Fontsource packages.
5. **Always write in [British / African / standard] English.** Consistent spelling builds credibility.
6. **Always ensure text meets WCAG AA contrast.** Accessibility is non-negotiable.
7. **Always include a Privacy Policy link** in website footers and email signatures.
8. **Always credit photography** according to licence terms when required.
9. **Always match the brand voice** in all written communications — social, email, print, in-person.
10. **Always update this document** when brand decisions change. Version and date every revision.

### Never

1. **Never stretch, rotate, or distort the logo.** It breaks visual integrity.
2. **Never use unapproved colours** in branded materials, including presentations and social graphics.
3. **Never use obviously posed or generic stock photography** in client-facing materials. It destroys trust.
4. **Never introduce fonts outside the approved system.** Consistency is the goal.
5. **Never make claims** (awards, rankings, "world-class") that are not supported by evidence.
6. **Never publish content that fails WCAG AA.** Legal and ethical obligation.
7. **Never use the logo on a background** where legibility cannot be guaranteed.
8. **Never mix formal and informal tones** within the same document or page.
9. **Never use an outdated version of this style guide.** Always work from the current versioned document.
10. **Never share editable brand source files** (AI, Figma, SVG originals) with parties outside the approved vendor list without written approval.
