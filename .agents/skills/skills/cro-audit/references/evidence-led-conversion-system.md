# Evidence-Led Conversion System

Use this reference for premium CRO audits, ecommerce audits, and revenue-focused website
reviews. The goal is to turn vague conversion opinions into ranked, evidence-backed
improvements.

## CRO Inputs

Gather only what exists, but prefer multiple evidence types:

- Analytics: traffic source, device split, landing pages, exit pages, funnel drop-off,
  form completion, checkout steps, revenue, average order value, and conversion rate.
- Behaviour evidence: heatmaps, scroll maps, session replay, click maps, on-site search,
  and field abandonment.
- Voice of customer: surveys, sales calls, support tickets, reviews, objections, live chat,
  and customer interviews.
- Usability evidence: task testing, mobile walk-through, accessibility friction, speed,
  and form error behaviour.
- Business evidence: margins, lead quality, sales cycle, fulfillment constraints, stock,
  delivery promises, and customer support capacity.

Do not diagnose only from screenshots when business data is available.

## Diagnosis Lens

For each issue, classify the likely cause:

- Motivation: the visitor does not see enough value or urgency.
- Relevance: the page does not match the visitor's intent or traffic source.
- Clarity: the offer, price, process, or next step is unclear.
- Anxiety: the visitor doubts trust, safety, quality, returns, delivery, privacy, or fit.
- Friction: the path requires too much work, too many fields, too many steps, or too much
  waiting.
- Distraction: competing links, secondary CTAs, visual noise, popups, or navigation pull
  attention away from the goal.

## Audit Procedure

1. Define the commercial goal and conversion event.
2. Map the journey from traffic source to landing page, page path, form/cart/checkout,
   confirmation, and follow-up.
3. Record observed friction and evidence source.
4. Tie each finding to the likely lost behaviour: bounce, low scroll, low click-through,
   cart abandonment, checkout abandonment, weak lead quality, low repeat purchase, or
   low order value.
5. Recommend the smallest credible change that could move the metric.
6. Prioritize with value, effort, confidence, and risk.

## Finding Format

Use this structure:

- Finding: what is wrong.
- Evidence: data, user behaviour, page observation, or missing required proof.
- Impact: why it matters commercially.
- Recommendation: what to change.
- Metric: what should improve.
- Priority: ship now, validate qualitatively, A/B test, or monitor.

## Ecommerce Checks

Product discovery:

- Category names match buyer language.
- Search and filters expose decision attributes.
- Product cards show price, availability, image quality, review signal, and differentiator.
- Internal links support comparison and discovery.

Product detail:

- Product fit is obvious in the first screen.
- Photos, video, dimensions, variations, specs, and benefits answer buyer questions.
- Price, shipping, taxes, returns, guarantees, and stock status are visible before cart.
- Reviews and proof are close to the add-to-cart decision.
- Cross-sells and upsells do not distract from the primary purchase.

Cart:

- Cart summary is clear and editable.
- Total cost is not hidden until late checkout.
- Trust and support signals appear near the checkout action.
- Recovery event and abandonment audience are configured.

Checkout:

- Guest checkout is available unless account creation is legally or operationally required.
- Fields are minimal, labelled, autofill-friendly, and validated inline.
- Payment methods are visible before the final step.
- Shipping cost, delivery timing, returns, privacy, and support are clear.
- Mobile keyboard types, tap targets, and error states are correct.

## Testing Rules

Ship directly when:

- The issue is a broken link, missing label, mobile bug, slow page, unclear error, missing
  policy, or obviously required trust information.

Validate qualitatively when:

- The team lacks traffic volume but the issue affects comprehension, anxiety, or trust.

A/B test when:

- The change affects pricing presentation, page order, offer framing, checkout flow,
  headline promise, CTA wording, urgency, guarantee, or personalization, and traffic volume
  is sufficient.

Avoid testing too many variables at once unless the experiment is intentionally a page-level
concept test.

## CRO Backlog Fields

Every backlog item should include:

- Page or step.
- Problem.
- Evidence source.
- Hypothesis.
- Proposed change.
- Primary metric.
- Secondary guardrail metric.
- Value score.
- Effort score.
- Confidence score.
- Owner.
- Status.
