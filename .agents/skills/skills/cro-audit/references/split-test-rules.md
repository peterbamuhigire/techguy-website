# Split-Test Rules Reference

Distilled from Russell Brunson's 108 Proven Split Test Winners (DotComSecrets Labs, 2013).
Each rule is derived from at least one directly observed split test with a stated lift.
Rules are formatted as actionable statements. Use Confidence Tier 1 (score 9-10) when generating hypotheses.

---

## Headlines — Top 5 Rules

**Rule H1: Use specific numbers, never round numbers.**
"Add 1,198 people a day" beat "Add 1,000 people a day" by +33.56%.
"Discover how this very blog grew to 10,000+ subscribers" beat the generic equivalent by 58%.
Round numbers look like guesses. Specific numbers signal verified data.

**Rule H2: Use present-tense language throughout.**
Present tense is perceived as true and currently happening.
Past tense implies the opportunity has passed. Future tense ("you will") is unproven.
Rewrite all headline copy so the result is happening now: "puts money in accounts" not "will put money in accounts."

**Rule H3: Red headlines beat blue headlines on sales pages.**
Red headline vs. blue headline: +313% revenue.
Aesthetics lose to conversion every time. Test red on all sales pages before any other colour variation.

**Rule H4: Frame headlines around specific real events, not generic claims.**
"How this very blog grew to..." (specific, past event) beat generic equivalents by 58%.
Ground every headline claim in a concrete, verifiable reference.

**Rule H5: Never place a human face directly beside a headline.**
A face next to a headline pulls eye-gaze away from the copy.
Brunson's test: headline with defocused background image over it beat headline beside a face by +112% to +230%.
Use images that look toward the copy, or use soft background images that frame the headline without competing with it.

## CTAs and Buttons — Top 5 Rules

**Rule C1: Show the order/CTA button immediately — never delay it.**
Five separate tests across different funnels all converged on the same result:
- Test 1: no delay vs. delay — no delay won by +43-66% EPC
- Test 2: no delay won by +92%
- Test 3: no delay won by +30.36%
- Test 4: no delay on upsell won by +188%
- Test 5: no delay vs. 11-minute delay — no delay won by +50%
Show the button. Let visitors click it when they are ready. Never force them to wait.

**Rule C2: Add "it's free" near any free-offer button.**
Adding "it's free" next to the opt-in button on a free service lifted sign-ups by +28%.
Visitors need reassurance that no credit card or payment is required. State it explicitly.

**Rule C3: Use action-forward copy above and on order buttons, not urgency-only copy.**
"Get Cloud Blogging" above the order button beat "Limited Time Offer" by +39%.
Describe what the visitor receives. Urgency copy alone does not motivate as strongly as benefit-action copy.

**Rule C4: Place CTA buttons below the video, not to the side.**
Side CTA placement failed completely. Brunson estimates it cost money on two launches before the test confirmed it.
Below-video CTA is the correct default for all video sales letters.

**Rule C5: For free trial pages, place a large CTA at the top of the page.**
Adding a big yellow CTA at the top of a long-form free trial page lifted conversions by +33%.
Free trial visitors have lower commitment barriers — get the CTA in front of them immediately.

## Forms — Top 5 Rules

**Rule F1: Start with a micro-commitment question before asking for the email.**
Asking "How did you hear about us?" before the email opt-in lifted opt-ins by +95%.
Nearly all sales in the split came from the micro-commitment variant.
The first "yes" (answering a survey question) makes the second "yes" (giving an email) feel natural.
Always include "Other" as a fourth option so no visitor is left without a valid answer. Including "Other" lifted EPC by +206%.

**Rule F2: Use inline order forms on the sales page — never redirect to a new page.**
Inline order form vs. redirect to separate order page: +75% in order form conversions (1.4% to 2.5%).
The momentum of the sales page carries over when the form appears on the same page. A redirect breaks the buying state.

**Rule F3: Use two-step order forms for products with payment.**
Collect name/email/phone in step 1; payment details in step 2.
Benefits: capture partial leads who abandon before payment; call centre follow-up converts 10%+ of step-1 completions who did not complete step 2.

**Rule F4: Place credit card fields on the left side of the order form.**
Left-side placement of credit card fields outperformed right-side. Likely related to natural reading direction and cognitive proximity to preceding content.

**Rule F5: Make every page HTTPS.**
HTTPS squeeze page vs. HTTP: +29.26%.
Low effort, high return. There is no legitimate reason to leave any page on HTTP.

## Trust Signal Placement — Top 5 Rules

**Rule T1: Place testimonials at the top of the order form, above the offer recap.**
Testimonials first, offer recap second: +80% revenue per visitor ($6.69 to $12.05).
The buying decision faces its highest anxiety at the order form. Testimonials address that anxiety first.

**Rule T2: Stack video testimonials below the sales video with "Need More Proof?" headline.**
Adding multiple video testimonials below the VSL doubled conversion (+101%).
Diverse testimonials give every visitor someone to identify with. The "Need More Proof?" headline pre-empts the objection.

**Rule T3: Format testimonials to look like Facebook posts, not marketing copy.**
Facebook-style testimonials beat standard testimonials by +296% in one test; +32% in a second test.
Authenticity signals (the format of a personal social media post) are more persuasive than polished testimonial designs.

**Rule T4: Trust seals belong only on order forms and checkout pages.**
Trust Guard logos on a webinar registration page reduced conversions by 13-14%.
Security badges on non-payment pages create friction and imply a threat where the visitor felt none before seeing the badge.

**Rule T5: Fill all empty space on order forms with short testimonials.**
Blank order form space vs. text testimonials filling the space: +44% (14% to 21% conversion).
Empty space on an order form signals incompleteness. Short testimonials maintain the persuasion environment through the completion process.

## Pricing and Offer Rules — Top 5 Rules

**Rule P1: Test payment plans for any offer above $100.**
Adding a 2-payment option to a $497 offer tripled conversion (+217%).
3-payment plan at $37/payment outperformed all lump-sum equivalents including $97 and $77 in a 9-option test.
Pre-select the lump-sum payment as "preferred" to anchor choice; the instalment option removes the barrier for price-sensitive buyers.

**Rule P2: Express all discounts in dollar amounts, not percentages.**
"$10 off" beat "10% off" by 22% even when the dollar and percentage amounts were mathematically equivalent.
Dollar amounts are concrete. Percentages require mental calculation. Remove the cognitive step.

**Rule P3: Limit pricing options to 1-2 choices; use a high-price decoy to anchor the primary offer.**
Removing a middle-priced three-bottle option (which split buyer attention) increased profit by +83%.
One high-priced anchor + one primary offer is the optimal pricing architecture.

**Rule P4: Frame continuity products as the main offer; include entry-level items as free bonuses.**
Dan Kennedy format (continuity as main offer, cheap item as bonus gift): +109% revenue, 4.5x more recurring accounts vs. the traditional low-price-entry upsell sequence.
Sell the membership, give the course. Do not sell the course, then upsell the membership.

**Rule P5: Remove credit card requirements from free trial offers wherever possible.**
No credit card required: +500% in front-end trial sign-ups, +50% in ultimate paid conversions.
The friction of entering a credit card for a free trial loses the majority of interested visitors.

## Layout Rules — Top 5 Rules

**Rule L1: Reduce choices on any page to the minimum required.**
25 above-fold options vs. 5 options: simplified page averaged +21% lift across two tests.
Hick's Law: decision time increases logarithmically with options. A confused mind says no.

**Rule L2: Direct images toward the copy, not at the camera.**
Duck facing toward ad copy vs. facing away: +57% CTR.
Apply to all images near CTAs or headlines. Subjects facing the camera pull the eye off the page. Subjects facing the copy pull the eye into the message.

**Rule L3: Remove sections that do not directly advance the conversion decision.**
Removing a symptom block and ingredient block from below a sales video lifted conversion +80%.
Over-selling creates doubt. Test stripped versions of complex pages before adding elements.

**Rule L4: Use timed lightbox pop-ups on content pages — configured for engaged visitors, not immediate entry.**
Lightbox pop-up (triggered at 10 seconds or scroll depth): +67% opt-ins with no cannibalisation of regular opt-ins.
Best practice: trigger on engaged visitors; match site design; include product image and download count; never show to existing subscribers.

**Rule L5: Simple, plain pages often beat expensive designed ones.**
"Ugly blue" plain text webinar page beat expensive designed page by +11%.
Within the ugly version, shorter copy beat longer copy by +26%.
Spending money on design does not correlate with higher conversions. Test your ugly version.

## The Master Control Blueprint

Brunson's winning formula, derived from 100+ tests across multiple markets. Treat this as the default starting point for any new sales funnel page before running further tests.

### Landing Page (Squeeze)
- Curiosity-based headline — never reveal what the offer is explicitly; over 50% opt-in rates are achievable with pure curiosity
- Three-step process header with icons (warm traffic only; cold traffic finds this intimidating — remove for PPC/cold sources)
- Step 3 text must read "Get Your Access" not "Finish" or "Complete"
- Mini-survey: "How did you hear about us?" with exactly 4 options; fourth option must always be "Other"

### Sales Video Page
- Red headline above the video
- Auto-play video (with pause button; no fast-forward or rewind controls — removing controls keeps viewers in sequence)
- Order button visible immediately from page load — never hidden
- Inline order form on the same page — no redirect
- Video spoiler box below the video listing 3-5 compelling points from the video (lifts video completion; Brunson's test: 4% to 16% conversion)

### Order Form
- Credit card fields on the left
- Facebook-style testimonials at the top right of the form
- Offer recap below the testimonials
- Trust seals on the form itself (never on other pages)
- No empty space — fill with short testimonials

## The DPL Technique (+74.61% Lift)

**What it is:** Trial closes — short agreement questions or statements inserted throughout a presentation (webinar, video sales letter, or long-form copy) to build a continuous "yes" pattern in the viewer's mind.

**Origin:** Ted Thomas taught this technique to Brunson. The name DPL (Dollars Per Lead) comes from the metric that changed: inserting "don't you agree" and similar trial close language throughout a single webinar moved DPL from $9.45 to $16.50 — a 74.61% increase.

**How it works (Nahai/Cialdini: Commitment and Consistency):** Once a person has said yes repeatedly to small, low-risk questions, they experience psychological pressure to remain consistent with that "yes-saying" identity. The final "buy now" ask feels like a continuation of an established pattern rather than a jarring demand.

**Where to insert trial closes:**
- Opening (3-4 excitement questions: "Are you excited for this?")
- After every testimonial (4-5 closes per testimonial: "Can you see how this would change things for you?")
- After revealing each feature or benefit ("Can you see how this works?")
- Before the price reveal ("Are you ready to hear what this investment is?")
- Directly before the CTA ("Who wants this?", "Are you ready?")

**Example trial closes:**
- "Are you guys excited for this too?"
- "Can you see how this software will shortcut your success?"
- "Do you want a free copy of it?"
- "Can you see yourself doing this?"
- "Isn't that worth it?"
- "Don't you agree that [specific outcome] is worth [specific price]?"

**Rule:** Write 20-30 trial closes on cards. During any webinar or video recording, insert minimum 4-5 after every testimonial. Measure DPL before and after. This single technique, on its own, consistently moves DPL by 40-75%.
