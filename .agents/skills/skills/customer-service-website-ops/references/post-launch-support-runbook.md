# Post-launch Support Runbook

The first 90 days after launch decide whether the engagement renews. Run this period deliberately.

## Day 0: launch day

- Pre-launch checklist signed.
- Launch announcement template approved.
- On-call rota in place.
- Monitoring active and verified.
- Rollback plan tested.
- Hypercare window declared (typically 7 days).

Account lead sends a launch confirmation message:

> [Site] is live as of [time]. Here is the link, the launch report, and the support channels for the next 7 days. We will reply to anything within [SLA]. The next scheduled review is [date].

## Days 1–7: hypercare

- Daily monitoring sweep.
- Daily check-in message to the client even if nothing is wrong.
- Triage every incoming issue same-day.
- Active outreach to a small sample of real users for feedback (if access permits).

End of week 1: written hypercare report covering uptime, performance, accessibility, defects found and fixed, content corrections, and observed user behaviour.

## Days 8–30: stabilisation

- Move to standard SLA. Communicate the change explicitly.
- Implement quick wins surfaced in hypercare.
- Begin the experiment backlog from `experimentation`.
- Schedule the first monthly review.

## Days 31–60: evidence

- Run the first set of structured experiments.
- Capture the first quantitative results: traffic shape, search performance, conversion, form completion, scroll depth, customer feedback.
- Convert anomalies into either fixes or experiments.
- Update the case-study draft with anonymised early results.

## Days 61–90: renewal positioning

- Compare actual KPI movement to the discovery target.
- Prepare the renewal conversation: what worked, what is in flight, what to invest in next.
- If KPI is on or above target, propose continuation or expansion.
- If KPI is below target, surface honestly with a corrective plan before the renewal date.

## Recurring rituals during this window

- Monday: triage new tickets, plan the week.
- Wednesday: technical health sweep (uptime, performance, errors).
- Friday: client update message (even if short).
- Last business day of the month: monthly report via `monthly-report` skill.

## Rituals that protect the relationship

- "No surprises" rule: if the client will see a number on Monday, they hear it from the agency on Friday.
- "Two-channel" rule: ticket plus human message for any Sev-1, Sev-2, or service breach.
- "Silent month" rule: if a full month passes with no significant inbound, run a short proactive review. Quiet retainers are at risk.

## Handover documents

By day 30 the client must have:

- CMS training video and quick reference.
- Editorial guide and tone of voice summary.
- Form-handling, lead-routing, and escalation procedures.
- Performance, accessibility, and security baseline reports.
- Owner-facing dashboard with KPI definitions.

By day 90 the client must have:

- First 90-day report covering KPI movement, lessons, and the proposed next quarter plan.
- Updated service blueprint reflecting how the work actually runs.
- Calendar of the upcoming quarter's commitments.

## Renewal decision

Renew if:

- KPI movement is on track or above.
- Operational rhythm is healthy.
- Both sides find the relationship profitable.

Walk away cleanly if any of those is false. The right exit at month three saves a year of mediocre work.
