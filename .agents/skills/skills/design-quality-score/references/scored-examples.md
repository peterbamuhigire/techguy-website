# Scored Examples

**Read this when**: calibrating a new operator to the rubric, or
second-opinion scoring a template.

**Purpose**: Worked examples that show how the rubric translates into
actual scores. The examples are descriptive, not real client sites; the
first published Phase 12 benchmark sites will replace these with
public, linkable versions.

## Example 1 — Home page, East African SaaS B2B product

### Scenario
- Product: inventory management for independent pharmacies.
- Audience: pharmacy owners in Kampala and Nairobi.
- Design system: warm-neutrals palette, a serif/sans pairing.
- Hero copy: "Stop losing stock. Start tracking every pack, every
  supplier, every shift." Under it: a named photograph of a pharmacy
  owner at their counter, not stock.

### Scoring

| Category | Score | Evidence | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 Typographic rhythm | 9 | 6-step modular scale, body 18/28, hero 56/62. | Rhythm clean; hero could breathe more on mobile. |
| 2 Colour discipline | 10 | 4-colour palette used; accent only on primary CTA. | No off-palette values found. |
| 3 Spacing consistency | 9 | 8-point grid; section padding `py-24` desktop, `py-16` mobile. | One asymmetric margin on footer. |
| 4 Hierarchy strength | 10 | Squint-test passes; CTA clearly primary. | Strong visual entry point. |
| 5 Copy quality | 10 | Concrete value, no banned phrases, Krug's rules obeyed. | "Stop losing stock" is specific and measurable. |
| 6 Trust-signal placement | 9 | Named pharmacy owner photo, attributed testimonial at CTA. | Badge strip below fold — move above fold for +1. |
| 7 Section originality | 10 | Owner photo, concrete numbers ("pack-level tracking") in features. | Could not be lifted onto a competitor. |

**Overall score**: 9 (minimum across categories).

**Decision**: ships.

## Example 2 — Services page, West African law firm

### Scenario
- Firm: mid-size litigation and commercial practice in Lagos.
- Audience: corporate clients and litigants.
- Design system: navy-and-ivory palette, single serif.
- Hero copy: "We defend Nigerian businesses in court." Under it: three
  practice areas with the partner named for each.

### Scoring

| Category | Score | Evidence | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 Typographic rhythm | 10 | Consistent 4-step scale; 1.6 line-height. | Clean. |
| 2 Colour discipline | 10 | 3-colour palette, accent gold on CTA. | Restrained. |
| 3 Spacing consistency | 9 | 8-pt grid; minor drift on practice-area card heights. | One card runs 12 pt taller. |
| 4 Hierarchy strength | 8 | CTA present but competes with second "book consultation" button in header. | Two competing CTAs flagged. |
| 5 Copy quality | 8 | One sentence over 25 words in the About intro. | Rewrite required. |
| 6 Trust-signal placement | 10 | Bar Association badge above fold, named partners, dated case outcomes. | Strong. |
| 7 Section originality | 8 | Three-column features uses default icon set from the design starter. | Custom icons or remove icons for +1. |

**Overall score**: 8 (minimum).

**Decision**: ships only after operator remediates categories 4 and 5
and re-scores. If remediation moves them to 9 and 8 respectively,
overall becomes 8 and ships.

## Example 3 — Blog post template, East African food export

### Scenario
- Client: a coffee exporter publishing on origin stories.
- Audience: specialty coffee buyers globally.
- Design system: earth-tone palette with photographic hero images.

### Scoring

| Category | Score | Evidence | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 Typographic rhythm | 10 | Long-form body type excellent; pull-quotes well-weighted. | Reference-grade. |
| 2 Colour discipline | 9 | Accent green used sparingly; body text `#222`. | Near-perfect. |
| 3 Spacing consistency | 10 | Editorial-grade vertical rhythm. | Consistent. |
| 4 Hierarchy strength | 10 | Clear reading path; subhead scale matches reading weight. | Strong. |
| 5 Copy quality | 10 | Specific place names, farmer quotes attributed, no filler. | Editorial. |
| 6 Trust-signal placement | 10 | Farmer photos with names, cupping-score badges with year. | Specific. |
| 7 Section originality | 10 | Section-break patterns inspired by the sacks-and-rope visual motif. | Distinctive. |

**Overall score**: 10.

**Decision**: ships as a benchmark candidate for the Phase 12 library.

## Example 4 — Home page, generic SaaS landing (intentional bad example)

### Scenario
- Hero: "Welcome to Cloudify — the leading cloud solution for modern
  teams."
- Background: purple-to-blue gradient.
- Feature section: three columns with outline Heroicons, identical
  two-line blurbs.
- Team section: six circular headshots with "Founder", "Co-founder",
  "CTO", etc.

### Scoring

| Category | Score | Evidence | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 Typographic rhythm | 7 | One-off hero font size. | Off-scale. |
| 2 Colour discipline | 5 | SaaS default purple-to-blue gradient. | Banned. |
| 3 Spacing consistency | 8 | Grid respected. | Adequate. |
| 4 Hierarchy strength | 6 | Hero competes with a second CTA. | Two CTAs. |
| 5 Copy quality | 3 | "Welcome to", "leading", "modern". | Banned patterns triggered. |
| 6 Trust-signal placement | 4 | "Trusted by" row with three greyscale logos. | Banned trust pattern. |
| 7 Section originality | 3 | Generic three-column features. | Would fit any competitor. |

**Overall score**: 3.

**Decision**: does not ship. Remediation plan required before any
further scoring.

## Calibration use

A new operator scores Example 4 blind (without seeing the scores) and
compares to the published scores. The operator must arrive within one
point per category. Systematic over-scoring or under-scoring is
feedback that the operator needs a walkthrough with the programme
lead.
