# Acquisition and Retargeting

Use this reference when ecommerce decisions depend on how traffic arrives and how
non-buyers are recovered.

## Principle

Traffic quality changes page strategy. Cold paid traffic needs fast trust and a narrow
ask. Warm traffic can handle more detail and higher commitment.

## By traffic temperature

| Temperature | Page posture | CTA posture |
|---|---|---|
| Cold | Lead with problem, proof, product fit, and trust | Single primary CTA |
| Warm | Deeper comparison, objections, social proof | Add upsell or urgency if real |
| Existing customers | Focus on re-order, bundles, and account convenience | Make repeat purchase nearly frictionless |

## Retargeting sequence

1. Build audiences from product viewers, cart visitors, and checkout abandoners.
2. Retarget with the next message, not the same message:
   - Product viewers: explain fit and proof.
   - Cart abandoners: remove risk, clarify shipping, payment, or return concerns.
   - Repeat buyers: promote replenishment or complementary products.
3. Keep the landing step aligned with the ad.

## Guardrails

- Do not use discounts as the first answer to every abandonment problem.
- Fix trust, delivery clarity, and checkout friction before increasing ad spend.
- If retargeting ROI is weak, inspect offer-market fit and on-page trust before changing the media mix.
