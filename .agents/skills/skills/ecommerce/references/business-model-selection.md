# Ecommerce Business Model Selection

Use this reference when the site strategy depends on how the business actually sells and
fulfils orders.

## Start with the operating model

Decide which model the site is supporting:

| Model | Primary Website Job | Key UX Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Owned storefront | Convert and retain customers | Trust, product clarity, checkout speed |
| Marketplace-led seller | Capture intent and push to listing or owned channel | Margin protection, brand differentiation, review proof |
| Dropship / distributed fulfilment | Set expectations and reduce post-purchase anxiety | Delivery honesty, returns, stock transparency |
| Catalog + quote / WhatsApp commerce | Qualify and route high-intent buyers | Clear product information, fast contact path, trust |
| Subscription / repeat order | Maximize reorder and retention | Account simplicity, reminders, bundle logic |

## Decision rules

1. If margins are thin and repeat purchase matters, prioritize email capture, reorder
   flows, and post-purchase trust over vanity homepage polish.
2. If fulfilment is variable, design around delivery honesty.
   - Show location, lead time, stock truth, and return policy before checkout.
3. If the store also sells through marketplaces, the owned site must add value.
   - Better education
   - Better bundles
   - Better post-purchase relationship
   - Better trust and support
4. If the model is quote-led or WhatsApp-led, do not force a fake cart experience.

## Business-model checklist

- What is the primary sales channel today?
- What margin room exists for paid acquisition?
- What causes refund or support load now?
- What repeat-purchase or upsell path exists?
- What fulfilment promises can the business actually keep?

Do not recommend aggressive conversion patterns until these are answered.
