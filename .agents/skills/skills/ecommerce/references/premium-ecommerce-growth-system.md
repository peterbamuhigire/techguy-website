# Premium Ecommerce Growth System

Use this reference when planning or auditing ecommerce sites for serious revenue outcomes.
Pair it with `acquisition-and-retargeting.md` and the checkout, funnel, and analytics
companion skills.

## Commercial Frame

An ecommerce site is a growth system made of:

- Qualified traffic.
- Product discovery.
- Product decision support.
- Cart and checkout trust.
- Post-purchase retention.
- Measurement and experimentation.

Do not treat ecommerce as a catalogue plus payment button. The buyer journey includes
search, comparison, doubt, purchase, delivery anxiety, repeat purchase, review generation,
and referral.

## Product Page Requirements

Every important product page should answer:

- Is this for me?
- What problem does it solve or desire does it satisfy?
- What exactly do I get?
- What makes this option better than alternatives?
- What proof supports the claim?
- What does it cost in total?
- When and how will I receive it?
- What happens if it does not fit, breaks, arrives late, or disappoints me?
- What should I do next?

Implementation checklist:

- Clear product name and variant logic.
- Decision-quality photos, video, or diagrams.
- Benefits and specs separated for scanners.
- Price, total cost expectations, availability, shipping, returns, and warranty visible.
- Reviews, ratings, testimonials, press, or expert proof near the buy decision.
- Size, fit, compatibility, material, or technical information where relevant.
- Related products that help the decision, not random distractions.
- Add-to-cart event, product-view event, and product-detail engagement events.

## Category and Search

Category pages should help buyers narrow, compare, and choose:

- Filters match real decision attributes.
- Sort options reflect buyer intent: price, popularity, newest, rating, availability.
- Product cards expose enough information to compare without opening every item.
- Empty states recover the shopper with alternatives or support.
- SEO copy helps the buyer and search engine without pushing products below reach.

## Cart and Checkout

Cart and checkout must reduce uncertainty:

- Guest checkout by default unless account creation is genuinely required.
- No surprise shipping, tax, handling, or service fees.
- Payment methods shown before checkout friction starts.
- Return policy, support, delivery timing, and privacy visible near the final decision.
- Inline errors with clear recovery instructions.
- Mobile-friendly input types and tap targets.
- Confirmation page explains next steps and starts the post-purchase relationship.

## Traffic and Retargeting

Traffic plans should be tied to buyer intent:

- Search and content: collection pages, comparison pages, buying guides, FAQ, and product
  education.
- Email: welcome, browse abandonment, cart abandonment, post-purchase, replenishment,
  review request, winback, and VIP/customer segments.
- Social: demonstrations, proof, behind-the-scenes, customer outcomes, product education,
  and launches.
- Paid: message-matched landing pages or product pages with clean conversion events.
- Retargeting: show the next objection answer, not the same generic ad.

Do not use discounts as the default recovery mechanism. Clarify shipping, returns, fit,
proof, payment safety, and product value first.

## Measurement

Track:

- Sessions and revenue by channel.
- Product view rate from category pages.
- Add-to-cart rate.
- Cart-to-checkout rate.
- Checkout completion rate.
- Payment failure and form error rate.
- Average order value.
- Repeat purchase rate.
- Email capture rate.
- Abandoned-cart recovery rate.
- Refund/return rate.
- Customer lifetime value where data allows.

Segment by device, channel, product category, new vs returning visitor, and customer vs
prospect. Aggregate conversion rate hides too many ecommerce problems.

## Premium Acceptance Standard

An ecommerce build is not premium unless it includes:

- Product decision support.
- Checkout risk reduction.
- Traffic-to-retargeting path.
- Event tracking across the funnel.
- Post-purchase retention plan.
- First optimization backlog.
