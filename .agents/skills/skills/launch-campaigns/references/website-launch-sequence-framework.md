# Website Launch Sequence Framework

Use this reference to turn a timed offer or campaign into a coordinated website-centered sequence.

Primary source:

- Jeff Walker, *Launch*

Adaptation rule:

Keep the sequencing logic.
Strip out hype, manipulation, and fake scarcity.

## Core model

The durable launch architecture is:

1. prelaunch attention
2. prelaunch education
3. prelaunch proof
4. offer reveal
5. follow-up and objection handling
6. close

This can support:

- service launches
- waitlists
- webinars
- new offers
- open-days
- seasonal campaigns
- programme enrolment
- relaunches

## Sequence design

### Stage 1: Prelaunch attention

Goal:

- alert the right people that something relevant is coming

Useful assets:

- homepage announcement bar
- teaser landing page
- waitlist block
- blog article framing the market problem
- short email or WhatsApp teaser

CTA posture:

- `Join the waitlist`
- `Get notified`
- `Read the guide`

### Stage 2: Prelaunch education

Goal:

- teach the market why the problem matters and why the approach is credible

Useful assets:

- educational landing page
- blog article or guide
- webinar registration page
- FAQ teaser section

CTA posture:

- `See how it works`
- `Get the brief`
- `Register for the session`

### Stage 3: Prelaunch proof

Goal:

- show that the approach works and reduce anxiety before the offer opens

Useful assets:

- case-study page
- testimonial block
- proof-led email
- comparison page
- short founder note addressing the main objection

CTA posture:

- `See results`
- `Read the case study`
- `Check if this fits`

### Stage 4: Offer reveal

Goal:

- present the offer clearly, with terms, proof, and next step

Useful assets:

- launch page
- pricing page update
- application form
- launch email
- WhatsApp launch note

CTA posture:

- `Book your consult`
- `Apply now`
- `See plans and pricing`
- `Start the application`

### Stage 5: Follow-up and objection handling

Goal:

- answer hesitations without rewriting the whole offer each time

Useful assets:

- objection email sequence
- FAQ page update
- comparison page
- short proof snippets repurposed from case studies

Main objections to handle:

- Is this for me?
- Why now?
- What does it cost?
- What if it does not work?
- How much effort will this require from me?

### Stage 6: Close

Goal:

- make the deadline explicit and easy to act on

Useful assets:

- close-soon announcement
- final email
- homepage CTA swap
- final FAQ proof block

Rule:

State the real reason the window closes.
If there is no real reason, do not invent one.

## Asset map by offer type

### Premium service launch

- homepage hero update
- focused service or campaign landing page
- one case study
- one objection article or FAQ section
- email or WhatsApp follow-up

### Event, webinar, or open day

- registration page
- reminder sequence
- speaker or proof section
- deadline reminder

### Waitlist or coming-soon

- teaser page
- value proposition
- one reason to care now
- one reassurance line
- thank-you next-step page

## Africa adaptation rules

- Prefer clarity over hype.
- Use trust signals early: names, places, dates, proof, and practical next steps.
- Respect lower-bandwidth and mobile-first behavior: concise pages, clear CTAs, minimal friction.
- WhatsApp often matters as much as email; write both when appropriate.
- High-trust sectors need sober urgency, not launch theatrics.

## Output standard

Every launch plan should specify:

- audience segment
- campaign objective
- key promise
- proof sources
- primary objection
- asset list by stage
- CTA for each asset
- exact open and close dates when known
