# Customer Insight Loop

Quantitative tells you what changed. Qualitative tells you why. A premium site needs both running on a steady rhythm.

## Cadence

- Continuous: analytics, error tracking, search rank.
- Weekly: forms, search terms, on-site search queries.
- Monthly: voice-of-customer review (5 calls + 20 written interactions).
- Quarterly: deep customer interviews (5–7 fresh interviews).
- Annual: full customer survey.

## Quantitative pipeline

- Web analytics: site behaviour, conversion, retention cohorts.
- Search console: queries, rank, click-through, indexation.
- CRM: lead quality, pipeline movement, contract value, retention.
- Email and lifecycle: open, click, reply, unsubscribe.
- Paid media: cost per lead, cost per acquired customer, return on ad spend by campaign.
- Customer support: ticket volume, common topics, time to resolution.

Connect them. A standalone Google Analytics view is folklore. The same lead must be traceable from first session to signed contract.

## Qualitative pipeline

- Sales call review: at least one call per week, transcribed, tagged for objections and language.
- Support ticket review: monthly, tagged for structural cause.
- Customer interview: 5 per quarter, mix of recent buyers, churners, and stalled prospects.
- On-site feedback widget on key pages, reviewed weekly.
- Social listening for sector and brand terms, reviewed monthly.

Capture verbatim quotes. They feed copy, SEO, and conversion architecture, and they protect the agency from inventing what customers think.

## Synthesis

Once a month, run a two-hour insight session:

- Read the top 10 quotes from the month.
- Look at the three KPI movements that surprised the team.
- Cross-reference: which qualitative theme explains which quantitative movement?
- Decide: one experiment, one content fix, one operational fix to ship next month.

Hand the experiment to `experimentation`, the content fix to `content-writing` and `seo`, the operational fix to `service-blueprint-website-delivery`.

## Storage and access

- One canonical location for the insight library.
- Tags by theme, persona, stage, sector.
- Searchable by all team members.
- Permissioned: customer-identifying detail removed in the shared layer.

## Insight as a defence

When a stakeholder pushes a change "because the CEO read an article", insight wins:

- "Our customers' top objection in the last 30 days is X, not the article's claim."
- "When we tested that hypothesis last quarter, the result was Y."

Premium agencies argue with evidence, not with seniority.
