# KPI Tree and Definitions

A measurement system is only useful when each number means the same thing every time.

## The three-layer tree

- Top layer (1 metric): business outcome. Revenue, qualified pipeline, signed contracts, retention revenue, donations, applications. Whatever the business decides growth means.
- Middle layer (3–6 metrics): behavioural KPIs the website moves directly. Conversion rate, qualified leads per month, AOV, repeat purchase rate, completion rate, contact-to-customer ratio.
- Bottom layer (8–12 metrics): leading indicators. Organic traffic to commercial pages, search rank for intent terms, form completion rate, scroll depth on key pages, email click-through, retention cohort 30/60/90.

The tree must connect: every leading indicator points to one behavioural KPI; every behavioural KPI points to the business outcome.

## Definition card per KPI

For every metric on the dashboard, write a one-card definition:

- Name (in plain language).
- One-sentence purpose.
- Formula.
- Source system.
- Refresh cadence.
- Owner.
- Target for this period.
- Comparison period (last month / last quarter / last year).
- Floor (the level below which an alert fires).

Without a definition card, a number is folklore.

## Worked example: lead-generation site

- Top: signed contracts per quarter (CRM, finance reconciled).
- Middle: qualified leads per month, lead-to-opportunity ratio, opportunity-to-contract ratio.
- Bottom: organic sessions on commercial pages, intent-keyword rank, contact-form starts, contact-form completions, time-to-first-response, email reply rate, demo bookings, sales-call show-up rate.

## Worked example: ecommerce site

- Top: gross revenue + repeat-customer rate.
- Middle: site conversion rate, AOV, repeat purchase rate at 90 days, refund rate.
- Bottom: PDP-to-cart rate, cart-to-checkout rate, checkout completion rate, post-purchase email engagement, abandoned-cart recovery rate, search-driven sessions on category pages.

## Worked example: content authority site

- Top: qualified inbound enquiries + share of voice in target topics.
- Middle: organic sessions on pillar pages, email subscribers, lead magnet downloads.
- Bottom: ranking distribution for cluster keywords, internal-link click-through, scroll depth, repeat-visit rate, newsletter open and click rates.

## Target-setting rules

- Anchor targets to the client's own baseline, not industry averages, when 3 months of clean baseline exists.
- For new sites, anchor to a credible reference range and label it "expected range, to be replaced after 90 days".
- Use ranges, not single numbers, for early targets. Replace with single numbers after 6 months of stable measurement.
- Tie one target per quarter to budget reallocation. Targets the client cannot afford to miss focus minds.

## Anti-patterns

- "Bounce rate" as a KPI. It conflates intent-aligned single-page visits with failures.
- "Engagement" as a primary KPI when the business is transactional.
- A KPI nobody owns. Without an owner, the number drifts.
- A KPI without a target. Reporting becomes weather, not management.
- Twelve KPIs on the same page with equal weight. The eye gives up.
