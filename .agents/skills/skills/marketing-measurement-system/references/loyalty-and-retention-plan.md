# Loyalty and Retention Plan

A premium website earns its fee twice: once on first conversion, again on retention. Design retention into the site, not as an afterthought.

## Why retention belongs on the website

- New customer cost is rising; retained customers cost a fraction.
- The website is often the first and most repeated post-purchase touchpoint.
- Retention content drives organic visibility through natural sharing and search.
- Loyalty signals (reviews, repeat purchase, referral) compound brand strength.

## Retention layers to design

1. Onboarding. The first 7 to 30 days after first conversion. The site supports onboarding through dedicated guidance, training video, FAQ depth, and a clear next step. Measure: time-to-first-value, onboarding completion.
2. Use and habit. Repeat usage reasons that bring the customer back to the site. Account dashboards, content library, learning paths. Measure: repeat-visit rate, depth-on-return.
3. Recognition. Status, milestones, named relationships. Measure: response to recognition emails, referral generation rate.
4. Recovery. Detection of disengagement; outreach script; chance to fix. Measure: reactivation rate.
5. Advocacy. Easy paths to refer, review, share. Measure: referral conversion, review volume and rating distribution.

## Website-side commitments per layer

- Onboarding: dedicated post-purchase journey, content tailored to first-time customers, plain-language FAQ for the first questions.
- Use and habit: predictable update cadence (newsletter, podcast, webinar), gated content with real value, quick-load mobile experience because returning customers do most of their reading on phones.
- Recognition: named team page, real photos, specific achievements; loyalty page where applicable.
- Recovery: triggered email and message paths surfaced in CRM, supported by lightweight survey on the site.
- Advocacy: review submission flow, referral page with explicit value exchange, shareable case studies and content blocks.

## Cohort tracking

- Cohort by first-purchase month or first-conversion month.
- Track 30/60/90/180/365-day retention.
- Compare cohorts to surface acquisition-side issues (a bad cohort signals an upstream problem, not a retention failure).

## Loyalty signals to publish on the site

- Repeat customer percentage.
- Years of service or tenure of major clients.
- Long-term partner logos.
- Real reviews with names, dates, sectors.
- Updated case studies showing retention, not just launch.

These signals do double duty: they retain existing customers and persuade new ones.

## Anti-patterns

- "Loyalty programme" as a standalone feature with no integration into the rest of the site.
- Discount-only loyalty that trains low-margin behaviour.
- Newsletters that recycle marketing copy and decay subscribers over time.
- Recognition pages that go stale within a quarter because nobody owns them.
- Hiding retention behind a portal nobody logs into.

## Hand-off

- Onboarding pages and content → `content-writing`, `page-builder`.
- Triggered recovery flows → `email-sender`, `experimentation`.
- Advocacy pages → `agency-positioning`, `brand-storytelling`.
- Service-recovery pathways → `customer-service-website-ops`.
- Quarterly retention review → `quarterly-business-review.md`.
