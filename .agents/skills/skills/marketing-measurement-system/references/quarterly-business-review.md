# Quarterly Business Review

Monthly reports describe; quarterly reviews decide. Run this as a working session that ends in budget reallocation, not a polite read-back.

## Attendees

- Client decision maker.
- Client stakeholder owning the commercial outcome (sales, ops, growth).
- Agency principal.
- Agency strategist.
- One frontline person from each side (account lead, sales rep) for ground truth.

## Pre-read (sent 5 business days before)

- KPI tree with quarter movement vs target.
- Insight summary: top 5 themes from voice-of-customer.
- Experiment register: what shipped, what won, what lost.
- Content shipped and traffic shape.
- Operational health: incidents, SLA compliance, structural fixes.
- Renewal context: contract dates, scope, capacity.

## Agenda (90 minutes)

1. Reality check (10 min): top KPI vs target. Read the number; do not soften it.
2. What worked (15 min): three shipped changes with measurable result. Why did they work?
3. What did not work (15 min): three changes that did not move the metric. Why? What did we learn?
4. Customer reality (15 min): three verbatim quotes that surprised us. What do they mean for next quarter?
5. Decisions (25 min): three decisions for next quarter. Each decision names a change in budget, content, or operations.
6. Risks and dependencies (5 min): what blocks the plan?
7. Renewal path (5 min): scope, price, capacity for next quarter.

## Decision quality

A decision counts when it has:

- A specific change (build, write, hire, fire, stop, start).
- A budget shift (move money, time, or capacity).
- An owner (named person).
- A date.
- A measurable check (when do we know it worked?).

Without those five, the meeting did not produce decisions.

## Common failure modes

- Reviewing too many metrics. Pick three. The rest are reference.
- Over-presenting. The meeting is a conversation, not a deck read-out.
- Avoiding the bad number. Lead with the bad number; make the recovery plan visible.
- Skipping the customer voice. Quotes change the room when numbers do not.
- Not closing the renewal loop. Renewal is on the table at every quarterly, not surprise-ambushed at month 11.

## Output document (sent within 48 hours)

- Decisions with owner and date.
- Budget reallocation summary.
- Risks and what is being done about them.
- Updated KPI targets if the quarter showed targets were unrealistic or too easy.
- Calendar of next quarter's key dates.
- Renewal status update.

## Cadence rule

- Quarterly review every 90 days, scheduled at signing of contract.
- If a quarterly is moved twice without rescheduling, escalate. The relationship is at risk before the contract is.
