# Commentary Formulas Reference

## Traffic Commentary Templates

### Sessions increased (good month)
"[X]% more people visited [Client]'s website in [Month] compared to [Previous Month]. The primary driver was [organic search / a specific article / a campaign / seasonal uplift] — [specific explanation, e.g., 'the article we published on [topic] is now ranking position [X] for [keyword], bringing in [Y] new visitors per week who are actively researching your service']."

### Sessions decreased (explain + reassure)
"Website visits were [X]% lower in [Month] than [Previous Month]. [Context, e.g., 'This is a seasonal pattern — [Month] is historically quieter in your industry' OR 'A Google algorithm update affected rankings for several competitive keywords; we've identified the affected pages and have a plan to recover over the next 6-8 weeks']. [What you're doing about it, e.g., 'We're focusing this month on strengthening the content that was affected.']"

### Sessions flat (context matters)
"Traffic held steady in [Month] — [X] visits, consistent with [Previous Month]. [Context, e.g., 'This is expected during this phase of the SEO campaign; the content we've been building takes 3-6 months to fully index and rank. We're tracking 8 articles in the Google Search Console index that are not yet ranking — these will begin surfacing over the next 60-90 days.']"

### Organic search growing (reinforce the strategy)
"Organic search brought [X] visitors this month — [Y]% more than last month. This is the compounding effect of the content strategy: each article we publish builds authority that makes the next one rank faster. At this trajectory, organic search will overtake direct as your primary traffic source by [estimated month]."

## Conversion Commentary Templates

### Leads increased
"[Client] received [X] leads this month — [Y] more than last month. [Z]% came from organic search, which means these are people who found you while actively looking for [service]. These are your highest-quality leads because they've already decided they need [service] and are now choosing who to trust."

### Leads decreased
"Lead volume dipped to [X] this month from [Y] last month. [Cause, e.g., 'Traffic was lower, which accounts for most of the difference. The conversion rate actually improved slightly, meaning visitors who did come were more likely to enquire.']. [Action, e.g., 'We're adding a lead capture element to the [page] page this month, which we expect will recover 3-5 additional leads per month.']"

### Conversion rate improved
"Your conversion rate rose to [X]% — up from [Y]% last month. The [specific change, e.g., 'new homepage headline'] we implemented in [month] is showing results: fewer visitors are leaving without taking action. At [X]% with your current traffic volume, this represents approximately [calculated number] additional enquiries per month compared to where you started."

### Conversion rate flat
"Conversion rate held at [X]% — consistent with industry benchmark of [Y]% for [sector]. The focus this month shifts to growing traffic volume; at your current conversion rate, each additional 100 visitors produces [calculated number] new enquiries."

## SEO Commentary Templates

### Keyword moved to first page (position 1-10)
"'[Keyword]' moved from position [X] to position [Y] this month — crossing onto the first page of Google results. This query gets approximately [search volume] searches per month in [region]. At your current click-through rate, we expect this to generate [estimated clicks] additional visitors per month once the ranking stabilises over the next 4-6 weeks."

### Keyword improving but not yet page 1
"'[Keyword]' improved from position [X] to position [Y]. We're closing in on the first page — typically the jump from position 11-15 to positions 1-10 produces the most significant traffic impact. We're strengthening this page with [action, e.g., 'additional supporting content'] this month."

### Keyword declined
"'[Keyword]' dropped from position [X] to position [Y]. [Cause if known, e.g., 'A competitor published a stronger piece on this topic' OR 'Google's update re-weighted this content type']. We're [action, e.g., 'updating the article with current data and additional depth'] to recover this position."

### New content early performance
"The article '[Title]', targeting '[keyword]', was published on [date] and has been indexed. It's not yet ranking — new content typically takes 6-12 weeks to reach its stable position. We'll report on its ranking in next month's update."

## Work Done Commentary Templates

### Content published
"Published: '[Article title]' — This article targets '[keyword]', which receives approximately [X] monthly searches from people at the [awareness stage] of considering [service]. It is designed to answer the question '[question]' before they contact us, which means enquiries generated from this article will arrive pre-educated and better qualified."

### Technical fix
"Fixed: [Technical issue] — This was causing [specific problem, e.g., 'search engines to miss 4 key pages during crawl / visitors to land on broken URLs / form submissions to fail on mobile']. The fix ensures [specific outcome, e.g., 'all service pages are now included in Google's index / mobile users can submit the contact form without errors']."

### Design / UX change
"Updated: [Page/element] — The previous version had a [X]% [bounce rate / exit rate], indicating [interpretation]. The new version [what changed and the psychological reason, e.g., 'leads with the most common client question rather than a generic headline, which creates immediate relevance for the visitor']. We're measuring the change over the next 30 days."

### SEO optimisation
"Optimised: [Page] for '[keyword]' — [What was changed: title tag, meta description, heading structure, internal links, etc.]. This page was ranking position [X] with its previous optimisation. We expect this to push it into the [target position range] within 4-8 weeks."

## Seasonal Context Bank

Use these when explaining metric changes caused by seasonality:

- **January:** Post-holiday uplift in B2B; consumer spending recovery
- **February:** Quieter in most B2B sectors; Valentine's for retail
- **March–April:** Q2 budget cycles activate; strong for professional services
- **May:** Pre-rainy season surge for construction, outdoor, agriculture sectors in East Africa
- **June–July:** Mid-year budget reviews; potential pause in B2B decision-making
- **August:** Back-to-school for education sector; slower for luxury
- **September:** Strong Q3 close; B2B activity resumes post-summer
- **October:** Q4 preparation; strong for retail and e-commerce
- **November–December:** Peak for retail/e-commerce; slower for B2B (decision-makers unavailable)
- **Ramadan:** Consumer behaviour shifts significantly in Muslim-majority markets; timing varies by year
