# Conversion Rules — Split-Test-Proven Page Building Instructions

Source: Russell Brunson, *108 Proven Split Test Winners* (DotComSecrets Labs, 2013).
These are direct instructions for Claude when building pages. Not for the client.

---

## Core Philosophy

- Test each element independently. The offer > headline > CTA timing > price point > layout, in that priority order.
- A confused mind says no. When in doubt, remove an element rather than add one.
- Warm traffic (existing list) and cold traffic (ads, PPC) behave differently — design separate pages for each where possible.
- Aesthetics lose to conversion. A plain, ugly page that converts beats a beautiful page that does not.

---

## HEADLINES

### Specificity beats generality
- Use the exact real number, never a round approximation: "1,198 subscribers" not "1,000 subscribers." Specific numbers lifted conversions +33.56%.
- Ground headlines in a specific real event or past result: "How This Very Blog Grew to 10,000+ Subscribers in 12 Months" beat the generic equivalent by +58%.

### Colour
- Red headlines outperform blue or brand-coloured headlines by up to +313% in revenue on sales pages.
- Test red headlines on all sales and landing pages even when it looks aesthetically wrong.

### Copy frame
- Write all headlines and CTAs in the present tense. "Simple tips putting money in bank accounts RIGHT NOW" outperforms future conditional ("you will be able to").
- Benefits and ease-of-entry outperform feature descriptions. Lead with what the visitor gets and how fast, not what the product does.
- On lead magnet pages, curiosity-based headlines (never reveal the offer) can hit over 50% opt-in rates.

### Image interaction with headlines
- Never place a human face directly beside a headline. Faces pull eye-gaze away from copy and reduce headline reads.
- Use defocused or transparent background images to frame headline sections: +230% (light overlay) and +112% (dark overlay) vs. headline alone or with a competing face.
- When using faces near copy, ensure the subject faces toward the text, not toward the camera (+57% CTR for image-toward-text vs. image-away).

### Animation
- Animated headlines beat static on some products (+134%) but lose on others (-28.5%). Always test both. Never assume animated wins.

## CTAs AND BUTTONS

### Colour
- Red buttons outperform green buttons by +34%. Red implies urgency.
- Use your brand's highest-contrast colour as a fallback, but test red as the first challenger.

### Copy
- "Get Started Now" beat "Sign Up for Free" by +7%. Favour action-forward language that implies low commitment.
- Preferred CTA patterns: "Get Access," "Claim Your Copy," "Get Started Now," "Download Free," "Try It Free."
- Place benefit-action copy above the order button: "Get [Product Name]" beat "Limited Time Offer" above the button by +39%.
- For link CTAs, the pattern **"You should [action] [here]"** beats plain statements by +173%. Use "you" + direct command + "here."
- Adding "it's free" near a free-offer button lifted conversions +28%.

### Placement
- Show the order/CTA button immediately — never delay until price reveal. Tests across five separate experiments showed no-delay wins: +43% to +188% depending on context.
- Place CTA buttons below the video, not to the side. Right-side CTA "bombed completely" in Brunson's tests.
- For long free-trial sales letters, place a large CTA button at the top of the page (+33%).
- For high-engagement video sales letters, also test the button above the video (+38%).

### Size
- Larger, visually prominent buttons consistently outperform small or text-link CTAs. Make the primary CTA the most visually dominant element on the page (Von Restorff — only one element should look this way).

## FORMS

### Opt-in forms
- **Mad Libs style** (fill-in-the-blank sentence format) beat standard web forms by +25–40% across 12+ sites. Use for lead generation pages.
- **Micro-commitment first**: Ask a single small question ("How did you hear about us?") before asking for the email. This format nearly doubled opt-ins (+95%) and generated almost all sales in Brunson's controlled test.
- Always include "Other" as one of the survey options. Without it, visitors who cannot answer correctly drop off — adding "Other" produced +206% EPC.
- Never use Facebook Connect as the only sign-up option. Always offer email as an alternative (+65.27% registrations, +49.92% upsell sales).

### Order forms
- **Inline order forms** on the sales page (no redirect to a new order page) convert +75% better (1.4% → 2.5%).
- **Two-step order forms**: Collect name/email/phone first, payment second. Follow up with everyone who completes step 1 but not step 2. Call centres converted 10%+ of partial leads.
- Credit card fields belong on the **left side** of the form.
- Never leave blank space on an order form. Fill empty space with short testimonials (+44%, 14% → 21% conversion).
- For high-ticket offers ($100+), add a 2–3 payment plan option. Pre-select the full payment as the "preferred" option. Adding split-pay tripled conversion on a $497 offer (+217%).

### Security
- HTTPS on every page is a low-effort baseline: +29.26% on squeeze pages. Implement on every page in every funnel.
- Trust seals and security badges belong only on order forms and checkout pages. Placing them on opt-in pages or webinar registration pages reduces conversions by 13–14%.

## SOCIAL PROOF

### Placement
- Stack video testimonials directly below the sales video with the heading "Need More Proof?" This doubled conversion (+101%).
- Place testimonials at the top of the order form, above the offer recap. This lifted revenue per visitor +80% ($6.69 → $12.05).
- On service and product pages, place testimonials immediately after the claim they support — not in a separate standalone testimonials section.
- Fill any unused space on order forms with short text testimonials (+44%).

### Format
- **Facebook-style testimonials** (styled to look like actual Facebook posts) outperform standard quote-box testimonials by +296% in one test and +32% in another. People assume Facebook posts are genuine opinions, not marketing copy.
- A large smiling customer photo used as the headline (with the testimonial text as the headline copy) beat the standard control by +103% on SaaS and information product pages.
- A "spoiler box" below any sales video — listing 3–5 key points from the video — lifted conversion from 4% to 16% (+73%). Previewing the video content causes more viewers to watch fully.
- A feature box (6 key product highlights in a visually distinct block, inspired by Sony packaging) beat the classic P.S. line by +63% (3.08% → 5.01%). Include a CTA inside the feature box.

## LAYOUT AND VISUAL HIERARCHY

### Simplification
- Reduce choices to the minimum. Cutting 25 above-fold clickable options down to 5 lifted conversions +21%.
- Remove any page section that does not directly advance the buying decision. Stripped-down pages often beat content-heavy versions by +80%. Test stripped versions.
- For free webinar/lead magnet pages, minimal copy wins. Plain, simple pages often outperform expensive designed ones (+11% to +26%).

### Fold and scroll
- On long-form pages, place a visible CTA above the fold so engaged readers do not have to scroll to find it.
- Avoid anything that creates a false bottom (content ending visually before it actually does). Alternate section backgrounds to signal continuation.

### Pop-ups and bars
- A timed lightbox pop-up (appears after 10 seconds or on scroll depth) lifts email opt-ins +67% on content pages. Never use on landing pages. Configure so it never appears for existing subscribers.
- A Hello Bar or top notification bar on blog/content pages generated $276 extra per 1,000 visitors with zero cannibalization of other opt-ins (+6.13%).

### Mobile
- Do not assume responsive = better. In one Brunson test, a mobile-responsive version got +7% more opt-ins but -83% fewer purchases. Test the mobile experience thoroughly. A non-responsive page optimised for mobile may outperform a poorly adapted responsive layout.
- Exclude mobile from ad campaigns unless the landing page and offer are specifically optimised for mobile (desktop-only targeting achieved 40.8% lower cost per lead + 5% higher conversion).

## TRUST SIGNALS

### Where they belong
- Security seals: order forms and checkout pages only.
- Opt-in pages, webinar registration pages: no security seals (they hurt conversions by 13–14%).
- HTTPS: every page, everywhere.

### Social proof as trust signal
- Real-time buyer activity widgets (showing recent purchases) lifted conversions +5% across 840,000 visitors. For high-traffic pages, add a "who else bought this" element.

## PRICING AND OFFERS

### Expressing price
- Always express discounts as dollar amounts, not percentages. "$10 off" beat "10% off" by +22%.
- For cold social media traffic, lead with a payment plan as the primary offer, not as an alternative. Three payments of $33 beat $99 one-time by +75.6%.
- Three-part payment plans at lower per-payment amounts frequently outperform equivalent lump sums. In one test: 3×$37 generated $16.15 EPC vs. $37, $47, $77, $97, 2×$37, 2×$47, 2×$97, 3×$47.

### Pricing page design
- Keep 1–2 options maximum. Use one high-price anchor to make the primary offer look reasonable.
- A three-option pricing table with a middle price removed can increase profit +83%. A high-price decoy anchors buyers toward the primary offer.
- For offers above $500, add a financing option. Even including it in follow-up emails lifted sales +30–35%.

### Trial offers
- A 30-day trial beat a 7-day trial by +110% (0.326% → 0.709%).
- Removing the credit card requirement from trial offers lifted front-end sign-ups +500% and overall paid customers +50%.
- Framing the core offer as the product with the cheap item as a "free gift for trying" (Dan Kennedy style) beat leading with the cheap item and upselling: +109% revenue, 4.5× more recurring accounts.

## BRUNSON'S MASTER CONTROL BLUEPRINT

After 100+ tests, this is the winning page structure. Use it as the default starting point for new funnels and adapt from there.

### Squeeze / Lead Magnet Page
1. Curiosity-based headline (do not reveal the offer — hint at the transformation)
2. Three-step process header; step 3 label must say "Get Your Access" not "Finish"
3. Mini-survey: "How did you hear about us?" with exactly 4 options; fourth option must be "Other"
4. Email opt-in field (appears after survey selection)
5. No navigation links, no distractions

### Sales Video Page
1. Red headline above the video
2. Auto-play video with pause button; no fast-forward or rewind controls
3. Order button visible immediately below video (never hidden until price reveal)
4. Spoiler box: 3–5 bullet points listing key moments from the video
5. Inline order form on the same page (no redirect to new order page)
6. "Need More Proof?" section with video testimonials stacked below

### Order Form Layout
1. Facebook-style testimonials at the top right
2. Offer recap below the testimonials
3. Credit card fields on the left side
4. Trust seals on the form itself (nowhere else)
5. Short text testimonials filling any empty form space
6. Payment plan option for offers above $100

## COPY PATTERNS: TRIAL CLOSES

Trial closes are short yes-questions inserted throughout copy to build a "yes momentum" before the CTA. Brunson's team added "don't you agree" style micro-closes throughout a webinar and increased Dollars Per Lead by +74.61%.

Use trial closes in:
- Video sales letters (after every benefit reveal)
- Long-form sales pages (after every testimonial block)
- Webinar scripts (minimum 4–5 after every testimonial)
- Email sequences (before a click CTA)

Example patterns:
- "Can you see how [outcome] would change your results?"
- "Makes sense, doesn't it?"
- "Isn't that exactly what you need?"
- "You can see why [X number] of people have already [taken action], right?"

Insert these in the copy at least once every 3–4 paragraphs on long-form pages.

- [ ] Headline uses a specific real number, not a round figure

- [ ] All CTA copy is in the present tense
- [ ] Primary CTA button is immediately visible — not hidden until price reveal
- [ ] CTA button is below the video, not to the side
- [ ] "It's free" added near CTA if the offer is free
- [ ] Testimonials use Facebook-style formatting
- [ ] Testimonials are placed at the top of the order form
- [ ] No empty space on order forms — filled with short testimonials
- [ ] Spoiler box present below any video sales letter
- [ ] Order form is inline on the page (no redirect)
- [ ] Credit card fields on the left side
- [ ] Security seals on order form only (not on opt-in or registration pages)
- [ ] Trust seals are absent from opt-in and webinar registration pages
- [ ] Page has no more than 2 pricing options (1 anchor + 1 primary)
- [ ] Discount expressed as a dollar amount, not a percentage
- [ ] Mobile experience tested manually — do not assume responsive CSS is enough
- [ ] HTTPS enabled on every page
- [ ] For free trial offers: no credit card required (if processor allows)
