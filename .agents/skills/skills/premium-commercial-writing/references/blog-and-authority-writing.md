# Blog And Authority Writing

Use this reference for blog posts, pillar pages, authority articles, educational sequences, and sales-enablement content.

## Strategic Role

Premium blogs should not be content filler. Each article should do at least one job:

- answer a buyer question
- rank for a useful search intent
- support a sales conversation
- build topical authority
- explain a tradeoff
- compare options
- show experience
- reduce support burden
- prepare a reader for an offer

## Article Brief

Define:

- audience
- query or buyer question
- awareness level
- article type
- promise to the reader
- proof or experience available
- internal links
- next action
- author/expert basis
- update schedule if the topic changes often

## Article Types

| Type | Use when | Required angle |
|---|---|---|
| Cost | buyers ask price before trust is built | ranges, factors, tradeoffs, what changes cost |
| Comparison | buyers are choosing between options | decision criteria and fit, not false neutrality |
| Problems | buyers fear failure | known risks, prevention, honest limits |
| Best/options | buyers need a shortlist | criteria, who each option fits, next step |
| How-to | buyers need practical progress | steps, pitfalls, examples, when to get help |
| Case insight | proof must be taught | context, decision, result, lesson |
| Opinion | authority needs a stance | reasoned point of view with evidence |
| Pillar | topic requires durable authority | comprehensive structure and cluster links |

## Writing Standard

Open with the real reader problem. Do not begin with generic industry scene-setting.

Strong articles:

- answer the main question early
- explain tradeoffs honestly
- include examples, experience, or process detail
- vary sentence and paragraph length
- include expert or author context relevant to the topic
- link to next-step resources or services naturally
- make one clear point per section

## Authority Signals

Include what is true and useful:

- named author with topic-relevant experience
- original examples, screenshots, photos, data, or client patterns
- update date for changing topics
- sources for factual claims
- clear limits of advice
- links to related service pages and cluster articles

Do not invent credentials or imply professional advice where the client cannot provide it.

## Series And Cluster Pattern

Turn isolated posts into a system:

1. pillar article for the broad topic
2. cost article
3. comparison article
4. problem/risk article
5. best/options article
6. case or example article
7. offer page or consultation page

Each article should link to the next logical decision point.

## AI-Search Readiness

For topics likely to be summarized by answer engines:

- include a concise answer block near the top
- use descriptive headings phrased around buyer questions
- define entities and terms cleanly
- make factual claims easy to attribute
- include author and organization context
- avoid burying the answer under brand storytelling

Coordinate with `seo/references/llms-txt-and-ai-crawlers.md` for site-level crawler files.

## Blog QA

- [ ] The article answers a real question, not just a keyword.
- [ ] The answer appears early.
- [ ] The article includes experience, proof, or examples.
- [ ] The author bio is specific to the article topic.
- [ ] Internal links support a cluster or sales path.
- [ ] The final CTA matches reader readiness.
- [ ] The article can be excerpted into an email, social post, or sales follow-up.
