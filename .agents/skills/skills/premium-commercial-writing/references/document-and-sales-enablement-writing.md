# Document And Sales Enablement Writing

Use this reference for proposals, briefs, guides, lead magnets, sales one-pagers, case-study PDFs, onboarding documents, and client-facing commercial documents.

## Document Job

A premium document should do one clear job:

- help a buyer understand a decision
- prepare a prospect for a sales call
- justify a premium fee
- reduce perceived risk
- transfer authority
- explain scope and exclusions
- support internal approval
- make delivery easier after the sale

## Commercial Document Brief

Define:

- reader and role in the buying process
- decision the document supports
- offer or recommendation
- proof needed
- objections likely to be raised internally
- action after reading
- format constraints
- approval or compliance requirements

## Structure Pattern

Use this order for most sales-enablement documents:

1. Executive answer: the point, recommendation, or promise.
2. Context: why the issue matters now.
3. Diagnosis: what is causing the problem or opportunity.
4. Options: tradeoffs, not a fake single path.
5. Recommendation: why this path fits.
6. Proof: examples, numbers, method, credentials, or case evidence.
7. Scope: what is included and excluded.
8. Risk: what could go wrong and how it is handled.
9. Next step: decision, call, approval, payment, or implementation path.

## Lead Magnet Standard

A lead magnet must be useful without giving away a confused checklist.

It should:

- solve a narrow problem
- show the brand's method
- give the reader a quick win
- create a natural next step
- qualify the right buyer
- avoid pretending a complex service can be reduced to a free template

## Proposal And Scope Language

Premium proposal language should:

- frame the website or content as a business asset
- connect work packages to outcomes
- state assumptions and dependencies
- show what the client must provide
- protect scope boundaries
- explain price through value, risk, and effort
- include acceptance criteria where possible

Avoid apologetic pricing, vague deliverables, and promises that depend on factors outside the team's control.

## Case Study PDF Or Sales One-Pager

Include:

- client context
- problem and stakes
- work performed
- proof of change
- why the result matters
- relevant method
- buyer-fit cue
- CTA to the next conversation

If the document will be sent before a meeting, include enough context to stand alone.

## Document QA

- [ ] The reader can tell what decision the document supports.
- [ ] The opening gives the main answer quickly.
- [ ] Proof supports the recommendation.
- [ ] Scope and exclusions are clear.
- [ ] The next step is explicit.
- [ ] The language is confident without overclaiming.
- [ ] The document can be skimmed by a busy decision-maker.
