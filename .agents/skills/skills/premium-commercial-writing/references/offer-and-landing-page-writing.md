# Offer And Landing Page Writing

Use this reference for offer pages, landing pages, campaign pages, waitlists, lead magnets, webinars, applications, and direct-response flows.

## Offer Brief

Before writing, define:

- offer name
- audience and exclusion
- traffic source and temperature
- buyer awareness level
- desired outcome
- mechanism or method
- deliverables
- price or price posture
- proof available
- risk reversal or reassurance
- urgency reason, if real
- direct CTA and fallback CTA

## Page Strategy By Offer Type

| Offer type | What the page must sell |
|---|---|
| Lead magnet | the usefulness of the resource and why it is worth the opt-in |
| Waitlist | the future result, qualification, and reason to join early |
| Consultation | the quality, relevance, and usefulness of the conversation |
| Webinar/event | the problem, promise, speaker credibility, and time value |
| Productized service | outcome, scope, process, proof, price logic, and next step |
| High-ticket service | fit, diagnosis, risk reduction, credibility, and business value |
| Ecommerce/product | product fit, sensory proof, terms, delivery, guarantee, and trust |

## Conversion Sequence

Default structure:

1. Hero: audience, result, differentiator, proof cue, CTA.
2. Problem: name the friction, cost of delay, and buyer context.
3. Promise: state what changes and why this approach is different.
4. Mechanism: explain the method enough to feel credible.
5. Proof: results, examples, process evidence, testimonials, credentials.
6. Offer: what is included, how it works, who it fits.
7. Value and price logic: compare against alternatives, not only competitors.
8. Risk reduction: guarantee, fit check, transparent terms, support, or limitation.
9. CTA: direct action with next-step microcopy.
10. FAQ: remaining objections and practical questions.

For long-form sales pages, coordinate with `long-form-sales-copy`.

## CTA Ladder

Match commitment to trust:

- Low trust: read guide, see examples, watch walkthrough, compare options.
- Medium trust: get brief, check fit, join waitlist, request estimate.
- High trust: book consult, start application, see pricing, buy now.

CTA microcopy should clarify:

- what happens next
- time required
- cost or no-cost status
- who responds
- what the buyer should prepare
- when they will hear back

## Proof Placement

Place proof where doubt appears:

- hero claim: proof cue or recognizable credential
- mechanism: process screenshot, method summary, or example
- price: comparison, value explanation, or scope clarity
- CTA: reassurance and next-step details
- FAQ: direct answers to common resistance

## Ethical Urgency

Use urgency only when it is real:

- event date
- limited capacity
- application window
- expiring bonus with a reason
- price change already scheduled
- operational deadline

Do not use false countdowns, invented scarcity, or pressure that damages premium trust.

## Offer Page QA

- [ ] The offer is clear before the design asks for action.
- [ ] The page qualifies the right buyer.
- [ ] The page handles the main buying objections.
- [ ] The CTA is specific and repeated at natural decision points.
- [ ] Proof appears before high-commitment CTAs.
- [ ] Any urgency is true and explainable.
- [ ] The page can be handed to `seo`, `page-builder`, and `cro-audit` without reinterpreting the strategy.
