# Website Page Writing Patterns

Use this reference when writing or revising commercial website pages.

## Universal Page Brief

For each page, define:

| Field | Decision |
|---|---|
| Page role | home, service, about, pricing, contact, case study, FAQ, resource, support |
| Audience | who this page is primarily for |
| Intent | what brought them here |
| Main question | what they need answered before moving on |
| Proof burden | what they must believe |
| Primary CTA | the commercially valuable next step |
| Secondary CTA | lower-commitment next step |
| SEO/GEO role | query, entity, comparison, local, or answer target |
| Internal links | where this page should send qualified readers |
| Measurement | event or conversion to track |

## Homepage

The homepage should orient and qualify.

Required sequence:

1. clear category and audience signal
2. premium promise or business outcome
3. proof cue near the hero
4. primary and secondary CTA
5. problem or change in the buyer's world
6. offer/service paths
7. proof and trust architecture
8. process or plan
9. case evidence or examples
10. final CTA with reassurance

Do not make the homepage a complete sales page unless the site has one offer and one dominant action.

## Service Page

The service page should help the buyer decide whether the service fits.

Include:

- who the service is for and not for
- the problem it solves
- outcomes and deliverables
- method or mechanism
- proof and examples
- timeline or working model
- price posture or qualification cue when appropriate
- FAQ that handles real buying objections
- CTA matched to buyer readiness

Avoid long feature lists that never explain business value.

## About Page

The about page should transfer trust, not narrate the founder's entire life.

Include:

- why the organization is credible for this buyer's problem
- the belief or standard behind the work
- relevant experience and proof
- team, process, or operating principles
- buyer-relevant story
- next step back into services, proof, or contact

The customer remains the hero. The organization is the guide with standards.

## Pricing Or Packages Page

The pricing page should reduce uncertainty.

Include:

- package fit and exclusions
- what changes for the buyer at each level
- proof that supports the fee
- scope boundaries
- risk reducers
- FAQs on price, timeline, process, ownership, support, and hidden costs
- CTA for purchase, consultation, or fit check

Do not hide every price signal when the market needs qualification.

## Contact Or Consultation Page

This page sells the conversation.

Include:

- who should book or contact
- what happens after submission
- expected response time
- what to prepare
- whether payment or commitment is required
- alternative contact paths where relevant
- reassurance about fit and privacy

Do not treat the page as an administrative form only.

## Case Study

The case study should make the next buyer believe the process and outcome.

Use this order:

1. client context and constraint
2. starting problem
3. stakes and failure risk
4. strategy or diagnosis
5. work performed
6. evidence of change
7. what the result means commercially
8. lessons or reusable insight
9. CTA to a related service or consultation

If metrics are confidential, use approved ranges, qualitative proof, or process evidence.

## FAQ

The FAQ is sales architecture, not leftover content.

Good FAQ questions handle:

- cost and value
- fit
- timeline
- process
- risk
- ownership
- support
- alternatives
- limitations
- next steps

Answer plainly. Do not turn every answer into a pitch.
