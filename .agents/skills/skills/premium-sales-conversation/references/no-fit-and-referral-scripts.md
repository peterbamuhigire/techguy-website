# No-fit and Referral Scripts

A respectful no is a referral that closes elsewhere. Use these patterns within 24 hours of a misfit call.

## When to send a no-fit message

- Fit score is 4–6 and budget cannot reach the floor even at reduced scope.
- Decision rights are absent and there is no path to the decider.
- The brief is incompatible with premium standards (template-only, no content investment, no measurement appetite).
- The cultural fit is wrong (the conversation already shows poor faith).

## Pattern

- Open by thanking the buyer for the conversation.
- State the fit decision honestly in one sentence.
- Give the reason in business terms, not in apology.
- Offer one referral or one alternative path.
- Close with the door open for a future conversation if conditions change.

## Template — budget mismatch

"Thank you for the call this week. After reviewing what you described — [project goal] — I do not think we are the right partner at the budget you have available. To deliver this at our standard the right floor is around [X], and a thinner build at a lower fee would not give you the result you described.

A team that does strong work in your range is [referral]. I have introduced clients to them before. If your budget changes or the scope shifts, please come back to us. We would be glad to have a second conversation."

## Template — decision rights missing

"Thank you for the time today. The next useful conversation needs [decider role] in the room, since the choices we will be discussing depend on their sign-off. If you can include them in a follow-up, I will hold a 45-minute slot in the next two weeks. If that is not possible right now, I would rather wait than start without alignment."

## Template — scope or standard mismatch

"Thank you for the conversation. After reviewing the brief, the scope you described is best served by a templated builder, not by a premium custom build. We do not deliver at the templated tier because we cannot keep our standard for performance, accessibility, and measurement at that price.

If template work is what you need, [referral / platform] is a credible route. If your priority shifts towards measurable lead generation or conversion, please come back to us."

## Template — bad-faith signals

When the conversation surfaces dishonest framing, undisclosed competitive bids without scope, or aggressive negotiation before any value exchange:

"Thank you for the call. We have decided not to put forward a proposal for this engagement. We wish you well with the project."

No referral. No second chance. Document the signal in the CRM so the agency does not re-engage.

## Referrals as an asset

- Maintain a short list of pre-vetted partners by tier and sector.
- Send the referral with a one-paragraph context note. Do not send a cold introduction.
- Track referral outcomes. The referral network is a long-term commercial asset; reciprocity matters.
- Decline referrals you do not trust. A bad referral burns the agency's name even when the agency was not on the project.

## Re-engagement window

If a no-fit prospect's situation changes (new funding, new decision, new scope), re-engagement is welcome. Set a 6-month check-in only if the prospect explicitly opted in. Otherwise let them initiate.
