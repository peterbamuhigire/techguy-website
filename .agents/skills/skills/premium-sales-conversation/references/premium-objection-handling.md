# Premium Objection Handling

Objections are a sign the buyer is engaging. Treat them as data. Never discount in the moment of the objection.

## Pattern

For every objection: acknowledge → reframe → evidence → confirm.

- Acknowledge the objection in the buyer's words. Do not flinch.
- Reframe to the underlying concern. Most price objections are risk objections.
- Provide concrete evidence: a number, a case, a guarantee, a structural choice.
- Confirm whether the concern is now resolved before moving forward.

## Price

- "It is more than we expected."
  - Acknowledge: "It is more than the average local quote, yes."
  - Reframe: "What we are pricing is the outcome — qualified leads, conversion, and a site that does not need a rebuild in two years. The hourly comparison hides that."
  - Evidence: name a comparable client with a measurable result and the cost of the alternative (rebuild, lost leads, brand damage).
  - Confirm: "Given that, is the question still price, or is it the timing of the spend?"

- "We can get this cheaper."
  - Acknowledge: "You can. Several local providers will quote less."
  - Reframe: "Cheaper sites usually solve a different problem. They do not include strategy, content depth, conversion architecture, accessibility, performance, measurement, and improvement. If you do not need those, we are not the right partner."
  - Evidence: show the comparison table from the offer ladder.
  - Confirm: "Is the cheaper scope what you actually want, or is it what you thought you had to settle for?"

## Timeline

- "We need it in four weeks."
  - Acknowledge: "Understood — that is the deadline."
  - Reframe: "We can ship a credible launch in four weeks if scope is reduced to one journey, one language, and a 5-page core. Full premium scope needs eight to ten."
  - Evidence: show prior project timelines and what was included at each duration.
  - Confirm: "Which trade-off do you prefer — full scope at the longer timeline, or staged launch at four weeks?"

## Scope

- "Can you just do the design and we will build it?"
  - Acknowledge: "Some agencies do that."
  - Reframe: "Our premium standard depends on the design surviving build. When the build is split, the conversion architecture and performance budget often do not survive. We are accountable for the result, so we own both."
  - Evidence: cite a specific case where a split build broke a known KPI.
  - Confirm: "If we cannot guarantee the result without owning the build, is that a deal-breaker, or do we adjust the scope another way?"

## Technology

- "We are committed to [legacy CMS / templated builder]."
  - Acknowledge: "That is your standard, and changing it is a separate decision."
  - Reframe: "Inside that platform, here is what we can deliver and what we cannot." Be specific about which premium standards the platform breaks.
  - Evidence: provide a short technical note on capability gaps and the cost of compensating.
  - Confirm: "Knowing those gaps, do you want to proceed inside the platform, or is the platform itself open for review?"

## Trust

- "We have been burned by an agency before."
  - Acknowledge: "That happens, and it changes how the next engagement should work."
  - Reframe: "We work in stage gates with explicit decisions and exit points. You can leave with the work to date at any gate."
  - Evidence: walk through the kickoff agreement structure and the gate dates.
  - Confirm: "If we make the first gate the paid discovery only, with a clear exit, does that match how you want to start?"

## Local and regional context

- "Our buyers are in [region]; will premium fees translate to value here?"
  - Acknowledge: "Local fee expectations are real."
  - Reframe: "Premium here is calibrated to local mobile reality, payments, languages, and trust patterns — that is why our work performs locally without copying a Western template."
  - Evidence: cite local performance numbers, mobile-money UX, low-bandwidth budgets, language support.
  - Confirm: "Is the concern price, or whether premium is built for your buyers? They are different questions."

## Stalls

- "Let me think about it."
  - Acknowledge: "Of course."
  - Reframe: "What specifically do you want to think through? Usually it is one of three things: price, timing, or fit." Surface which one.
  - Confirm: "Is now a good time to put a date in the diary for the decision, so we hold capacity?"

## Hard no path

If after one round of objection handling the prospect is still off-fit, do not press. Send a clean no-fit message within 24 hours, with one referral if appropriate.
