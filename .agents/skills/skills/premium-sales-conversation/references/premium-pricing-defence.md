# Premium Pricing Defence

Premium fees are defended with structure, not with charm. The structure is set before the call.

## Pre-call: anchors

Before any sales conversation, fix:

- The agency's published fee floor for each offer in the ladder.
- The minimum monthly retainer for ongoing work.
- The paid discovery fee for the next-step agreement.
- The capacity calendar that justifies "we cannot start before X".

These are fixed for the quarter. They do not move per call.

## In-call: anchor early

- State the order-of-magnitude fee within the first half of the call, before objections form. "Premium engagements in this category typically start at [floor]."
- Tie the anchor to the buyer's outcome, not to hours.
- If the buyer flinches, ask: "Is the issue the size of the investment, or the size of the outcome?"

## Outcome pricing logic

Express price as a fraction of the outcome it is designed to produce:

- For lead-gen sites: price vs additional qualified leads per quarter at the buyer's average deal size.
- For ecommerce: price vs additional revenue at current conversion improvement targets.
- For brand and authority: price vs cost of one bad procurement decision the site is designed to prevent.

Show the math in the proposal. If the math is unfavourable, the brief is wrong, not the price.

## Defending against discount asks

- Never discount without removing scope. A "10% discount" without scope removal trains the buyer to expect more.
- Offer a smaller, complete scope at a lower fee — not a half-discounted version of the full scope.
- Offer phased payment when the buyer's cash flow is the issue. Cash-flow problems and value problems are different.
- Walk away when the discount ask exceeds the agency's margin floor. The contract is unprofitable from day one.

## Premium signals during the call

- Specificity: numbers, dates, names, sectors. Vagueness tells the buyer to negotiate harder.
- Calendar discipline: starting "next month, second slot, after the [name] launch". Available now is fine for the first call. Available now for two months running is a price-cut signal.
- Reference rights: "we can introduce you to [client] if you want to verify".
- Reciprocity: ask for the buyer's commitment in return ("we will hold capacity for two business days while you decide").

## Premium signals after the call

- Same-day written next step, with the floor price stated.
- Paid discovery option as the easiest first commitment.
- Optional retainer included in the proposal so it is a normal next step, not a surprise.

## Floor price violations

The floor price is the floor. If a senior salesperson goes below it without written approval, the agency loses on the engagement and the next three engagements that learn from that buyer's referral.

If the agency cannot win at the floor consistently, the offer needs upgrading before the floor moves down.
