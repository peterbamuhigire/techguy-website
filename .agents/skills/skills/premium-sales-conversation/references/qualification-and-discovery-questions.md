# Qualification and Discovery Questions

A premium sales call is a diagnosis, not a pitch. Use these question patterns. Listen more than you speak.

## Trigger and motivation

- What happened in the last 30 days that made now the right time to look?
- If you do nothing for the next 12 months, what changes for the business?
- Who else is feeling this — sales, operations, leadership? How do they each describe it?

## What has been tried

- Walk me through what you tried before this conversation.
- What was the closest you came to solving it? Why did it stop short?
- If you had unlimited budget, what would you build first? Why that?

## Outcome and measurement

- If we had this conversation in twelve months and it was a clear win, what would have changed in the business?
- What metric on a dashboard would move? By how much?
- Who would notice? Who would care most?

## Decision rights

- Who else needs to be in the room before this becomes real?
- Who signs the contract? Who signs the invoices?
- What has stopped a project like this from happening before?

## Budget and value

- Have you set aside a budget range, or are you sizing it from this conversation?
- What is the cost to the business of the current situation, per month?
- For context, premium engagements in this category usually start in the [X]–[Y] range. Is that aligned to where you are?

## Timeline and operational readiness

- What date is this useful by? What event drives that date?
- Who on your side will own content, decisions, and approvals?
- What other initiatives are competing for that person's attention right now?

## Fit and constraints

- Are there technologies, vendors, or partners you must keep or must avoid?
- Are there compliance, regulatory, or accessibility requirements we should bake in from day one?
- What is non-negotiable for you in how an agency works with you?

## Fit scoring

Score each on 0–2:

- Trigger and motivation (real urgency vs vague interest).
- Decision rights (we can talk to the decider this month vs unknown).
- Budget order of magnitude (matches premium floor vs below floor).
- Operational readiness (named owner with capacity vs nobody).
- Sector and capability fit (we have proof in this domain vs first-time).
- Cultural fit (treats the agency as a partner vs as a vendor).

Sum:

- 10–12: pursue. Move to proposal or paid discovery this week.
- 7–9: paid discovery only. The unknowns must be priced.
- 4–6: low fit. Refer or send a templated decline within 24 hours.
- 0–3: hard no. Decline with respect and offer one referral.

The rule is enforced. A misfit close costs more than the lost revenue.
