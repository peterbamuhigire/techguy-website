# Legal Sector Templates

Design strategy for law firms, attorney practices, and legal services websites. Built from "Internet Branding for Lawyers: Building the Client-Centered Website" (Lantz, ABA Law Practice Management Section).

## Files

| File | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **sector-brief-template.md** | Customisation questionnaire (REQUIRED) |
| **design-tokens.md** | Reference palettes by practice area (DO NOT USE AS-IS) |
| **legal-branding.md** | Color psychology, typography, voice, the Value Proposition Framework, One Great Thing |
| **client-trust-design.md** | Client psychology, homepage architecture, attorney profiles, trust signals, contact forms, local SEO, conversion optimization |
| **legal-type-patterns.md** | 8 practice area types with distinct design systems: PI, Family, Criminal, Estate, Immigration, Business, Employment, Workers' Comp |
| **ethics-constraints.md** | Bar advertising rules, prohibited claims, disclaimer requirements, developer responsibilities |

## Usage

### For Any Law Firm Website

1. Read **legal-branding.md** — understand color psychology, voice, and the client-centered vs. traditional split
2. Read **legal-type-patterns.md** — find the firm's primary practice type and its design direction
3. Read **client-trust-design.md** — homepage architecture, attorney profiles, trust signals, local SEO
4. Read **ethics-constraints.md** — understand what cannot be on the site; include required disclaimers
5. Copy **sector-brief-template.md** to `docs/sector-brief.md` and customise fully
6. Run the design-system skill

### The Most Important Principle

Law firm websites fail when they market to other lawyers instead of frightened people searching for help. Every design and copy decision must answer the prospective client's question: **"Does this firm understand my situation and can they help me?"**

The Five Key Messages every law firm site must communicate:
1. We understand your specific problem
2. We have the right expertise for your situation
3. You will be served personally (not handed to a paralegal)
4. We have helped clients like you (social proof)
5. The first step is easy (free consultation, response commitment)

### Ethics Note

Before any law firm site goes live, the attorney must review all content against their state bar's advertising rules. Never publish "expert" claims, outcome guarantees, or testimonials without checking jurisdiction-specific rules. Include attorney-client disclaimers on all contact forms. See **ethics-constraints.md** for the full pre-launch checklist.
