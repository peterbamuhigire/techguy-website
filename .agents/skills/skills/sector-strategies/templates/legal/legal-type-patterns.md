# Legal Sector Type Patterns

## Overview

Eight distinct law firm types — each requires different design psychology, trust architecture, and content strategy. A personal injury firm and a corporate M&A firm serve clients in entirely different emotional states and must communicate differently.

---

## Type 1: Personal Injury

**Client emotional state**: Hurt, confused, financially worried, distrustful of insurance companies
**Primary fear**: Accepting a low settlement, not getting full compensation, being manipulated

**Design Direction**
- Color: Strong trust blue + warm accent (amber or burgundy) — confident and compassionate
- Typography: Clear serif heading + highly readable body (min 18px)
- Photography: Attorneys in natural settings, not courts; empathy over authority
- Tone: "We fight for people like you" — advocacy language

**Content Priorities**
1. What types of accidents you handle (car, truck, slip & fall, medical malpractice)
2. What the process looks like (remove mystery)
3. No upfront costs (contingency fee explanation prominent)
4. Case results (where ethics permits — with disclaimer)
5. Client testimonials (named, specific outcome)

**Above-the-fold must answer**:
- "Do they handle my type of accident?"
- "Can I afford this?"
- "Are they successful?"

**SEO page strategy**: Separate pages for car accidents, truck accidents, motorcycle, slip and fall, dog bite, medical malpractice, wrongful death. City-level pages for each.

**Trust signals that convert**: Contingency fee guarantee ("No fee unless we win"), Google review aggregate rating, named case results, Super Lawyers / Martindale rating

## Type 2: Family Law (Divorce, Custody, Adoption)

**Client emotional state**: Heartbroken, protective of children, terrified about the future, often in acute crisis
**Primary fear**: Losing custody, financial devastation, extended conflict, children suffering

**Design Direction**
- Color: Calming teal + sage — resolution, not battle
- Typography: Warm serif headings + humanist sans body
- Photography: Attorneys in warm, non-intimidating settings; avoid anything that implies courtroom drama
- Tone: Empathetic first, competent second — "We help families navigate this"

**Content Priorities**
1. Acknowledge the emotional reality before legal details
2. Explain your process for minimizing conflict where possible
3. Children's interests: how the firm protects them
4. What clients can realistically expect (no false promises)
5. Emergency availability for urgent custody situations

**Specific page types needed**:
- Divorce process page
- Child custody page (most searched)
- Child support page
- Spousal support/alimony page
- Adoption page (separate emotional tone — positive)
- Domestic violence/protective orders page

**Avoid**: Aggressive language about "winning" divorce; this alienates clients seeking resolution

## Type 3: Criminal Defense

**Client emotional state**: Terrified, ashamed, desperate, uncertain about the future, often calling for a family member
**Primary fear**: Jail time, public record, job loss, family impact, deportation (if non-citizen)

**Design Direction**
- Color: Deep navy/charcoal + strong accent — authority, strength, protection
- Typography: Strong geometric sans or authoritative serif — projects power
- Photography: Confident attorney, direct eye contact — project competence and resolve
- Tone: Direct and protective — "I will defend your rights"

**Content Priorities**
1. Urgency acknowledgment: "You need to act now"
2. 24/7 availability statement (criminal charges don't respect business hours)
3. What the attorney will do immediately to protect the client
4. Clear explanation of the process (reduces terror of the unknown)
5. Attorney's track record in this specific type of defense

**Critical conversion element**: Phone number must be visible immediately. Many clients call in acute crisis — mobile-first click-to-call is mandatory.

**Specific charge pages needed** (each a unique landing page):
- DUI/DWI
- Drug charges
- Assault/battery
- Domestic violence charges
- Theft/fraud
- Federal charges
- Juvenile offenses

**Trust signals**: Former prosecutor experience (strongly converts), board certifications in criminal law, specific case dismissals/reductions (where ethics permits)

## Type 4: Estate Planning & Probate

**Client emotional state**: Responsible, future-focused, often motivated by a life event (new baby, illness, death of parent)
**Primary fear**: Family conflict after death, minor children unprotected, estate going to wrong people, probate nightmare

**Design Direction**
- Color: Deep blue/navy + warm gold or cream — legacy, trust, permanence
- Typography: Classic serif headings — heritage and reliability
- Photography: Families, mature professionals in warm settings; never stark legal environments
- Tone: Reassuring and planning-focused — "We help protect what you've built"

**Content Priorities**
1. Life events that trigger estate planning need (new child, marriage, illness, retirement)
2. Plain-English explanation of wills, trusts, powers of attorney
3. What happens if you don't have documents (the nightmare scenario)
4. Process: what does a consultation look like, what documents result
5. Pricing transparency (flat fee estate planning converts well)

**Service pages needed**:
- Wills
- Revocable living trusts
- Powers of attorney
- Healthcare directives
- Business succession planning
- Probate administration

**Pricing note**: Estate planning is well-suited to fixed-fee pricing displayed on the website. Transparency removes the "how much will this cost?" barrier.

## Type 5: Immigration Law

**Client emotional state**: Hopeful, frightened, vulnerable, often navigating a system in a language not their own
**Primary fear**: Deportation, family separation, bureaucratic error destroying their case, long delays

**Design Direction**
- Color: Warm blue + terracotta or amber — welcoming, hopeful, human
- Typography: Highly readable body font; consider offering multiple language options
- Photography: Warm, multicultural imagery; attorney with clients in consultation
- Tone: Welcoming and clear — "We guide you through every step"

**Critical design requirement**: Multi-language support is not optional for most immigration practices. At minimum: English + Spanish; many practices need additional languages (French, Haitian Creole, Mandarin, Portuguese).

**Content Priorities**
1. Immediate statement of languages spoken by the firm
2. Visa categories handled (with plain-English explanations)
3. Step-by-step process for the most common cases
4. Processing time expectations (honest, not optimistic)
5. What documents clients need to gather

**Service pages needed**:
- Family-based immigration (I-130, I-485)
- Employment visas (H-1B, L-1, O-1)
- Green card process
- Citizenship/naturalization
- Asylum
- Deportation defense
- DACA
- Consular processing

**Trust signal specific to immigration**: Multilingual staff listed explicitly; country-specific experience; community organizations the firm works with

## Type 6: Business / Corporate Law

**Client emotional state**: Analytical, outcome-focused, risk-aware, evaluating multiple options, often time-pressured
**Primary concern**: Deal risk, liability exposure, regulatory compliance, partnership disputes

**Design Direction**
- Color: Slate/charcoal + deep teal or dark green — sophisticated, modern, transactional
- Typography: Sharp, modern sans-serif — precision and professionalism
- Photography: Professional environments, boardrooms, attorneys in working settings (not posed)
- Tone: Direct and results-focused — "We protect your business"

**Content Priorities**
1. Industries served (tech startup? real estate? manufacturing? healthcare?)
2. Types of transactions and matters handled
3. Speed and responsiveness (business clients prioritize this)
4. Attorney credentials relevant to their business context
5. Pricing models (retainer? project-based? hourly?)

**Service pages needed**:
- Business formation and structure
- Contract drafting and review
- Mergers and acquisitions
- Commercial real estate
- Intellectual property
- Employment matters (for businesses)
- Business litigation

**Trust signals that work**: Named clients (where permitted), transaction size handled, industry-specific experience, relevant publications or speaking

**Conversion note**: B2B legal clients often have longer evaluation cycles. Case studies with measurable outcomes (deal successfully closed, dispute resolved, contract renegotiated, saving X) are more important than testimonials.

## Type 7: Employment Law (Plaintiff-Side)

**Client emotional state**: Angry, humiliated, financially vulnerable, second-guessing themselves
**Primary fear**: Not being believed, retaliation, losing their livelihood, missing the statute of limitations

**Design Direction**
- Color: Strong blue + amber — confident advocacy, not combative
- Typography: Clear, readable — clients may be unfamiliar with legal processes
- Photography: Attorney in professional but approachable settings; strength without aggression
- Tone: Validating and empowering — "What happened to you was wrong. We can help."

**Content Priorities**
1. Types of cases handled (discrimination, harassment, wrongful termination, wage theft)
2. Statute of limitations warning — urgency without panic
3. What a consultation looks like (many clients fear retribution just for calling)
4. Contingency fee explanation
5. What clients should not do before calling (don't resign, document everything)

**Service pages needed**:
- Workplace discrimination (race, gender, age, disability, religion)
- Sexual harassment
- Wrongful termination
- Wage and hour violations
- Retaliation claims
- FMLA violations

---

## Type 8: Workers' Compensation

**Client emotional state**: Injured, worried about medical bills, unsure of rights, potentially intimidated by employer
**Primary fear**: Losing their job, being denied coverage, medical bills, not being able to support family

**Design Direction**
- Color: Dependable blue + warm neutral — protective and reassuring
- Typography: High readability body font — many clients are blue-collar workers, not reading legal documents
- Photography: Working-class settings (not corporate boardrooms)
- Tone: Protective and clear — "We protect workers. It's all we do."

**Content Priorities**
1. What to do immediately after a work injury (step-by-step)
2. Rights the worker has that the employer may not have told them
3. No cost to file; contingency fee
4. What happens if they're fired after filing
5. Common employer/insurer tactics and how the attorney counters them

## Shared Design Patterns Across All Types

### Homepage Pattern (Applies to All)

```
HERO: [What problem you solve] + [Geographic area] + [Primary CTA: Free Consultation]
     [Trust bar: years | clients helped | rating | association]

PRACTICE AREAS: Clear cards linking to individual pages (client language, not legal terms)

ATTORNEY INTRO: Brief + approachable (full bio on separate page)

SOCIAL PROOF: Testimonials or case results (ethics-compliant)

CONTACT: Phone (tap-to-call) + Form + Response time commitment
```

### What Differentiates Each Type's Homepage

| Type | First Sentence of Hero | Primary Trust Signal |
|------|------------------------|----------------------|
| Personal Injury | "Hurt in an accident? We fight for the compensation you deserve." | Case results |
| Family Law | "Protecting your family through difficult times." | Testimonials about process |
| Criminal Defense | "Arrested? Call now. 24/7 emergency response." | Former prosecutor experience |
| Estate Planning | "Plan for your family's future — it's simpler than you think." | Flat fee pricing |
| Immigration | "We guide you to the life you came here to build." | Languages spoken |
| Business Law | "Your legal partner for growth, transactions, and protection." | Industry experience |
| Employment | "What happened to you was wrong. Let's hold them accountable." | Cases won |
| Workers' Comp | "You were injured at work. You have rights. We protect them." | No fee unless we win |
