# Nonprofit Sub-Sector Patterns

Different nonprofit sub-sectors have distinct audiences, emotional registers, and visual languages. Generic "nonprofit design" ignores these differences.

## Environment & Agriculture

**Audience:** Sustainability-conscious donors, farming communities, research partners, government.

**Visual world:** Fields, soil, harvests, processing, community gatherings, natural textures.

**Palette direction:**
- Primary: Forest green (#1a472a–#2d6b3f range)
- Accent: Gold/amber (#d4a843) — harvest, excellence
- Supporting: Earth tones, cream backgrounds
- Avoid: Neon greens (artificial), pure white (clinical)

**Typography:** Grounded and warm. Rounded/humanist sans body. Slab serif or strong geometric headlines for authority without coldness.

**Photography:** Lush landscapes, natural light. Workers in the field (dignity, pride). Processing/innovation. Consistent warm grading — golden hour feel.

**Signature elements:** Leaf/growth motifs in subtle patterns. Organic border-radius. Texture overlays (paper grain, natural fiber). Operational geography maps.

## Healthcare & Medical

**Audience:** Patients, donors, medical professionals, partner hospitals.

**Palette direction:**
- Primary: Blue (#1565C0–#1976D2) or teal
- Accent: Warm coral or green (hope, vitality)
- Supporting: Clean whites, light grays, soft blue tints
- Avoid: Red as primary (emergency signal), dark backgrounds (oppressive)

**Typography:** Clean, highly readable. Humanist sans with generous x-height. Medical terms need extra legibility.

**Photography:** Caring interactions (not suffering). Recovery and wellness. Clean, well-lit environments. Community health sessions.

**Signature elements:** Clean card layouts (clinical precision). Progress bars for outcomes. Accessibility features prominent.

## Education & Youth

**Audience:** Parents, students, donors, educators.

**Palette direction:**
- Primary: Blue or green (trust, growth)
- Accent: Bright, energetic (yellow, orange, coral)
- Youth-focused: brighter, more saturated
- Adult education: more muted, professional

**Typography:** Friendly and readable. Rounded sans for youth. Geometric for adult ed. Extra-generous sizing.

**Photography:** Children engaged and learning (never posed). Diverse representation. Achievement moments. Inviting facilities.

**Signature elements:** Larger border-radius (friendly). Illustration integration (custom, not clip art). Interactive elements. Student testimonial grids or manually controlled proof galleries.

## Humanitarian & Disaster Response

**Audience:** Emergency donors, long-term supporters, partner agencies, media.

**Palette direction:**
- Primary: Deep red or orange (urgency, action)
- Grounding: Navy or dark blue (stability)
- Accent: White or light (hope, clarity)
- Avoid: Too many warm colors (chaotic), pastels (undermines urgency)

**Typography:** Bold, no-nonsense. Strong sans-serif with heavy weights. Maximum readability for crisis comms.

**Photography:** Resilience and rebuilding (not devastation). Aid workers in action. Communities receiving support with dignity.

**Signature elements:** Urgent CTA styling (bold, high-contrast, large). Real-time campaign counters. Crisis maps. News feed prominent.

## Arts, Culture & Community

**Audience:** Art lovers, community members, sponsors, grant-makers.

**Palette direction:**
- Bold, vibrant, unique — this sector rewards creative risk
- Can break the "one primary" rule with multi-color palettes
- Avoid: Corporate blues, muted palettes

**Typography:** Most creative freedom. Distinctive display fonts allowed. Artistic headline typefaces. Body still readable.

**Photography:** Performances, exhibitions (energy, emotion). Community participation (diverse, joyful). Behind-the-scenes. Event atmosphere.

**Signature elements:** Dramatic visual treatments. Full-bleed photography with creative overlays. Event calendar as primary nav element. Gallery/portfolio layouts.

## Research & Science

**Audience:** Academic community, funders, policy makers, media.

**Palette direction:**
- Primary: Deep blue or green (authority, knowledge)
- Accent: Gold (#c5a55a–#d4a843) — academic excellence
- Supporting: Cool grays, clean whites
- Avoid: Overly warm palettes (undermines scholarly credibility)

**Typography:** Authoritative. Serif display for tradition and rigor. Clean sans body for modern communication.

**Photography:** Lab and research environments. Scientists at work. Data visualizations. Field research. Conference moments.

**Signature elements:** Publication/journal listings. Data viz integration. Research timelines. Citation-style credibility. Download CTAs (papers, reports, datasets).

## Cross-Sector Rules

Apply regardless of sub-sector:

1. **Authentic photography always.** Stock is instantly recognizable and trust-destroying.
2. **One CTA per viewport.** No competing primary actions visible simultaneously.
3. **Impact before ask.** Show what you've done before requesting support.
4. **Mobile-first always.** 52% of nonprofit traffic is mobile, higher in developing regions.
5. **Speed matters.** Rural/developing audiences on slow connections. Optimize aggressively.
6. **Consistency compounds.** Same visual language: website, email, social, print.
7. **Update regularly.** Stale content signals organizational weakness.

## Branding Personality Traits

| Trait | Design expression |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Bold** | High contrast, strong type weights, dramatic imagery, confident white space |
| **Warm** | Earth tones, rounded shapes, authentic photos, conversational copy |
| **Urgent** | Red/orange accents, bold CTAs, progress bars, deadline language |
| **Authoritative** | Deep colors, serif type, structured layouts, data-driven content |
| **Playful** | Bright colors, rounded UI, illustrations, micro-interactions |
| **Balanced** | Mix of serious and optimistic, professional but approachable |

Choose 2-3 traits. Every design decision reinforces them. If a component doesn't express at least one trait, it's defaulting to generic.

## Logo Usage on Websites

- Header: max 48px height, crisp at all resolutions
- Footer: same or slightly smaller, with full org name
- Favicon: simplified mark only, recognizable at 32x32px
- Loading/splash: centered, full lockup
