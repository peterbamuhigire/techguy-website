# Client Acquisition & Trust Design

Professional services sell on trust and relationships. 82% of clients research providers online before making contact. The website must answer: "Are they expert?", "Can I trust them?", "Will they understand MY problem?"

## The Trust-First Test

A prospective client evaluates in 5 seconds:

- **"Do they offer what I need?"**: Service list visible immediately
- **"Are they qualified?"**: Credentials, certifications, years of experience
- **"Have they done this before?"**: Case studies, client logos, testimonials
- **"How do I start?"**: Clear contact CTA or consultation booking

## The Client Funnel

```
[Awareness]       →  [Research]        →  [Evaluation]      →  [Engagement]
Homepage/search      Service pages        Case studies         Free consultation
Referral             Team profiles        Testimonials         Contact form
Content/SEO          Industry expertise   Credentials          Phone call
```

### Page Flow
1. **Homepage**: Hero + value prop → Service grid → Trust bar (logos) → Case study highlights → Team → CTA
2. **Services**: Service description → Methodology → Outcomes/benefits → Related case studies → Enquiry
3. **Case Studies**: Client challenge → Approach → Results (quantified) → Testimonial → Next steps
4. **Team**: Professional photos → Credentials → Specialisations → Bio → Contact per member
5. **Contact**: Consultation booking → Enquiry form → Office locations → Phone/email → Map

Each service page should follow:

```

```

## Trust Architecture

### Tier 1: Must-Have
- **Professional credentials**: Certifications, licences, professional body memberships
- **Years in practice**: "Serving clients since 2005"
- **Client logos**: 4-6 recognisable names
- **Contact info**: Phone + email on every page

### Tier 2: High-Impact
- **Case study metrics**: Quantified outcomes ("Saved client $400K annually")
- **Team credentials**: Degrees, certifications, years of experience
- **Named testimonials**: Client name, title, company, photo
- **Industry expertise**: Sector specialisations clearly listed
- **Awards/recognition**: Industry awards, rankings, press mentions

### Tier 3: Depth
- **Thought leadership**: Blog articles, industry guides, whitepapers
- **Methodology**: Proprietary process or approach visualised
- **Community**: Pro bono work, industry involvement, teaching
- **Insurance/compliance**: Professional indemnity, regulatory compliance

## Testimonial Strategy

Professional services testimonials must be specific and named:

- **Client name + title + company** (never anonymous)
- **Specific outcome**: "Reduced our tax liability by 23% in the first year"
- **Working relationship**: "Responsive, thorough, and genuinely invested in our success"
- **3-5 testimonials on homepage**, more on dedicated page
- **Video testimonials**: 60-90 seconds, hosted on YouTube

## Common Mistakes
1. **Jargon-heavy service descriptions** — clients want outcomes, not methodologies
2. **No client logos or testimonials** — the fastest trust signal missing
3. **Team photos that look like mugshots** — professional, warm, approachable
4. **Buried contact information** — booking should be 1 click away
5. **No case studies** — "We helped clients" vs "We saved ABC Corp $400K"
6. **Generic "About Us"** — personal stories and team personality matter
7. **No process explanation** — clients want to know what working with you looks like
