# Travel Type Design Patterns

No two travel websites should look the same — even within the same type. Each travel type has distinct trust signals, audience expectations, and emotional design triggers. Choose your type, then customise heavily via your sector-brief.

## Luxury Safari & Lodge

**Audience:** Affluent travellers (35-65), honeymooners, high-net-worth families
**Trust signals:** Exclusivity, guide expertise, lodge quality, awards, celebrity/press mentions

### Design Direction

- **Palette:** Premium, muted. Deep navy + gold, charcoal + copper, or forest + cream. Never bright/saturated.
- **Typography:** Elegant serif headlines (Cormorant, DM Serif Display) + clean sans body. Generous letter-spacing.
- **Photography:** Professional, atmospheric. Golden hour landscapes, intimate lodge interiors, close-up wildlife.
- **Tone:** Refined, exclusive, understated. "Where extraordinary becomes your everyday."

### Key Design Elements

| Element | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Full-bleed hero imagery** | Dark overlay, minimal text, single CTA |
| **Lodge showcase** | Large photos, room types, amenity icons |
| **Guide profiles** | Names, years of experience, specialities |
| **Press/media bar** | "As seen in..." logos (Condé Nast, Travel + Leisure) |
| **Bespoke CTA** | "Design Your Journey" instead of generic "Book Now" |
| **Price positioning** | "From $X per person" — never discounted/sale language |

### Unique Components
- Itinerary visualiser with day-by-day luxury breakdown
- Lodge room comparison (side-by-side)
- Private guide booking interface
- Season calendar (best time to visit)
- Concierge contact form (not generic enquiry)

## Budget & Backpacker

**Audience:** Young travellers (18-35), gap year, digital nomads, value-seekers
**Trust signals:** Value for money, real traveller reviews, safety, social community

### Design Direction

- **Palette:** Bold, energetic. Bright teal + orange, electric blue + yellow, or lime + charcoal. High saturation.
- **Typography:** Bold geometric sans (Rubik, Outfit, Space Grotesk). No serifs — too formal.
- **Photography:** Authentic, user-generated feel. Backpackers at hostels, street food, group selfies.
- **Tone:** Direct, fun, honest. "Epic adventures. Honest prices. No hidden fees."

### Key Design Elements

| Element | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Price-first cards** | Price prominent, "from $X/day" with clear inclusions |
| **Comparison tables** | Budget vs mid-range vs premium clearly laid out |
| **Traveller reviews** | Real photos, informal language, social media style |
| **Group discount banner** | "Book with 4+ friends — save 15%" |
| **Hostel/budget stay cards** | Ratings, shared vs private, location proximity |
| **Safety section** | Travel insurance, safety tips, emergency contacts |

### Unique Components
- Budget calculator ("Estimate your trip cost")
- Group booking tool (split payments)
- Traveller community feed (Instagram-style)
- Packing list generator
- Free cancellation badge (prominent)

## Cultural Immersion

**Audience:** Curious travellers (30-60), culture enthusiasts, food lovers, photographers
**Trust signals:** Local expertise, authentic experiences, cultural sensitivity, guide knowledge

### Design Direction

- **Palette:** Warm, earthy. Terracotta + warm gold, ochre + deep brown, or warm violet + cream. Heritage feel.
- **Typography:** Distinctive serif (Fraunces, Literata, Lora) + warm sans (Plus Jakarta Sans, Nunito). Character-rich.
- **Photography:** Cultural moments — local crafts, cooking, ceremonies, markets, architecture. Warm tones.
- **Tone:** Warm, curious, respectful. "Meet the people behind the places."

### Key Design Elements

| Element | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Story-driven hero** | Local person/scene, not generic landscape |
| **Experience cards** | Cultural activity focus (cooking class, pottery, dance) |
| **Local guide profiles** | Extensive bios, cultural expertise, languages spoken |
| **Cultural calendar** | Festivals, ceremonies, seasonal events |
| **Photo journal format** | Blog/diary style content layout |
| **Community impact** | How tourism supports local communities |

### Unique Components
- Cultural etiquette guide per destination
- Local cuisine gallery with story
- Artisan workshop showcase
- Language phrase card (basic local phrases)
- "Ask a Local" Q&A section

## Eco-Tourism & Sustainable Travel

**Audience:** Environmentally conscious (25-55), nature lovers, conservation supporters
**Trust signals:** Eco-certifications, carbon offset, community benefit, conservation impact

### Design Direction

- **Palette:** Natural, organic. Forest green + sky blue, sage + sand, or olive + warm cream. Never synthetic.
- **Typography:** Organic rounded sans (Quicksand, Nunito) or natural serif (Lora). Approachable, not corporate.
- **Photography:** Pristine nature, wildlife, community projects, sustainable facilities. Minimal human presence.
- **Tone:** Purposeful, hopeful, informed. "Travel that gives back more than it takes."

### Key Design Elements

| Element | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Impact statistics** | "X trees planted", "X% carbon offset", "X families supported" |
| **Eco-certification badges** | Green Globe, Rainforest Alliance, B Corp prominently displayed |
| **Carbon calculator** | Show trip carbon footprint + offset option |
| **Conservation stories** | How tours fund conservation projects |
| **Sustainable practices** | What the company does (solar, local hiring, waste reduction) |
| **Wildlife guidelines** | Responsible viewing practices |

### Unique Components
- Trip carbon footprint calculator
- Conservation project showcase
- "Choose to Offset" donation integration
- Sustainability report (annual, downloadable)
- Wildlife species guide per destination
- Community impact map

## Adventure & Expedition

**Audience:** Thrill-seekers (20-45), fitness enthusiasts, experienced travellers
**Trust signals:** Safety record, guide certifications, equipment quality, difficulty ratings

### Design Direction

- **Palette:** Bold, high-contrast. Deep red + electric blue, charcoal + neon accent, or slate + safety orange.
- **Typography:** Bold geometric sans (Clash Display, Sora, Outfit). Strong weight. Uppercase accents.
- **Photography:** Action shots — climbing, rafting, hiking, diving. Dynamic angles, motion blur.
- **Tone:** Bold, empowering, direct. "Climb higher. See further. Live wilder."

### Key Design Elements

| Element | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Difficulty ratings** | Clear 1-5 scale with fitness requirements |
| **Equipment list** | What's provided vs what to bring |
| **Safety credentials** | Guide certifications, safety equipment, protocols |
| **Activity categories** | Climbing, rafting, diving, trekking — filterable |
| **Fitness requirements** | Honest physical requirements per tour |
| **Weather/season info** | Best conditions for each activity |

### Unique Components
- Fitness requirement quiz ("Am I ready for this?")
- Gear checklist generator
- Activity difficulty comparison chart
- Emergency protocol page
- Training tips/preparation guide
- Achievement badges for completed tours

## Wellness & Retreat

**Audience:** Stressed professionals (30-60), couples, health-conscious travellers
**Trust signals:** Practitioner credentials, retreat reviews, programme details, facility quality

### Design Direction

- **Palette:** Calm, muted. Sage green + blush pink, soft blue + ivory, or lavender + cream. Low saturation.
- **Typography:** Light elegant serif (Cormorant Garamond, EB Garamond) + thin sans (Jost, Outfit light). Generous white space.
- **Photography:** Serene — yoga poses, spa interiors, natural settings, healthy food. Soft focus, natural light.
- **Tone:** Calm, nurturing, transformative. "Find your stillness."

### Key Design Elements

| Element | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Programme timeline** | Day-by-day schedule with activities |
| **Practitioner profiles** | Detailed credentials, philosophy, specialities |
| **Retreat comparison** | Side-by-side programmes (detox, yoga, meditation) |
| **Testimonial focus** | Transformation stories, before/after narratives |
| **Facility gallery** | Spa, yoga studio, accommodation, gardens |
| **Generous white space** | Breathing room reflects the retreat experience |

### Unique Components
- Programme finder quiz ("What retreat is right for you?")
- Dietary accommodation form
- Pre-retreat preparation guide
- Practitioner booking calendar
- Mindfulness content/blog section
- Package comparison table (3-5-7 day options)

## Cruise & Ocean Travel

**Audience:** Couples (40-70), families, group travellers, retirees
**Trust signals:** Ship quality, itinerary detail, port reviews, safety record

### Design Direction

- **Palette:** Maritime. Deep blue + coral, navy + white + gold, or teal + warm accent. Classic nautical.
- **Typography:** Clean sans for navigation (DM Sans) + serif for elegance (Lora). Balanced.
- **Photography:** Ocean panoramas, ship interiors, port destinations, onboard activities.
- **Tone:** Exciting yet reassuring. "Your floating resort awaits."

### Key Design Elements

| Element | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Ship showcase** | Deck plans, cabin types, amenity tours |
| **Route maps** | Interactive itinerary maps with port stops |
| **Port guides** | What to do at each destination |
| **Cabin comparison** | Inside, ocean view, balcony, suite — visual comparison |
| **Onboard activities** | Entertainment, dining, spa, kids' club |
| **All-inclusive clarity** | What's included vs extra cost |

## City Breaks & Urban Tourism

**Audience:** Weekend travellers (25-50), couples, culture seekers, foodies
**Trust signals:** Local knowledge, neighbourhood guides, real recommendations, quick booking

### Design Direction

- **Palette:** Urban sophisticated. Charcoal + vibrant accent, dark slate + coral, or off-black + electric colour.
- **Typography:** Modern sans (Space Grotesk, Satoshi, General Sans). Sharp, contemporary.
- **Photography:** Street scenes, architecture, food, nightlife, street art. High contrast, urban feel.
- **Tone:** Insider knowledge, contemporary. "The city, like a local."

### Key Design Elements

| Element | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Neighbourhood guides** | Area-by-area breakdown with character descriptions |
| **Restaurant/bar picks** | Curated food guide, not generic listings |
| **Walking routes** | Self-guided tour maps with stops |
| **Event calendar** | Concerts, exhibitions, festivals — filterable |
| **Quick-book experiences** | 2-4 hour activities, instant confirmation |
| **Local tips section** | Transport, tipping, safety, best times |

## Family Travel

**Audience:** Parents (30-50), multigenerational groups, school holiday travellers
**Trust signals:** Child safety, family-friendly ratings, age-appropriate activities, flexible cancellation

### Design Direction

- **Palette:** Warm, friendly. Warm blue + sunny yellow, soft green + coral, or sky blue + orange. Approachable.
- **Typography:** Rounded friendly sans (Nunito, Quicksand for accent) + clean sans body (DM Sans, Inter).
- **Photography:** Families together — kids exploring, parents relaxed, multigenerational moments. Joyful, natural.
- **Tone:** Reassuring, fun, practical. "Adventures the whole family will remember."

### Key Design Elements

| Element | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Age-appropriate badges** | "Suitable for ages 4+", "Teen-friendly", "All ages" |
| **Family pricing** | Per-family or per-person with child discounts visible |
| **Safety features** | Child supervision, first aid, medical access |
| **Activity descriptions** | What kids will do vs what parents will do |
| **Packing list** | Family-specific gear and essentials |
| **Cancellation flexibility** | "Kids get sick — we understand. Free cancellation." |

### Unique Components
- Family trip planner (ages, interests, dates → recommendations)
- Kid activity preview (photos of children doing activities)
- Parent testimonials specifically about family experience
- "What to pack for kids" checklist
- Dietary/allergy accommodation form
- Nap/rest time considerations in itinerary

## Branding Personality Framework

Every travel brand has a unique personality within its type. Rate these 6 traits to define YOUR brand's visual identity:

| Trait | Spectrum | Design Impact |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| **Formality** | Casual ←→ Formal | Typography weight, colour saturation, imagery style |
| **Price Position** | Budget ←→ Luxury | White space amount, gold accents, serif presence |
| **Adventure Level** | Relaxed ←→ Extreme | Colour boldness, imagery dynamism, CTA urgency |
| **Authenticity** | Polished ←→ Raw | Photography style, voice tone, layout precision |
| **Scale** | Intimate ←→ Grand | Group size messaging, exclusivity signals |
| **Sustainability** | Standard ←→ Eco-first | Green palette weight, impact metrics prominence |

**Example mapping:**
- Luxury safari: Formal + Luxury + Relaxed + Polished + Intimate + Eco-first
- Budget backpacker: Casual + Budget + Moderate + Raw + Grand + Standard
- These two travel brands should look COMPLETELY DIFFERENT even though they're both "tour & travel"

## Multi-Language Considerations

All travel brands in East Africa should consider multi-language support:

- **URL structure**: `/en/tours/`, `/fr/circuits/`, `/sw/safari/`
- **Content expansion**: French ~30% longer, Kiswahili ~20% longer than English
- **Currency display**: Show both local and international currencies
- **Cultural adaptation**: Adjust imagery and messaging per language market
- **Booking terms**: Translate completely, including cancellation policies and T&Cs
