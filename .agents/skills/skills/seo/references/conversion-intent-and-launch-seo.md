# Conversion Intent And Launch SEO

Use this reference when SEO work must support not just ranking, but business outcomes from clusters, campaigns, and launch windows.

Primary upstream skills:

- `sales-copywriting`
- `launch-campaigns`
- `blog-idea-generator`
- `blog-writer`

## Core rule

SEO metadata must match intent and move the reader to the next right step.
Do not optimise titles and descriptions as isolated keyword assets.

## Metadata by intent

### Informational pages

Best for:

- guides
- FAQs
- blog posts
- glossary entries

Metadata posture:

- promise clarity
- reflect the search question
- use a softer CTA in the description

Examples of CTA posture:

- `Read the guide`
- `See the checklist`
- `Understand the costs`

### Commercial investigation pages

Best for:

- service pages
- comparison pages
- sector pages
- case-study led landing pages

Metadata posture:

- state the audience or problem
- signal proof or differentiation
- use a stronger CTA in the description

Examples of CTA posture:

- `See plans and pricing`
- `Compare your options`
- `Book a diagnostic`

### Transactional or launch pages

Best for:

- campaign landing pages
- waitlists
- booking pages
- applications
- event registration

Metadata posture:

- make the action explicit
- include timing or availability only when real
- keep the value proposition clear

Examples:

- `Join the waitlist`
- `Apply before 31 May`
- `Register for the session`

## Cluster logic

For each topic cluster, define:

- pillar page
- supporting articles
- comparison or objection page
- conversion page

SEO work should reinforce that structure through:

- internal links
- canonical decisions
- schema selection
- consistent title and description posture

## Launch SEO rules

When a campaign has a timed window:

- update titles and descriptions to match the current campaign state
- avoid stale urgency after the window closes
- switch closed offers to waitlist, replay, or next-step variants
- update structured data where dates or availability matter

## Conversion-aware metadata checklist

- Title reflects page intent, not just the target phrase.
- Meta description names the reward or next step.
- CTA posture matches buyer stage.
- Commercial pages signal proof, trust, or fit.
- Informational pages connect to the next cluster or conversion step.
- Launch pages are reviewed when the campaign opens and closes.

## Anti-patterns

- same meta description style on every page
- informational page metadata that sounds like a hard sell
- expired launch metadata left live for months
- keyword-heavy titles with no audience or outcome signal
- cluster pages with no next-step path
