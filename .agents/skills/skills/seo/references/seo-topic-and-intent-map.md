# SEO Topic And Intent Map

Use this reference when turning strategy into SEO planning.
The goal is to map search intent to the right page purpose and proof burden.

## Core Rule

SEO for premium websites is not a keyword dump.
It is a structured alignment between:

- audience problem
- search intent
- page role
- trust proof
- CTA timing

## Intent Types

- Informational: user wants to understand a problem, option, or concept
- Commercial investigation: user is comparing approaches or providers
- Transactional: user is ready to contact, book, request, or buy
- Navigational: user is trying to reach a known company or resource

## Mapping Rules

### Informational Intent

Best for:

- educational pages
- FAQs
- trust-building content
- transparent "They Ask" articles

CTA posture:

- lighter CTA
- transitional CTA
- assignment-selling content

### Commercial Investigation

Best for:

- service pages
- comparison pages
- sector pages
- case-study-supported landing pages

CTA posture:

- stronger CTA
- trust proof close to CTA

### Transactional Intent

Best for:

- contact pages
- service-intent landing pages
- booking pages

CTA posture:

- direct CTA
- minimal friction
- strong credibility reinforcement

## Proof Alignment

Do not rank pages on intent alone.
The page must also prove what that searcher needs to believe.

Examples:

- "best law firm website designer" needs sector credibility and proof
- "website redesign cost" needs pricing honesty and scope explanation
- "web design agency near me" needs legitimacy and locality cues

## Anti-Patterns

- mapping multiple unrelated intents to one page
- using blog content where a service page should exist
- writing keywords with no decision-stage awareness
- hiding critical trust signals below the fold on commercial pages
