# Agency Delivery Blueprint

A reusable blueprint template across the five engagement phases. Lanes run left to right; phases run top to bottom.

## Lanes

1. Customer actions — what the buyer or website owner does.
2. Frontstage evidence — what they see, read, sign, receive.
3. Frontstage staff — agency and client people they meet.
4. Backstage staff — people who never appear but whose work makes the promise true.
5. Support processes — repeatable procedures and SLAs.
6. Systems — tools, automations, integrations.

## Phase 1: Discovery

- Customer actions: book intake call; share business context; sign discovery agreement; pay discovery fee.
- Frontstage evidence: discovery proposal, signed agreement, recorded interview clip, written discovery report.
- Frontstage staff: discovery lead, sales lead.
- Backstage staff: research analyst, transcription, junior strategist.
- Support processes: 48h proposal turnaround; weekly client check-in; written decision log.
- Systems: CRM, scheduling, recording and transcription, document store, signed-agreement archive.

## Phase 2: Strategy and content architecture

- Customer actions: review experience map, approve sitemap, supply or commission content.
- Frontstage evidence: experience map, sitemap, page-goal table, content brief, copy drafts.
- Frontstage staff: strategist, content lead.
- Backstage staff: SEO analyst, accessibility reviewer, brand reviewer.
- Support processes: stage gate before design; written sign-off per artefact; content review SLA.
- Systems: content workspace, SEO tool, brand asset store.

## Phase 3: Design and build

- Customer actions: review design system, review prototype, supply assets, approve component library.
- Frontstage evidence: design system, key page mocks, working prototype, accessibility statement.
- Frontstage staff: design lead, engineering lead.
- Backstage staff: developers, QA engineer, accessibility specialist, performance engineer.
- Support processes: design decision presentations with rationale; weekly demo; bug triage rota.
- Systems: design tool, version control, CI pipeline, accessibility scanner, visual QA.

## Phase 4: Launch

- Customer actions: approve launch checklist, train on CMS, sign go-live.
- Frontstage evidence: launch report, performance baseline, accessibility report, security headers report, training video.
- Frontstage staff: launch lead.
- Backstage staff: ops, monitoring, security reviewer.
- Support processes: pre-launch checklist; rollback plan; post-launch smoke tests; 7-day hypercare window.
- Systems: deploy pipeline, monitoring, error tracking, rollback automation.

## Phase 5: Retainer and improvement

- Customer actions: monthly review, surface change requests, approve experiments.
- Frontstage evidence: monthly report, experiment register, content calendar, training updates.
- Frontstage staff: account lead, strategist.
- Backstage staff: developers, content writers, analyst.
- Support processes: SLA for each request type (content edit, bug fix, feature, experiment); change-request gate; quarterly business review.
- Systems: ticketing, analytics, experimentation platform, content workspace.

## Using the template

- Copy the template for each engagement.
- Replace generic roles with named people. Names trigger accountability; roles do not.
- For every "frontstage evidence" cell, link to the actual artefact.
- Every cell that depends on the client must show client-side owner and SLA.
- Cells without owners are blockers, not options.
