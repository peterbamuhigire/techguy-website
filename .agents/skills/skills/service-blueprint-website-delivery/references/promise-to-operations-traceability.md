# Promise to Operations Traceability

Every claim on the website triggers an operational commitment. Trace it before publishing.

## Claim audit

For each page that will go live, list every implicit and explicit claim:

- Response and turnaround promises ("reply within 24 hours", "quote in 48 hours").
- Quality promises ("dedicated account lead", "African-realistic performance").
- Coverage promises ("nationwide", "available in English and French").
- Outcome promises ("most clients see X within Y").
- Service promises ("90-day support window included").

## Trace each claim

| Claim | Page | Lane that owns it | Owner | SLA | Evidence the agency can show | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Status values:

- Operational: the agency can deliver this today.
- Conditional: the agency can deliver this if condition X is true (e.g. retainer signed).
- Aspirational: the agency cannot deliver this today. Either build the capability before launch, or rewrite the claim.

## Rewrite rules for unkept claims

- Replace category claims ("we are the best") with specific evidence ("Lighthouse 95+ on the last 12 launches, audited monthly").
- Replace time-bound promises with conditional language only when the condition is itself credible ("during business hours, weekdays").
- Replace coverage claims with current scope and a roadmap, not a guess ("English now; French planned Q3").
- Remove claims that are only partially true. Premium credibility breaks faster from one false claim than ten missing claims.

## Internal commitments triggered

For every operational claim, generate the internal artefact that makes it real:

- Rota, on-call schedule, or coverage matrix.
- Standard operating procedure for the activity.
- Customer-facing recovery script for when the SLA breaks.
- Monitoring and alerting that detects the breach before the customer reports it.

Hand these to `agency-client-retention` and `customer-service-website-ops` for execution.
