## Weekly Rhythm Template

A repeatable weekly content rhythm. Adjust weighting based on client's character and business type.

### Week Structure (1 active platform example: Instagram)

| Day | Bucket | Format | Content Direction |
|-----|--------|--------|------------------|
| Monday | Finished Work | Single image or carousel | Showcase a completed project, client result, or delivered service |
| Tuesday | Process | Reel or Story | Behind-the-scenes of current work; how something is made or done |
| Wednesday | Inspiration | Carousel or text + image | Share an idea, resource, or article that relates to the brand's field |
| Thursday | Community | Single image or text post | Feature a local event, industry news, or another brand or person worth highlighting |
| Friday | Personal Experience | Story or single image | A personal anecdote, team moment, or honest reflection that humanises the brand |
| Saturday | Finished Work or Inspiration | Reel | High-reach format; publish best-performing content type of the week |
| Sunday | (optional) | Story only | Casual behind-the-scenes or "what's coming next week" teaser |

**For two active platforms:** Run the same weekly structure on Platform 1; run a condensed 3-day version (Monday / Wednesday / Friday) on Platform 2.

**For three active platforms:** Full weekly rhythm on primary platform; 3-4 posts per week on secondary; 1-2 posts per week (Finished Work and Inspiration only) on tertiary.

## Content Repurposing Matrix

One original piece of content repurposed across 8 formats.

| Source Asset | Target Format | Target Platform | What Changes |
|-------------|---------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Blog post (1,200 words) | Summary carousel (5-7 slides) | Instagram, LinkedIn | Extract key points; design as slides; add CTA to read full post |
| Blog post | Twitter thread | Twitter/X | One insight per tweet; 5-8 tweets; link to full post in final tweet |
| Blog post | Short video (60-90s) | TikTok, Instagram Reels | Record talking-head version of the key argument; add text overlay |
| Blog post | Infographic | Pinterest, Instagram | Visualise the data, steps, or framework from the post |
| Blog post | LinkedIn article | LinkedIn | Reframe for professional audience; expand on the business application |
| Blog post | Email newsletter section | Email list | 150-word excerpt with a "read more" link back to the post |
| Blog post | Quote graphic | Instagram Stories, Facebook | Pull the single best sentence; design as a branded image |
| Blog post | Pinterest pin | Pinterest | Design a vertical (2:3) featured image; write keyword-rich description |

**Repurposing workflow:**
1. Publish the original blog post on the website (Ring 1 — Home Base)
2. Same day: Schedule the Twitter thread and LinkedIn post
3. Day 2: Design and schedule the Instagram carousel
4. Day 3: Record and post the short video for TikTok and Reels
5. Day 4: Create and schedule the infographic for Pinterest
6. Day 5: Send the email excerpt to the subscriber list
7. Ongoing: The quote graphic and quote stories can be recycled monthly

## Caption Formula Templates

### Formula 1: Problem-Agitate-Solve (for educational or service content)

```
[Relatable problem in one sentence.]

Most [target audience] deal with this because [reason].

Here is what actually works: [Solution in 2-3 sentences.]

[CTA: question, save prompt, or link in bio direction]

[Hashtags]
```

**Example (web agency, Instagram):**
```
Most small business websites get no traffic after launch.

This happens because the site was built without a content strategy — there is nothing to rank for and nothing to share.

Fix: build your blog plan before launch, not after. We cover this in our free guide.

What is the biggest frustration with your current website? Drop it below.

#websitedesign #smallbusiness #digitalmarketing
```

### Formula 3: Insight Hook (for LinkedIn and Twitter thought leadership)

```
[Counterintuitive or unexpected opening statement.]

[1-2 sentences that earn the read: why this matters.]

[3-5 bullet points or numbered steps that deliver the promised value.]

[Closing sentence that reframes or reinforces the opening.]

[CTA: share if useful, reply with question, or link to full article]
```

**Example (LinkedIn post):**
```
The worst thing a new business can do on social media is try to be on every platform.

I have watched this mistake cost agencies and their clients months of wasted effort.

What works instead:
- Choose 2-3 platforms based on where your audience actually spends time
- Go deep on those platforms before expanding
- Measure engagement rate, not follower count
- A 500-follower account with 5% engagement outperforms a 10,000-follower account with 0.2%

Dominate a small space before expanding. That is the only path that compounds.

Which platforms have worked best for your clients? Reply below.
```

## Hashtag Strategy Guide

### Research Method

1. Start with the client's core service or product (e.g., "interior design Kampala")
2. Search Instagram or TikTok for that term; note the top related hashtags shown
3. Record the post count for each hashtag
4. Build a set of hashtags across three tiers:

| Tier | Post Count | Role | Count to Use |
|------|-----------|------|-------------|
| Large | 1M+ posts | Broad discovery; short-lived exposure | 1-2 per post |
| Mid-size | 100K-1M posts | Balance of reach and relevance | 3-5 per post |
| Niche | Under 100K posts | Targeted audience; longer shelf life; more likely to be "top post" | 3-5 per post |

5. Create a branded hashtag for every client (#[clientname], #[clientcampaign]); use in every post
6. Build 3-4 sets of 10-15 hashtags and rotate through them to avoid appearing spammatic

**Set A (General digital/web):**
`#webdesign #websitedesign #digitalmarketing #smallbusiness #onlinepresence #brandingstudio #ugandabusiness #eastafricabusiness #designagency #webdevelopment #brandidentity #marketingstrategy`

`#socialmediamarketing #contentcreator #socialmediatips #socialmediamanager #digitalagency #contentstrategy #instagrammarketing #onlinemarketing #marketingtips #businessgrowth #creativeagency`

## Post Types Checklist by Platform

Use this checklist when building a monthly content calendar to ensure format variety.

### Instagram
- [ ] Single photo post (Feed)
- [ ] Carousel (2-10 slides) — educational, portfolio, step-by-step
- [ ] Reel (15-90 seconds) — priority format for reach
- [ ] Story (15-second segments; use daily for engagement maintenance)
- [ ] Story with poll, question, or quiz sticker (monthly minimum: 4)
- [ ] IGTV or longer video (optional; use for tutorials)
- [ ] Pinned post (top 3 posts pinned to profile)

### LinkedIn
- [ ] Short text post (2-5 paragraphs)
- [ ] Document / PDF carousel (uploaded as document)
- [ ] Native video
- [ ] Long-form article (LinkedIn publishing platform)
- [ ] Poll (2-4 answer options)
- [ ] Company page update
- [ ] Founder / team member personal post (personal profiles outperform company pages)

### Facebook
- [ ] Status update with question
- [ ] Photo post or photo album
- [ ] Native video
- [ ] Facebook Live
- [ ] Shared blog post link (with a custom description, not default preview text)
- [ ] Facebook Group post (if group exists for the client)
- [ ] Event post (for launches, webinars, or live sessions)

### Twitter / X
- [ ] Single tweet (opinion or observation)
- [ ] Thread (5-10 tweets)
- [ ] Quote tweet with commentary
- [ ] Poll (2-4 options)
- [ ] Native video (short clip)
- [ ] Reply to trending topic or industry conversation

### TikTok
- [ ] Original short-form video (15-60 seconds)
- [ ] Trend participation (trending audio with brand-relevant content)
- [ ] Response video (Stitch or Duet)
- [ ] Before-and-after format
- [ ] Educational "did you know" format
- [ ] Behind-the-scenes / day-in-the-life

### Pinterest
- [ ] Static image pin (2:3 vertical ratio)
- [ ] Infographic pin
- [ ] Text overlay image (quote or tip)
- [ ] Product or portfolio photo pin
- [ ] Video pin
- [ ] Board creation / reorganisation (quarterly)
- [ ] Third-party content repinned to brand boards

## Monthly Calendar Review Checklist

Before submitting or executing next month's calendar, verify:

- [ ] All five Content Buckets represented across the month
- [ ] No single bucket exceeds 40% of total posts
- [ ] Monthly theme reflected in at least 30% of posts
- [ ] Seasonal anchors and upcoming events are planned 2+ weeks in advance
- [ ] Repurposing matrix applied: each blog post is distributed across all active platforms
- [ ] Hashtag sets rotated (no two consecutive posts use the same set)
- [ ] At least one community-focused post per week (features a partner, client, or industry peer)
- [ ] At least one high-engagement format per week per platform (poll, question, carousel, Reel)
- [ ] Response time protocols documented and team briefed
- [ ] UTM parameters set on all link posts for website attribution tracking
