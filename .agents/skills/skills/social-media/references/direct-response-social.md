# Direct Response Social

Use this reference when social media must drive a measurable business result instead of
general visibility alone.

## Core rule

Social media is a transport layer, not the finish line. Each campaign must specify:

1. Offer
2. Audience
3. CTA
4. Destination
5. Follow-up
6. Measurement

## Campaign structure

| Layer | Question |
|---|---|
| Offer | What specific action are we asking for? |
| Creative | What message earns the click? |
| Destination | Where does the click land? |
| Capture / conversion | What is the next step? |
| Follow-up | What happens after the form, message, or purchase? |

## Good metrics

- Cost per lead
- Qualified leads generated
- Appointment rate
- Webinar registration and show-up rate
- Audit application rate
- Revenue influenced by campaign

Weak metrics by themselves:

- Reach
- Likes
- Shares
- Comments

## Retargeting rules

- Build remarketing audiences from page visitors, video viewers, and engaged users.
- Change the message by audience stage.
- Use proof and objection removal in retargeting, not just repetition.
- Keep the CTA consistent with the audience temperature.
