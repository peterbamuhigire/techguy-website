# Social Media Skill

Social media management is not simply posting content. It is a multi-disciplinary role combining copywriting, visual design, data analysis, community facilitation, and funnel management. Done with a system, it produces measurable client results and creates a reliable agency retainer stream.

---

## When to Use

**Alongside a website build:** Run this skill after design-system and before deploy. Use the client's brand voice (from brand-alignment) and content pillars (from they-ask-you-answer) to produce a social media strategy that launches simultaneously with the website. Output: `docs/social-media-strategy.md`.

**Standalone social audit/strategy:** Run independently when an existing client needs their social media assessed and restructured. Conduct a free "health check" using Hubspot or Klout scoring, then deliver a strategy document as a paid deliverable.

**Upsell path:** Every website client is a candidate for a social retainer. After launch, use the social media strategy document as the proposal document for ongoing management.

## Platform Selection: The 4-Ring Channel Target

Structure channel selection as a target emanating outward. Each ring has a distinct role. Never assign two platforms the same role.

| Ring | Role | Typical Platform |
|------|------|-----------------|
| Ring 1 — Home Base | All content lives here; establishes expertise and archives work | Website / blog / portfolio |
| Ring 2 — Gallery / Amplifier | Distributes what is at the home base; drives traffic back to the website | Instagram, LinkedIn |
| Ring 3 — Voice / Soapbox | Commentary, personality, community; builds the human side of the brand | Twitter/X, LinkedIn |
| Ring 4 — Viral Discovery | Organic reach to audiences who have never heard of the brand | Pinterest, TikTok, Reddit |

**Platform selection criteria:**
- Match platform to the client's content strengths: visual industries use Instagram and Pinterest; professional services use LinkedIn; retail and product businesses use TikTok and Facebook
- Choose 2-3 platforms and execute them well; 8 mediocre accounts signal an unresponsive brand
- Never open an account the client cannot maintain; an inactive account is worse than no account
- Go where the client's audience already spends time — not where the platform is most popular globally

**Selection questions to ask the client:**
1. Is your product or service primarily visual? (yes → Instagram, Pinterest, TikTok)
2. Are your buyers primarily other businesses or professionals? (yes → LinkedIn priority)
3. Is real-time engagement and news commentary part of your brand? (yes → Twitter/X)
4. Does your audience skew under 35? (yes → TikTok, Instagram Reels)

## The Three-Element Brand Model

Before selecting platforms or creating content, define these three elements. They are the foundation that prevents all future inconsistency.

### Purpose
What the brand does, who it does it for, and what impact it creates. Leads to a purpose statement:

"We are [brand name], which is [what you do, specifically how you are different], and what we want to do for people is [impact]."

Purpose statements should be revisited annually as the brand evolves. Without a clear purpose, every post is an improvisation.

### Character
How the brand says things — tone, voice, values. Defined through 8-10 character attributes ranked in order of importance (e.g., integrity, warmth, expertise, directness, creativity). Character prevents misreading: sarcasm is read as arrogance without defined character. The online persona must match the in-person persona — no jarring transition when a client meets the team.

### Content
Everything created and published, shaped by purpose and character. Defined through three dimensions:
- **Profession** — professional successes, capabilities, and credentials
- **Personality** — interests, values, and perspectives outside the work
- **Passions** — what defines the brand beyond the transactional

The best content sits at the intersection of all three. Engaging content functions as an open-ended question to the reader — it makes them want to react, share, or respond.

## Content Strategy: The 5 Content Buckets

Content Buckets prevent the "what do we post today" problem. Each bucket is a distinct content category with its own purpose and platform fit.

| Bucket | What It Contains | Best Platforms |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| Finished Work | Completed projects, deliverables, case studies | Instagram, LinkedIn, Pinterest, website |
| Process | Behind-the-scenes; how the work was made | Instagram Stories, Twitter/X, blog |
| Inspiration | Things that inspire the work: articles, images, ideas | Twitter/X, Pinterest, Instagram |
| Community | Local events, other people's work, industry happenings | Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter/X |
| Personal Experience | Anecdotes, humanity, struggles — makes the brand human | Instagram, blog, LinkedIn |

**Weekly content rhythm:** Rotate through all five buckets each week. No bucket should dominate more than 40% of output. The client's brand character determines which buckets to weight most heavily — a professional services firm weights Finished Work and Community; a creative studio weights Process and Personal Experience.

**Planning a week's content:**
- Monday: Finished Work (showcase what was delivered last week)
- Tuesday: Process (behind-the-scenes from current work)
- Wednesday: Inspiration or Community (curated third-party content)
- Thursday: Personal Experience (brand humanity)
- Friday: Finished Work or Inspiration (end the week with value)

## Platform-Specific Strategy

| Platform | Best Content Type | Optimal Frequency | Primary Audience | Agency Relevance |
|----------|------------------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Facebook | Community posts, groups, live video, paid ads | 1-2 per day | Broad, all ages | High — community building and paid amplification |
| Instagram | Photos, Reels, Stories, IGTV | 1-3 per day at optimal times | Visual, 18-34 dominant | High — visual brand building for most clients |
| LinkedIn | Articles, posts, company updates | 1 per day on weekdays | Professionals, decision-makers | High — essential for B2B and professional services |
| YouTube | Tutorials, demos, vlogs, Shorts | 1-3 per week | All ages, topic-specific | Medium — high value when client can sustain video |
| TikTok | Short-form video (60s), trends, behind-the-scenes | 1-3 per day | Under-35 dominant | Medium — high for retail, consumer, and hospitality |
| Twitter/X | Short posts, threads, commentary, customer service | Multiple per day | News-driven, voice-oriented | Medium — strong for thought leadership clients |
| Pinterest | Images, infographics, tips, product photos | Multiple per day | Female-dominant (80%), purchase-intent | High for e-commerce, hospitality, interiors, food |

**Key data points for client conversations:**
- 57% of customers increase spending on a brand when they feel connected to it
- 76% of customers prefer a connected brand over a rival
- 64% of customers want brands to interact with them on social media
- Tweets with platform-optimised images produce 55% more leads than text-only tweets
- Instagram captions perform best at 138-150 characters (despite the 2,200 character limit)

## The 20/80 Content Rule

Spend 20% of time creating content. Spend 80% distributing and repurposing it.

One blog post becomes:
1. The original article (home base)
2. An Instagram carousel (key points as slides)
3. A Twitter thread (one insight per tweet)
4. A LinkedIn article (professional framing)
5. A Pinterest infographic (visual summary)
6. A short video (talking-head or screen recording)
7. An email newsletter section
8. A quote graphic for stories

This is not duplication — each format serves a different audience and platform context. The core insight is the same; the packaging is platform-native.

**Rule in practice:** Before creating new content, ask: "What existing content has not yet been distributed to all platforms?" Only after the answer is "nothing" should new content be created.

## Content Calendar Framework

### Daily Workflow (10-Step Routine)
1. Identify and prioritise essential tasks for the day
2. Monitor trending hashtags, keywords, and industry news (trends expire within minutes)
3. Create content — blogs, videos, graphics grounded in current trends
4. Curate third-party content to supplement original material
5. Schedule posts at optimal times using scheduling tools
6. Contact or follow new targeted accounts
7. Respond to all comments, messages, and mentions
8. Monitor brand mentions using automated keyword searches
9. Monitor top 5 competitor accounts — content types, volume, promotions
10. Measure daily results — do not wait for the monthly review

### Monthly Themes
- Assign one overarching theme per month aligned to the client's business cycle
- Plan seasonal anchors 6 weeks in advance (product launches, industry events, public holidays)
- Reserve 20% of the calendar for reactive/real-time content (trends, news)
- Review prior month's performance before finalising next month's plan

See `social-media/references/content-calendar-template.md` for the full monthly template and repurposing matrix.

## Community Management Rules

**Response time standards:**
- Comments and direct messages: respond within 2 hours during business hours
- Negative reviews or complaints: respond within 1 hour; never delete unless abusive
- Neutral questions: respond within 4 hours

**Tone consistency:** All responses must match the defined Character attributes. The person replying on social media is the brand — responses that deviate from character create distrust.

**Negative comment handling:**
1. Acknowledge the concern without being defensive
2. Take the conversation to a private channel (DM or email) for resolution
3. Never argue publicly; never delete a valid criticism
4. After resolution, a brief public follow-up ("glad we could sort this out") signals responsiveness to observers

**Escalation:** Create an internal escalation process: social media manager handles Tier 1 (general questions), client team handles Tier 2 (product/service specifics), management handles Tier 3 (PR or legal exposure).

## Analytics and Reporting

### KPIs by Objective

| Objective | Primary KPIs | Secondary KPIs |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Brand awareness | Reach, impressions, follower growth | Share of voice, mentions |
| Engagement | Engagement rate, comments, shares | Saves, click-through rate |
| Traffic | Click-through rate, link clicks | Sessions from social (Google Analytics) |
| Lead generation | Form completions, DM enquiries | Cost per lead (paid campaigns) |
| Conversion | Sales from social, revenue attributed | Return on ad spend |

**Engagement rate benchmark:** (likes + comments + shares) / reach × 100. A rate above 1% is good; above 3% is excellent for most business accounts.

**Do not track vanity metrics alone.** Follower count tells nothing about business impact. How much followers share content is a more significant indicator than how many followers exist.

### Monthly Reporting Format
1. Executive summary (3 sentences: what happened, what worked, what changes next month)
2. Reach and impressions (vs prior month)
3. Engagement rate per platform
4. Click-through and traffic to website
5. Top 3 performing posts (with explanation of why they worked)
6. Bottom 3 performing posts (with action taken)
7. Follower growth and net change
8. Competitor snapshot (1-2 observations)
9. Next month plan summary

## Agency Service Structure

### Three-Tier Package Model

**Launch (2 platforms)**
- Platform setup or audit and optimisation
- Content calendar (12 posts/month per platform)
- Community management (comments and messages)
- Monthly analytics report
- Positioning: $800-$1,200/month
- Best for: small businesses launching social media for the first time

**Grow (4 platforms)**
- Everything in Launch
- 20 posts/month per platform
- Blog-to-social repurposing (2 blog posts/month converted to social content)
- A/B content testing
- Bi-weekly check-in call
- Positioning: $1,500-$2,500/month
- Best for: growing businesses with an established brand and website

**Scale (6 platforms)**
- Everything in Grow
- 25+ posts/month per platform
- Influencer identification and outreach
- Paid social campaign management (ad spend billed separately)
- Weekly reporting
- Quarterly strategy review
- Positioning: $3,000-$5,000/month
- Best for: established businesses with aggressive growth targets

**Ad management:** Always bill separately from management fees. Standard model: client pays ad spend directly; agency charges 15-20% of ad spend as management fee, with a minimum of $300/month.

### Upsell Path from Website Build
1. At website launch, deliver a free social media health check alongside the site
2. Present the social media strategy document as the proposal for an ongoing retainer
3. Start the client on Launch tier — reduce friction to the first yes
4. Review at 90 days; upgrade clients showing engagement growth to Grow tier
5. Advanced analytics and paid campaigns are natural Scale upgrades at 6-12 months

## Integration With Other Skills

- **brand-strategy / brand-alignment:** Brand voice, character attributes, and visual identity feed directly into social media tone and content standards. Run brand-alignment before defining the Character element.
- **they-ask-you-answer:** The Big 5 content pillars and Selling 7 video formats map directly onto the Content Buckets. Blog articles from they-ask-you-answer become the content that is repurposed across all social platforms.
- **blog-writer:** Every blog article is a source asset for social content. Apply the 20/80 rule — distribute each article across all active platforms before writing the next one.
- **ecommerce-funnel:** Social media is the primary traffic source for the top of the ecommerce funnel. Platform selection and content strategy must align with the funnel's traffic temperature model (cold discovery on TikTok/Pinterest; warm nurturing on Instagram/Facebook).
- **sales-copywriting:** Caption formulas, CTA language, and headline structures from sales-copywriting apply directly to social post copy.
