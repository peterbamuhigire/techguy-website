# Platform Playbook

Detailed, platform-by-platform strategy guide for social media management. Use this alongside SKILL.md when building a client's platform-specific content plan or briefing a team member on a specific channel.

---

## Facebook

**Algorithm notes:**
- Organic reach has declined significantly since 2014; expect 2-6% organic reach on posts to page followers
- Facebook Groups receive preferential algorithmic treatment over page posts; where appropriate, create a group alongside the page
- Video (especially live video) receives the highest organic reach of any content format
- Posts that generate comments outperform posts that generate only likes; ask questions

**Content formats that work:**
- Status updates with a strong question or opinion (drives comments)
- Native video (uploaded directly, not YouTube links — Facebook suppresses external links)
- Facebook Live (real-time video; notifies followers proactively)
- Link posts with custom thumbnails (use UTM parameters to track traffic)
- Photo albums (events, behind-the-scenes, project completion)
- Facebook Groups for community building (not just broadcasting)

**Posting frequency:** 1-2 posts per day. Posting more than twice per day dilutes engagement rate and can suppress individual post reach.

**Engagement tactics:**
- End every post with a question or a specific call to action
- Reply to every comment within 2 hours; Facebook's algorithm rewards active engagement
- Use Facebook Messenger as a customer service channel; enable automated responses for out-of-hours
- Pin your best-performing post or most important announcement to the top of the page
- Run "this or that" polls to drive quick engagement without requiring effort from followers

**Hashtag strategy:** 1-3 hashtags maximum; Facebook is not a hashtag-driven platform. Use branded hashtags for campaigns only.

**Paid amplification:**
- Facebook Ads are the most sophisticated self-serve ad platform available; targeting by age, interest, location, and behaviour is precise
- Boost posts only after they demonstrate organic engagement; never boost underperforming content
- Use custom audiences (website visitors via pixel, email list upload) for retargeting
- Lookalike audiences from existing customer lists are the most cost-effective cold audience tactic

## Instagram

**Algorithm notes:**
- The algorithm prioritises posts with high early engagement; the first 30 minutes after posting are critical
- Reels receive the broadest organic reach of any Instagram format
- Stories appear to followers in chronological order and are not algorithmically ranked; consistent daily Stories maintain top-of-mind presence
- Saves are weighted more heavily than likes; content that provides reference value (recipes, tutorials, checklists) accumulates saves over time

**Content formats that work:**
- Single image posts (high-quality photography or graphic design)
- Carousel posts (multiple images or slides; higher average engagement than single images due to swipe interaction)
- Reels (short-form video; currently the primary growth format; algorithm promotes to non-followers)
- Stories (15-second segments; disappear after 24 hours; excellent for behind-the-scenes and interactive content)
- IGTV / longer video (used for tutorials and in-depth content)

**Posting frequency:** 1 post per day at the client's optimal time (check Instagram Insights for when followers are most active). Follower growth is associated with 2-3 posts per day when content quality is maintained, but never post for the sake of posting.

**Caption strategy:**
- Optimal engagement occurs at 138-150 characters (despite the 2,200 character limit)
- Lead with the most important sentence; Instagram truncates captions after 2-3 lines
- Use line breaks generously; dense caption blocks are not read
- End with a clear call to action: ask a question, prompt a save, or direct to the link in bio

**Hashtag strategy:**
- Use 5-15 hashtags per post (full 30 is no longer recommended; it can suppress reach)
- Mix of tiers: 2-3 large (1M+ posts), 4-6 mid-size (100K-1M posts), 4-6 niche (under 100K posts)
- Niche hashtags drive the most qualified engagement; large hashtags surface content briefly in a high-volume feed
- Create a branded hashtag for every client; use it consistently across all posts
- Rotate hashtag sets to avoid appearing spammy; build 3-4 sets and cycle through them

**Engagement tactics:**
- Reply to every comment; use the reply with the commenter's name for personalisation
- Engage with followers' own content (like and comment on posts from active followers)
- Use Instagram Stories polls, questions, and quiz stickers for direct audience interaction
- Post Stories daily even when feed posts are less frequent; it maintains algorithmic relevance

**Paid amplification:** Promote Reels and high-performing carousel posts. Use Instagram Shopping for product clients. Stories ads perform well for awareness campaigns.

## LinkedIn

**Algorithm notes:**
- LinkedIn rewards content that generates immediate, meaningful engagement (comments especially)
- Native articles reach both followers and non-followers via LinkedIn's content recommendations
- Personal profiles consistently outperform company pages for organic reach; the founder or key team member should post, not just the company page
- Posting on Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday mornings (7-9am local time) tends to outperform other times

**Content formats that work:**
- Short posts with one clear insight (3-5 paragraphs; no wall of text)
- Native documents (PDF carousels uploaded as documents; high engagement format)
- Native video (recorded and uploaded directly)
- Long-form articles (for thought leadership; searchable on LinkedIn and Google)
- Poll posts (2-4 answer options; drives engagement and algorithmic distribution)
- Behind-the-scenes and team content (humanises B2B brands)

**Posting frequency:** 1 post per weekday. Consistent daily posting on LinkedIn compounds over time because the algorithm continues surfacing older posts to new connections.

**Engagement tactics:**
- The first comment on your own post (adding a relevant resource or question) boosts early engagement signals
- Tag clients, collaborators, and partners sparingly and only when genuinely relevant
- Join and participate in LinkedIn Groups relevant to the client's target industry
- Respond to every comment with substantive replies, not just acknowledgements
- Connect with commenters — an active commenter who is not yet a follower is a warm lead

**Hashtag strategy:** 3-5 hashtags, placed at the end of the post. Use industry-specific terms. LinkedIn hashtags follow specific topics; using well-followed hashtags (e.g., #digitalmarketing, #leadership) distributes content to non-followers who follow those tags.

**Paid amplification:** LinkedIn Ads are expensive (CPCs of $5-15 are typical) but offer unmatched B2B targeting by job title, company size, industry, and seniority. Use for high-value B2B clients where the cost per lead justifies the spend.

## YouTube

**Algorithm notes:**
- YouTube's algorithm prioritises watch time and click-through rate from thumbnails; both must be optimised
- Playlists group related content and keep viewers on the channel longer; create playlists for every content series
- YouTube is the second-largest search engine; video titles and descriptions must be keyword-optimised
- YouTube Shorts (vertical video under 60 seconds) is the growth format; cross-post content from TikTok and Instagram Reels

**Content formats that work:**
- Tutorials and how-to guides (highest search demand)
- Product or service demonstrations
- Case studies and testimonials (builds trust at the consideration stage)
- Thought leadership / talking-head videos
- Behind-the-scenes and vlog-style content
- YouTube Shorts (repurposed from TikTok or Reels)

**Posting frequency:** 1-3 videos per week for consistent channel growth. Quality over quantity — a well-optimised video every week outperforms rushed daily uploads. Shorts can supplement at higher frequency.

**Engagement tactics:**
- Use custom thumbnails with bold text and a clear human face (if appropriate); thumbnails drive click-through rate more than anything else
- Place a verbal and on-screen CTA at the end of every video: "subscribe," "watch next," or "download the guide"
- Add cards and end screens to every video linking to related content
- Respond to comments in the first 24 hours; YouTube's algorithm rewards active comment sections
- Create chapters using timestamps in the description — this improves watch time and searchability

**SEO for YouTube:**
- Include the primary keyword in the video title, first line of description, and file name before upload
- Write descriptions of at least 200 words; YouTube uses description text for search indexing
- Add closed captions; they improve search indexing and accessibility
- Tags are less important than titles and descriptions but still worth completing

**Paid amplification:** YouTube pre-roll ads (skippable) and mid-roll ads work well for brand awareness. In-feed video ads (showing in search results) work for consideration-stage campaigns.

## TikTok

**Algorithm notes:**
- TikTok's "For You Page" (FYP) shows content to non-followers; follower count is less important than content quality
- A video with 100 followers can reach 1 million people if it performs well in the initial distribution batch
- TikTok tests each video with a small sample audience; strong engagement triggers wider distribution
- Completion rate (percentage of viewers who watch the full video) is the primary ranking signal

**Content formats that work:**
- Short educational videos ("did you know..." and "3 things about..." formats)
- Process videos (showing how something is made or done)
- Trend participation (using trending audio and adapting it to the brand's context)
- Behind-the-scenes and team content
- Before-and-after transformations (website launches, design work, renovation)
- Response videos (use the Stitch and Duet features to respond to relevant content)

**Posting frequency:** 1-3 videos per day for growth; minimum 1 per day to maintain algorithmic relevance. TikTok rewards consistency and volume more than any other platform.

**Engagement tactics:**
- Hook the viewer in the first 1-2 seconds; state the value of the video immediately
- Use text overlays throughout to reinforce the audio; many users watch without sound initially
- Reply to comments with a video response where possible (TikTok's response video feature)
- Participate in trending audio and challenges with a brand-relevant angle
- Use 3-5 hashtags maximum: 1-2 trending or broad, 2-3 niche

**Hashtag strategy:** Hashtags on TikTok function differently from Instagram — they signal content category to the algorithm. Use #fyp or #foryoupage sparingly (oversaturated); niche hashtags that describe the content type and industry are more effective for reaching a relevant audience.

## Twitter / X

**Algorithm notes:**
- Twitter shows both chronological and algorithmic "For You" timelines; recency still matters
- Threads (a series of connected tweets) receive higher engagement and distribution than single tweets
- Engagement (especially replies and retweets) within the first hour after posting is the primary amplification signal
- Twitter is the platform most driven by real-time events; tie content to what is happening now whenever relevant

**Content formats that work:**
- Short, opinion-driven observations (1-2 sentences)
- Threads (5-10 tweets expanding on a single topic)
- Quote tweets with commentary on industry news (adds the brand's voice to trending conversations)
- Short videos (native video performs better than YouTube links)
- Polls (2-4 options; drives interaction from followers who may not otherwise engage)
- Text-only threads (often outperform image posts on Twitter for engagement)

**Posting frequency:** 3-8 times per day. Twitter's fast-moving timeline means more frequent posting is expected and accepted. Use a scheduling tool to space posts throughout the day.

**Engagement tactics:**
- Reply to industry conversations and trending topics; Twitter rewards active community participants
- Retweet and quote-tweet content from industry peers, clients, and collaborators
- Monitor brand mentions in real-time; Twitter is a customer service channel — unanswered mentions are visible to all followers
- Pin the most important tweet (latest campaign, product launch, or best-performing content) to the profile

**Hashtag strategy:** 1-2 hashtags per tweet. Using more than 2 hashtags is associated with lower engagement on Twitter. Use trending hashtags only when genuinely relevant — hashtag hijacking reads as opportunistic and damages brand character.

## Pinterest

**Algorithm notes:**
- Pinterest functions as a visual search engine, not a social media feed; content has a long shelf life (a pin from 2 years ago can still drive traffic today)
- Keyword-rich pin descriptions and board titles are essential for search discovery
- Vertical images (2:3 ratio) receive priority placement in the feed
- Consistency matters: accounts that pin daily are rewarded with broader distribution

**Content formats that work:**
- Infographics (high save rate; shared broadly; drives discovery)
- Step-by-step tutorials with numbered steps visible in the image
- Product photography (e-commerce and lifestyle brands)
- Inspirational quote graphics (personal branding, wellness, lifestyle)
- Blog post featured images (drive traffic back to the website)
- Video pins (less common but emerging)

**Posting frequency:** 5-15 pins per day for active growth. Pinterest rewards volume and consistency more than any other platform. Use Tailwind (Pinterest's official scheduling partner) for batch scheduling.

**Engagement tactics:**
- Create boards that reflect the client's Content Buckets (Finished Work, Inspiration, Process)
- Write keyword-rich descriptions for every pin (150-300 words recommended)
- Include a clear CTA in the pin description directing to the website
- Repin relevant third-party content into thematic boards (this builds board authority without requiring original creation)
- Join group boards in the client's niche to reach non-followers

**Hashtag strategy:** Use 2-5 keyword-focused hashtags. On Pinterest, hashtags link to hashtag feeds — use them to appear in the hashtag stream. However, SEO-optimised descriptions and board titles matter more than hashtags for Pinterest discovery.

**Audience note:** 80% female, 70 million users, 42% of US adult women have accounts. Half of regular Pinterest users visit the platform before making a purchase. Average order value from Pinterest visitors is $58.95 — higher than most other social referral sources. Prioritise for e-commerce, hospitality, interiors, food, and lifestyle clients.
