# Assignment Selling Templates

## Pre-Discovery Call Email

**Subject:** Before our call on [Day] — 3 things to review (20 min)

Hi [Name],

Looking forward to speaking on [Day] at [Time].

To make our conversation as useful as possible for you — and to make sure we don't waste each other's time if we're not a good fit — I'd like to ask you to spend about 20 minutes reviewing three resources before we talk:

**1. How much does a professional website cost? (10 min)**
[Link to pricing article]
This will give you honest context for our pricing conversation so there are no surprises.

**2. Who we work best with — and who we don't (5 min)**
[Link to The 80% Video or "When you should NOT hire us" article]
If you're in the wrong-fit category, better to know now.

**3. A result similar to your situation (5 min)**
[Link to most relevant case study]
This is what the process looks like in practice, and what kind of results are realistic.

If after reviewing these you still feel we might be a good fit, I'm genuinely looking forward to our conversation.

If you'd rather not do this homework — no problem. We can proceed with the call as scheduled.

See you [Day].

[Name]

## Pre-Call Questionnaire

Send alongside the pre-call email. Keep it under 5 questions.

**Subject:** [Agency Name] — Quick questions before [Day]

Hi [Name],

Four questions to help me prepare for our call. Takes about 5 minutes:

1. What does your business do, and who is your ideal client?

2. What is your #1 business goal for the next 12 months? (Be specific — "grow revenue by 40%", "open a second location", "launch in a new market")

3. What is your website's current biggest problem? (What does it fail to do that you wish it did?)

4. Have you worked with a web agency or freelancer before? If yes — what went well and what didn't?

5. What's your approximate budget range for this project? (Helps me know what solutions to prepare.)
   [ ] Under $3,000
   [ ] $3,000–$8,000
   [ ] $8,000–$15,000
   [ ] $15,000–$25,000
   [ ] $25,000+

Thanks — see you [Day].

## Post-Discovery Objection Content Map

When a prospect raises one of these objections after the discovery call, send this content:

| Objection | Content to Send |
|-----------|----------------|
| "It's too expensive" | Pricing breakdown article + total cost of doing nothing calculation |
| "We had a bad experience before" | "Warning signs of a bad agency" article + references from 2 clients |
| "We need to think about it" | Customer journey video most relevant to their industry |
| "Can AI just do this now?" | The 80% Video + "Doctor-Pilot-Teacher" explainer if written |
| "Can you show me more examples?" | 3 case studies in their sector + a benchmark article for their industry |
| "We're getting other quotes" | Comparison article + pricing breakdown + "questions to ask every agency" |

## Content Promotion Stack

After publishing each Big 5 article:

**Day 1:** Publish on website blog
**Day 2:** Share on LinkedIn with a 3-paragraph commentary (not just a link)
**Day 3:** Send to email list with a brief intro paragraph
**Day 7:** Repurpose key points as 5-tweet thread or 5 LinkedIn posts (one per week)
**Day 14:** Add to pre-discovery call assignment stack if relevant
**Day 30:** Check Google Search Console — what queries is it ranking for? Are there related questions to write about?
