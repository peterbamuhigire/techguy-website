# Big 5 Article Templates

## Pillar 1: Pricing Article Template

**Title formula:** "How Much Does a [Service] Cost in [Region]? (The Honest Breakdown)"

**Word count:** 1,200–2,000 words

**Structure:**

### Introduction (100-150 words)
Open with the question as stated. Acknowledge that most agencies won't answer it. Commit to giving a real answer.

"If you've tried to get a straight answer on website pricing in [region], you've probably been told 'it depends' — and then sent a discovery call link. We're going to do something different: give you real numbers, explain exactly what drives them, and tell you when our pricing doesn't make sense for your situation."

### The Short Answer (50-100 words)
Give a real range before explaining it.

"A professionally built website in [region] from a quality agency typically costs between $[X] and $[Y]. Here's exactly what determines where in that range you land."

### The Breakdown (500-800 words)
Break down each cost component:
- Discovery and strategy: what it includes, why it costs what it costs
- Design: custom vs. template, number of revisions, brand integration
- Development: static vs. dynamic, CMS, integrations
- Content: copywriting, photography, video
- SEO setup: keyword research, on-page, technical
- Testing and launch: QA, speed, mobile
- Ongoing: hosting, maintenance, monthly optimisation

### What Drives Cost Higher (200-300 words)
- E-commerce functionality
- Custom integrations (booking systems, CRMs, databases)
- Multiple languages
- Large page count
- Rush timelines

### What Keeps Cost Lower (100-150 words)
- Client provides all copy and photos
- Template-based design
- Smaller page count
- Longer timeline

### When NOT to Hire Us (100-150 words)
Be honest about who this pricing doesn't suit. Refer them elsewhere.

### Next Step CTA
"If this range makes sense for your situation, the best next step is a 20-minute diagnostic call where we look at your current online presence and identify the 3 biggest opportunities. No pitch — just a diagnosis."

## Pillar 2: Problems Article Template

**Title formula:** "When You Should NOT Hire [Agency Name] (And Who You Should Hire Instead)"

**Word count:** 800–1,200 words

**Structure:**

### Introduction
"Most agencies will tell you they're perfect for everyone. We're not. Here's an honest list of situations where we're the wrong choice — and what you should do instead."

### The Wrong Fit List (3-5 scenarios)
For each:
- The situation: "If you need a website in 2 weeks..."
- Why we're not right: "We take 8-12 weeks because we don't skip the strategy and testing phases. Rushing produces a site that looks good but doesn't convert."
- What to do instead: "A freelancer on Fiverr can produce something fast. It won't perform as well, but if speed is the priority, that's your best option."

### The Right Fit List (3-4 scenarios)
"Here's who we do our best work with..."
- Business type, budget range, goals, mindset about the process

### The Stakes
"Why does this matter? Because the worst outcome for both parties is a client who hired us expecting something we can't deliver. We'd rather you go elsewhere than work together with mismatched expectations."

### CTA
"If you read the wrong-fit list and didn't see yourself — let's talk."

## Pillar 3: Comparison Article Template

**Title formula:** "Agency vs. Freelancer vs. DIY: Which Is Right for [Target Sector] Businesses?"

**Word count:** 1,000–1,500 words

**Comparison table format:**

| Factor | DIY (Wix/Squarespace) | Freelancer | Agency |
|--------|----------------------|------------|--------|
| Cost | $0-$500/year | $1,000-$5,000 | $5,000-$25,000+ |
| Timeline | 1-4 weeks | 4-8 weeks | 8-16 weeks |
| Quality ceiling | Medium | High | Highest |
| SEO capability | Basic | Variable | Comprehensive |
| Ongoing support | Self-managed | Variable | Included |
| Best for | Startups testing ideas | Established SMBs, limited budget | Businesses that need leads from their site |

**For each option, cover:**
- What you get
- What you don't get
- When it makes sense
- When it doesn't

**Honest conclusion:** Recommend the option that genuinely fits the reader's situation, even if it's not the agency.

## Pillar 5: Best in Class Article Template

**Title formula:** "The Best [Industry] Websites in [Region] — And What Makes Them Work"

**Word count:** 1,200–1,800 words

**Structure:**

### Introduction
"We've spent [time] analysing the highest-converting [industry] websites in [region]. Here's what the best ones have in common — and what most get wrong."

### The Criteria (define your standards)
"A 'best' website in [industry] isn't the one that wins design awards. It's the one that converts visitors into enquiries. We evaluated sites on: [list 5-6 specific criteria]."

### The Examples (3-5 sites)
For each:
- What they do well (specific, not vague)
- Screenshot or description of the specific element
- Why it works (the psychology behind it)
- What they could improve (shows your expertise)

### The Common Patterns
"After analysing [X] sites, here's what the best ones have in common..."
- List 5-7 specific, actionable characteristics

### What Most Sites Get Wrong
"Here's what 80% of [industry] sites do that costs them leads..."

### CTA
"Want to see how your site compares? We'll run a free 47-point audit and show you exactly where you stand against these benchmarks."
