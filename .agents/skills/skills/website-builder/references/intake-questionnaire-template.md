# Intake Questionnaire Template

Use this template across two moments:

1. `Pre-call intake` to qualify the lead and gather basics
2. `Discovery interview` to diagnose the business problem properly

The sequence intentionally follows a SPIN-style flow:

- situation first
- problem second
- implication third
- need-payoff fourth

This prevents shallow website briefs and protects against quoting the wrong solution.

## Part 2 - Discovery Interview

Document answers in the client's own language where possible.

### Section A - Situation Questions

Use to establish facts, not to impress.

- How does the business make money today?
- Which services, offers, or products are most important?
- Who is the ideal customer for the website?
- What usually happens before a prospect contacts you?
- What channels currently bring enquiries or sales?
- What tools are already in place for analytics, CRM, forms, or bookings?
- What content, proof, and brand assets already exist?
- Which competitors do prospects compare you against?
- What technical constraints already exist on hosting, CMS, integrations, or internal IT?

### Section B - Problem Questions

Use to surface friction, confusion, and missed opportunities.

- Where is the current website failing the business?
- What do prospects misunderstand before they speak with you?
- Which pages, offers, or conversion paths are weakest?
- What content is missing or out of date?
- Where do approvals get delayed internally?
- What happens when a prospect is not a fit?
- What trust or compliance concerns cause hesitation?
- Which internal bottlenecks usually slow projects down?

### Section C - Implication Questions

Use to expose the cost of inaction.

- What does the current problem cost in lost enquiries, poor leads, or weak positioning?
- If this stays unchanged for another 6 to 12 months, what happens?
- How does weak messaging affect sales conversations?
- What happens when the wrong type of lead books a call?
- What happens when content or approvals arrive late?
- What reputational risk exists if compliance or trust signals are weak?
- How much rework happens because requirements are not agreed early?

### Section D - Need-Payoff Questions

Use to define success in business terms.

- If the website performed properly, what would it allow the business to do?
- What would better-fit leads change for the sales process?
- What would faster approvals improve operationally?
- What would strong proof and clear positioning make easier?
- What would a better intake and discovery process save in time or margin?
- What would success look like 90 days after launch?
- Which metrics would tell you this investment was worthwhile?

## Required Discovery Outputs

Discovery is not complete until it can produce:

- discovery summary
- positioning summary
- sitemap and page goals
- offer and CTA hierarchy
- style brief
- content inventory
- SEO brief
- launch checklist input

## Discovery Rules

- Do not promise scope before this is complete.
- Do not let email or WhatsApp become the definitive brief.
- Record assumptions explicitly.
- Flag unknowns that affect scope, compliance, or timeline.
- If content readiness is weak, recommend paid discovery before a fixed build.
