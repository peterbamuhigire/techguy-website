# Alignment Workshop Runbook

Workshops produce decisions, not posters. Run this only when stakeholders can attend and the client expects to leave with binding choices.

## Pre-work (one week before)

- Send the current-state map and three to five "moments of truth" to all attendees.
- Ask each attendee to mark, privately, the moment they think hurts the business most.
- Confirm the decision rights: who can approve scope, content, pricing, and visual direction.

## Attendee shape

- Decision maker (one).
- Sales lead, support lead, operations lead, marketing lead.
- One frontline person who actually serves customers.
- Agency: facilitator, content lead, design lead.

A workshop without the decision maker is rescheduled, not run.

## Agenda (2.5 hours)

1. Frame (10 min). State the business goal, the question being decided, and what counts as a decision today.
2. Walk the current-state map (25 min). Read it stage by stage. Stop at every "this is wrong" — that is the data.
3. Surface the moments of truth (20 min). Each attendee names one. Cluster on the wall or a shared board.
4. Decide on the future state per stage (45 min). For each priority moment, decide: kept, changed, removed, or new. Write the decision verbatim.
5. Translate to website implications (30 min). For each future-state decision, name the page, the proof, and the metric.
6. Backstage routing (10 min). For each implication that needs operations to change, name the owner and a date.
7. Close (10 min). Read back decisions, owners, dates. Confirm escalation route for any unresolved issue.

## Facilitation rules

- One conversation at a time. No side meetings.
- "Yes, and" before "yes, but". Disagreements are captured, not closed by seniority alone.
- Every decision gets an owner and a date. No owner means no decision.
- Park items are tracked publicly with a deadline. They expire on that date.

## Output document (sent within 24 hours)

- Future-state map (or marked-up current-state map).
- Decisions log: stage, decision, rationale, owner, date.
- Website implication table from `map-to-sitemap-conversion.md`.
- Backstage backlog routed to `service-blueprint-website-delivery`.
- Risks, parked items, and open questions with owners.

## When the workshop fails

If the room cannot agree on the goal, the decision rights, or the customer evidence, stop the workshop. Recommend paid discovery or a smaller premium scope. Building on misalignment guarantees post-launch rework.
