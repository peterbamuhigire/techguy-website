# Premium Website Discovery Interview Guide

Outside-in interviews that drive site architecture and content priorities.

## Sample design

- Five to seven interviews per buyer type. Stop only when two consecutive interviews surface no new themes.
- Mix recent buyers, near-buyers who chose a competitor, and lost-cause buyers. Avoid only-happy-customer bias.
- Include at least two interviews with the client's own sales, support, and operations staff for backstage signal.

## Format

- 30 to 45 minutes. Recorded with consent. One interviewer, one notetaker.
- Open with the buyer's own goal, not the client's product.
- Use silence. Most useful detail surfaces in the second pause after a question.

## Question bank

Behaviour, not opinion. Past, not hypothetical.

1. Walk me through the last time you needed to solve [the problem the site addresses]. What happened first?
2. What did you try before talking to [this kind of provider]?
3. When you started looking online, what did you actually type? What did you click?
4. What information were you looking for that you could not find?
5. Who else was involved in the decision? What did you have to send them?
6. What made you feel safer about choosing one option over another?
7. What almost stopped you from going ahead? What broke that fear?
8. What did you expect to happen after you submitted the form / paid / booked?
9. If a site like this had existed, what would have made you trust it within five seconds?
10. What words do you use with friends to describe what we do? (Capture verbatim — feeds copy and SEO.)

## What to capture per interview

- Verbatim quotes, especially objection language and category language.
- Decision moments: what tipped them.
- Fear moments: what slowed or blocked them.
- Information gaps: what they had to find elsewhere.
- Proof needs: case study, certification, person, photo, number.
- Comparison set: which alternatives they considered.
- After-purchase expectations: what they expected next.

## Synthesis pattern

After all interviews:

- Cluster quotes into stages of the future-state map.
- Tag each cluster with the website implication: page, copy, proof, form, CTA, analytics event.
- Mark which clusters are fact-supported vs opinion-supported. Fact wins.
- Surface contradictions; do not average them away. They usually signal two different buyer types.

## Validation thresholds

A theme earns a place on the future-state map when:

- It appears in at least 30% of interviews, OR
- It appears in at least one interview AND analytics confirm it, OR
- It is a known sector-specific compliance or trust requirement, regardless of count.

## Hand-off

Pass the synthesis to:

- `website-experience-mapping` for the future-state map.
- `content-writing` and `sales-copywriting` for verbatim language.
- `seo` for actual search terms used.
- `form-ux-design` for required vs optional fields.
- `cro-audit` for objection placement on landing pages.
