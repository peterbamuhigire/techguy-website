# Style Brief

---

## Overall Mood

**How should visitors feel when they land on the site?**
Bold, Professional, and Warm.

Confident without being corporate-stiff. The site should feel like meeting a seasoned expert who is genuinely approachable — someone who has built systems across continents and can explain complex technology plainly. Think "trusted senior consultant" not "anonymous agency."

---

## Colour Preferences

**Brand colours:**
Rich earth tones with a technology edge. The palette should feel unmistakably African yet modern and professional — grounded, warm, and distinctive.

- **Primary**: Deep forest green (#1B4332 or similar) — authority, growth, reliability
- **Secondary**: Warm gold/amber (#D4A843 or similar) — excellence, warmth, African earth
- **Accent**: Burnt orange (#C75B12 or similar) — energy, calls to action, highlights
- **Dark backgrounds**: Rich dark charcoal (#1A1A2E or similar) — for hero sections, navigation, footer
- **Light backgrounds**: Warm off-white (#FAF8F5 or similar) — for content sections, readability
- **Text**: Near-black on light (#1A1A1A), warm white on dark (#F5F0EB)

**Colours to avoid:**
- Generic corporate blue (too common, too template-looking)
- Bright neon colours (unprofessional)
- Purple gradients (overused in SaaS/tech templates)
- Pure white backgrounds (too cold — use warm off-white instead)

---

## Dark Mode Direction

Yes — dark hero sections, dark navigation, dark footer. Light content sections for readability. The contrast between dark and light sections creates visual rhythm and sophistication. Hero sections should feel immersive. Content sections should feel open and readable.

## Design Preferences

**Photography style**: Peter's actual photos throughout. Professional headshot as anchor image. Product screenshots for portfolio. No stock photography of people — use CSS gradients or solid colour placeholders where photos are not yet available. When photos arrive, they should feel natural and professional — not staged studio shots.

**Typography direction**: Distinctive heading font that conveys authority and character — bold, modern, with personality. NOT Inter, Roboto, Arial, or system fonts. Consider serif or semi-serif headings for gravitas (something like Playfair Display, DM Serif Display, or Fraunces). Clean sans-serif body font with excellent readability (like DM Sans, Work Sans, or Plus Jakarta Sans). All fonts self-hosted via Fontsource.

**Animations**: Subtle and purposeful. Gentle fade-ins and slide-ups on scroll using GSAP. Animated number counters for the statistics section. No flashy or distracting animations. Movement should feel confident and measured — like the brand itself.

**Layout density**: Spacious with generous whitespace. Large bold typography for headings. Clean sections with clear visual separation. Content-rich where needed (services, portfolio) but never cluttered. Let whitespace communicate confidence.

**Icons**: Lucide or Phosphor icon set. Consistent style throughout — line icons, not filled. Used for service pillars, contact details, social links, and feature lists.

## Special Elements

- **African-inspired geometric pattern** — Subtle geometric pattern (inspired by East African textiles or Kente cloth geometry) as a background texture or section divider. Used sparingly to reinforce cultural identity without overwhelming the content. Could be an SVG pattern at low opacity.

- **Code-inspired elements** — Terminal-style snippets or syntax highlighting accents in the portfolio section to reinforce the developer identity. A subtle monospace font treatment for tech labels or skill tags.

- **Map visualisation** — In the About section, a stylised map of Africa highlighting countries where Peter has worked (Uganda, Kenya, Tanzania, Rwanda, DRC, Senegal, Sierra Leone, Guinea). Not a generic Google Maps embed — a designed element.

- **Consultation booking CTA** — The primary CTA ("Book a Consultation") should be visually distinct throughout the site. Use the burnt orange accent colour for CTA buttons to make them stand out against both dark and light backgrounds.

---

## What to Avoid

- Generic corporate blue colour schemes
- Stock photography of white people in offices
- Template-looking card layouts with uniform spacing (the "SaaS startup" look)
- Inter, Roboto, Arial, or other overused system fonts as primary typeface
- Cluttered layouts with too many competing visual elements
- "Freelancer" or "gig worker" vibes — this is a professional consultancy
- Excessive gradients or glass-morphism effects
- Animations that feel playful or casual — keep movement confident and measured
- External placeholder image services (no Unsplash, Placeholder.com, Picsum)
- Cookie-cutter testimonial carousels — if testimonials scroll, do it with taste
