## Article 1 — "How Much Does Software Development Cost in Uganda?"

**File:** `src/pages/en/blog/software-development-cost-uganda.astro`
**Slug:** `software-development-cost-uganda`
**Primary keyword:** `software development cost Uganda` / `how much does software development cost Uganda`
**Pillar:** 1 (Cost and pricing)
**Internal links to:** `/en/software-development-uganda/`, `/en/website-development-uganda/`, `/en/contact/`
**Estimated word count:** 2,000
**Date:** 2026-05-05

**Meta title:**
```
How Much Does Software Development Cost in Uganda? — A Transparent Breakdown
```

**Meta description:**
```
Software development costs in Uganda explained: from simple websites to custom ERP systems. What factors affect price, what to expect for each type of project, and where the common pricing traps are.
```

**H1:**
```
How much does software development cost in Uganda? A transparent breakdown
```

**Opening (first 150 words — write in full):**
```
Software development costs in Uganda are poorly documented, widely misquoted, and frequently misunderstood by buyers and sellers alike. A Ugandan business owner who has received three proposals for a similar system and found prices ranging from UGX 2,000,000 to UGX 50,000,000 for what appears to be the same thing is not unusual — they are the norm. The variation is real, and most of it is explained by factors that the proposals themselves rarely disclose.

This article explains how software development in Uganda is actually priced, what those prices cover, what they do not cover, and what a reasonable budget looks like for each category of software project. It is written from 15 years of building and commissioning software in Uganda and across East Africa — so the figures here are not aspirational; they are based on actual Ugandan market pricing.
```

**Article structure:**
- H2: The four categories of software development in Uganda
  - Website (static site vs. WordPress vs. custom)
  - Web application (database-driven, custom logic)
  - Mobile app (Android/iOS, native vs. hybrid)
  - ERP / enterprise system (multi-module, multi-user)
- H2: Price ranges by category (UGX and USD)
  - Simple website: UGX 2,000,000–5,000,000 (USD 550–1,400)
  - WordPress website with CMS: UGX 3,000,000–8,000,000 (USD 800–2,200)
  - Custom web application: UGX 8,000,000–40,000,000+ (USD 2,200–11,000+)
  - Mobile app: UGX 15,000,000–80,000,000+ (USD 4,000–22,000+)
  - ERP system: UGX 30,000,000–200,000,000+ (USD 8,000–55,000+)
- H2: What the price includes — and what it does not
  - What to ask: hosting, maintenance, training, support, source code ownership
- H2: Why proposals vary so much in Uganda
  - Different scope assumptions
  - Different quality of delivery
  - Different maintenance models
- H2: How to evaluate a software development proposal
  - 5 questions to ask before signing
- H2: Is it cheaper to use a freelancer or a firm?
  - Honest comparison
- Contextual CTA (in-text): "If you are evaluating a custom software requirement, the [Software Development Uganda page](/en/software-development-uganda/) has more detail on the practice's approach and typical engagement structures."

```
The alternative is not complicated. Seven questions, asked directly and listened to honestly, will identify whether an IT consultant has genuine competence in the specific problem you need solved. **Meta title:**
```

```
Custom Software vs. Off-the-Shelf in Uganda — When to Build and When to Buy
```

**Meta description:**
```
Should your Ugandan business build custom software or buy an existing system? A direct comparison of the cases for each option — with examples from Uganda and East Africa.
```

**H1:**
```
Custom software vs. off-the-shelf systems for Ugandan businesses — when to build and when to buy
```

**Opening (first 150 words):**
```
The question of whether to build custom software or buy an existing system is one of the most consequential technology decisions a Ugandan business can make — and one that is almost always made without adequate information. The conventional wisdom — "buy if it exists, build only if nothing else works" — is partially right and frequently misapplied. The cases for custom development are more numerous than most technology buyers realise, and the cases for off-the-shelf are more limited in the Ugandan context than most technology vendors will tell you.

This article sets out the honest decision framework: what custom development costs versus what it delivers, what off-the-shelf systems cost (including the hidden costs that vendors rarely mention), and the specific scenarios in which each option is clearly right.
```

**Article structure:**
- H2: The real cost of off-the-shelf software in Uganda
  - Licensing, implementation, customisation, training, ongoing support
  - The workaround tax (staff time spent adapting to software that does not fit)
- H2: The real cost of custom development
  - Build cost, maintenance, iteration
- H2: Decision framework: 5 questions
  - Does a system exist that covers at least 85% of our specific requirements?
  - What is the cost of the workarounds required for the remaining 15%?
  - What is the true total cost of ownership over 5 years?
  - Do we have the internal capacity to manage an off-the-shelf system?
  - What is the competitive impact of owning proprietary technology?
- H2: The Uganda-specific factors that shift the balance toward custom
  - Offline capability requirements
  - UGX and local payment method integration
  - Language requirements
  - Non-technical user base
- Case examples from portfolio (Maduuka, Longhorn ERP)

```
Most Ugandan businesses do not have an ICT infrastructure — they have an ICT accumulation. A server bought in 2018 that nobody knows how to maintain.
```
