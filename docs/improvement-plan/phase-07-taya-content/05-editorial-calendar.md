# 05 — Editorial Calendar: 12-Month Publication Schedule

## Overview

A structured 12-month publication schedule covering Phase 7 new articles plus ongoing content maintenance. Publication is one article per week — a sustainable pace for a solo practice with consulting commitments.

The calendar begins from the start of Phase 7 implementation (assumed: May 2026).

---

## Publication Principles

1. **One article per week** — consistent publication signals an active site to Google and builds reader expectation.
2. **Uganda cluster first** — highest intent, fastest ranking wins.
3. **FR cluster second** — near-zero competition, unique advantage, should rank quickly.
4. **East Africa cluster third** — regional reach, builds after Uganda establishes authority.
5. **Case studies last** — require client approval; timing is external.
6. **Interleave new content with article updates** — existing articles benefit from periodic content updates (new data, new links, updated pricing).

| Column | Content |
|--------|---------|
| Week | Publication week number |
| Date | Target publication date |
| Article title | Exact title |
| Slug | URL slug |
| Status | Draft / In review / Scheduled / Published |
| Primary keyword | The target keyword |
| Internal links | Which geo pages and other articles it links to |
| Word count | Target / actual |
| Published URL | Final URL once live |

## Evergreen vs. Dated Content

**Evergreen articles** (do not include a year in the title):
- "How to Evaluate IT Consultants in Uganda"
- "Custom Software vs. Off-the-Shelf in Uganda"
- "How to Find a Reliable IT Consultant in East Africa"

These articles are updated annually with new data but do not change URL.

**Dated articles** (include a year — update annually with a new article or a URL redirect):
- "What Does a Website Cost in Uganda in 2025?"
- "Software Development Rates Across East Africa" (update data annually)

For dated articles: when publishing the 2026 version, update the 2025 article with a visible notice ("Updated for 2026 — see our latest pricing guide") and add an internal link to the new article. Do NOT redirect the old URL unless the content is being entirely replaced — both articles can coexist and both can rank for slightly different search intents.
