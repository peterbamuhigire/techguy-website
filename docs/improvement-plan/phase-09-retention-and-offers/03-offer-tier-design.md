# 03 — Offer Tier Design: Foundation / Growth / Authority Project Tiers

## Current State

No defined project tiers exist. Every engagement is scoped individually from scratch. This creates two problems: buyers who do not know what to expect from an engagement have no frame of reference, and the practice has no systematic way to anchor the conversation around scope and value before a proposal is written.

## Target State

Three named project tiers — Foundation, Growth, Authority — that apply across service areas and give buyers a frame of reference before the diagnostic call. These tiers do not replace individual scoping; they set the context.

## Why This Matters

When a buyer has no frame of reference for what an engagement involves or costs, they either do not enquire (because they assume it is out of budget) or they enquire and experience sticker shock. Naming and briefly describing three tiers — from "first-time ICT engagement" to "full strategic retainer" — filters enquiries toward buyers who understand the scope and surfaces buyers who would self-select to the right level.

## The Three Tiers

### Tier 1 — Foundation

**Who it is for:** Organisations engaging with structured ICT consulting or professional software/website development for the first time. Defined scope, defined deliverable, clear starting point.

**Typical engagement examples:**
- ICT systems audit with written report and priority recommendations
- 5-page professional website with SEO and GSC setup
- Feasibility assessment for a custom software requirement
- Business plan for a single business or funding round

**Scope characteristics:**
- Single deliverable
- Single location
- Up to 3 months' duration
- No ongoing retainer component
- Fixed-price engagement after diagnostic

**Indicative investment range:** UGX 3,000,000–15,000,000 (USD 800–4,100)

**Full copy (for pricing page and proposal context):**
```
Foundation — a single, well-defined engagement for organisations starting with structured ICT or professional development services.

The Foundation tier covers engagements with a clear starting point, a specific deliverable, and a defined timeline. It is structured as a fixed-price agreement: you know exactly what you will receive, when you will receive it, and what it will cost before the work begins.

Typical Foundation engagements: a systems audit, a professional website, a custom software feasibility assessment, or a business plan. Scope is confirmed and agreed in writing before billing begins.

Investment range: UGX 3,000,000–15,000,000 depending on scope. Specific fee agreed after the diagnostic call.
```

---

### Tier 2 — Growth

**Who it is for:** Organisations with a functional technology base that need to extend, improve, or systematically optimise what they have. Multi-deliverable engagements, typically 3-6 months.

**Typical engagement examples:**
- Full ICT strategy with implementation management (not just recommendations)
- Custom web application or ERP implementation
- Website plus SEO Foundation retainer (6-month commitment)
- Multi-country ICT infrastructure improvement
- Full business planning plus investor pitch preparation

**Scope characteristics:**
- Multiple deliverables in sequence
- May involve multiple locations
- 3-6 months' duration
- Often transitions to a retainer arrangement on completion
- Milestone-based billing rather than single fixed price

**Indicative investment range:** UGX 15,000,000–80,000,000 (USD 4,100–22,000)

**Full copy:**
```
Growth — a structured multi-phase engagement for organisations ready to move from planning to implementation.

The Growth tier covers engagements that require more than a single deliverable: implementing a strategy, not just writing one; deploying a system, not just designing it; building a content library, not just setting up a website. Billing is structured around milestones — each phase is scoped, delivered, reviewed, and signed off before the next phase begins.

Growth engagements often transition naturally into a retainer arrangement at completion — the relationship that started with an implementation continues with ongoing support.

Investment range: UGX 15,000,000–80,000,000 depending on scope and duration. Specific milestones and fees agreed after the diagnostic.
```

---

### Tier 3 — Authority

**Who it is for:** Established organisations with complex technology requirements, multiple countries, or the need for ongoing ICT leadership as a strategic function. Typically a combination of project work and retainer.

**Typical engagement examples:**
- Multi-country ICT strategy and implementation with ongoing Fractional ICT Manager
- Full digital transformation programme (strategy, systems, website, content, ongoing optimisation)
- Enterprise software development with long-term support and iteration
- Senior advisory role for board or investment committee

**Scope characteristics:**
- Long-term engagement (6+ months, often 12-24 months)
- Multi-country or enterprise-scale
- Combination of project work and ongoing retainer
- Regular strategic reporting to senior leadership
- Dedicated contact relationship

**Indicative investment range:** UGX 80,000,000 and above (USD 22,000+) — typically structured as monthly commitment plus project fees

**Full copy:**
```
Authority — a long-term strategic engagement for organisations that need ICT leadership as an ongoing function, not a periodic project.

The Authority tier is structured as a combination of project delivery and ongoing retainer. It covers the organisations for which technology is not a periodic concern but a continuous strategic function: multi-country operations, complex systems portfolios, or businesses where ICT decisions directly affect competitive positioning.

Authority engagements begin with a full ICT audit and strategic assessment. They continue as a long-term advisory and implementation relationship. Billing is structured as a monthly retainer with project fees for defined deliverables.

Investment: monthly commitment agreed after full scope assessment. Typically from UGX 6,000,000/month.
```

## Using the Tier Framework in Client Communication

**In the diagnostic call:**
After understanding the client's situation, position the appropriate tier:
```
"Based on what you've described, this sounds like a [Foundation/Growth/Authority] engagement. That means [brief description of scope]. Typical investment for this type of work is [range]. Does that align with what you were expecting to invest?"
```

**In proposals:**
Label the proposal with the tier name. This sets the context before the client reads the price and positions the fee relative to the type of engagement, not as an isolated number.

**In ongoing communication:**
After a Foundation engagement completes, reference the Growth tier as the natural next step:
```
"You have a functional [system/website/plan]. The next logical step for an organisation at your stage is [Growth engagement description]. Would you like to discuss what that would look like?"
```
