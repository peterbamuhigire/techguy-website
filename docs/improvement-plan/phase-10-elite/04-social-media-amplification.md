# 04 — Social Media Amplification: LinkedIn and Twitter/X Strategy

## Skills Applied
- `sales-copywriting` — `references/website-messaging-framework.md` (Social media)
- `east-african-english` — formal register, British spelling

## Current State

Social media exists as a listed service but is not used systematically for the practice's own visibility. The practice's LinkedIn and Twitter/X accounts exist but have no documented publication cadence, content format, or strategic purpose.

From the analysis: "Social media for the practice itself is unstructured. No system for amplifying published content. No LinkedIn thought leadership posts. The practice should model what it sells."

## Strategy

**Primary platform:** LinkedIn — the primary B2B channel for the practice's target buyer (senior managers, directors, technology decision-makers in established East African organisations)

**Secondary platform:** Twitter/X — primarily for thought leadership amplification and AI search citation (AI systems index Twitter/X content)

**Purpose:** Not follower growth. Brand authority and content amplification. Every post either: directs a buyer to a site page, establishes a specific credential, or demonstrates expertise on a topic a buyer would search.

**Cadence:**
- LinkedIn: 3 posts per week (Monday, Wednesday, Friday)
- Twitter/X: 5 posts per week (daily — shorter format)

## LinkedIn Post Templates (5 formats)

### Format 1 — Insight post (the most shareable format)

**Structure:** 1 contrarian or specific insight → 3-5 supporting points → call-to-action

**Template:**
```
[Bold claim or contrarian statement. 1-2 sentences.]

Here is why this matters for organisations in [Uganda / East Africa / Francophone Africa]:

• [Specific point 1 — concrete, not generic]
• [Specific point 2]
• [Specific point 3]
• [Specific point 4 — optional]

[1 sentence conclusion that reinforces the original claim]

[Link to relevant article on techguypeter.com — if applicable]
[CTA: "Agree? Disagree? What has your experience been?"]

#ICTUganda #SoftwareDevelopment #EastAfrica [2-3 relevant hashtags]
```

**Example post (use verbatim or adapt):**
```
Most ERP implementations in East Africa fail before the software is ever opened.

Here is why:

• The business processes were not documented before the system was selected
• The system was chosen because the vendor gave the best presentation — not because it fit the actual workflow
• Staff training was completed in English for a team that works primarily in French
• The "implementation" ended when the system went live — adoption was treated as the client's problem

The technology is rarely the failure. The process is almost always the failure.

I have seen this pattern across pharmaceutical distribution in West Africa, property management in Uganda, and agricultural institutions in Kampala. The pattern is consistent.

More on this: [link to ERP implementation mistakes article]

What has been your experience with technology implementations in Africa?

#ICTConsulting #ERP #UgandaTech #EastAfrica
```

### Format 2 — Case excerpt post (proof in public)

**Structure:** Client situation (anonymised) → what changed → one specific outcome → link or CTA

**Template:**
```
A [sector] organisation operating across [geography] came to this practice with [the problem — specific, not vague].

The situation:
[2-3 sentences describing the before state]

The engagement:
[2-3 sentences describing the approach — no jargon]

The outcome:
[1-2 sentences with a specific, nameable result]

This is the type of engagement this practice is built for.

If your organisation faces a similar situation: [CTA / link]
```

**Example post:**
```
A pharmaceutical distribution business operating across three West African countries had a different ERP version in each country — and no reliable way to see cross-country inventory in one view.

The situation:
Staff in Dakar and Freetown were using different data entry conventions. Monthly reconciliation required 3 days of manual spreadsheet work. Management decisions were made on figures that were, at best, 2 weeks out of date.

The engagement:
A data standardisation phase. Network infrastructure improvements in the weakest office. ERP integration with offline capability. Bilingual (English/French) staff training delivered on-site.

The outcome:
One integrated platform. Cross-country inventory visible in real time. Regional reporting that previously took days now takes minutes.

This is the type of engagement this practice is built for.

Full portfolio: techguypeter.com/en/portfolio/

#ERP #ICTConsulting #WestAfrica #SoftwareDevelopment
```

### Format 3 — Question post (high engagement format)

**Structure:** 1 direct question to the audience → brief context → invite responses

**Template:**
```
[Specific, answerable question relevant to your audience]

[2-3 sentences of context that make the question more specific or interesting]

[Invitation to respond — specific, not generic "what do you think?"]

#RelevantHashtag #Uganda #EastAfrica
```

**Example post:**
```
What is the most expensive technology mistake your organisation has made in the past 5 years?

Not the most dramatic. Not the most public. The one that cost the most time, money, or operational stability — whether or not it was visible to the outside.

I ask this because the most common answer across 15 years of East African ICT engagements is not hardware failure or cyber attack. It is paying for a system that was never fully adopted by the staff who were supposed to use it.

What has been yours?

#ICTUganda #TechLeadership #EastAfrica
```

---

### Format 4 — Article amplification post

**Structure:** 1 compelling line from the article → why it matters → link → hashtags

**Template:**
```
[Quote or specific data point from the article — the most surprising or useful line]

[1-2 sentences of context: why this matters to the specific reader]

Full article: [link] — [2-3 word description]

#RelevantHashtag
```

**Example post:**
```
"Software development costs in Uganda range from UGX 2,000,000 to UGX 200,000,000+ for what can appear, from the outside, to be the same type of project."

Understanding that range — and what actually drives the variation — is the difference between a proposal you can evaluate and one you accept on faith.

Full breakdown (with 2025 UGX/USD figures): [link]

#SoftwareDevelopment #Uganda #ICT
```

---

### Format 5 — Credential/authority post

**Structure:** Specific credential or experience → why it is relevant to buyers → no CTA

**Template:**
```
[Specific credential, engagement, or milestone — stated plainly, not boasted]

[1-2 sentences of why this is relevant to the audience — what it means for their work with you]

[No explicit CTA — let the authority speak]
```

**Example post:**
```
15 years ago this month, I deployed my first ERP system — a pharmaceutical inventory management installation in Freetown, Sierra Leone.

The lesson from that installation has shaped every implementation since: the technology was straightforward. The adoption was the work.

Getting a team to use a system they did not design, in an environment with intermittent power and connectivity, in two languages — that is the actual ICT consulting challenge. Not the software selection.

#ICTConsulting #ERP #WestAfrica
```

## Twitter/X Templates (5 short formats)

Twitter/X posts are shorter and more frequent. The tone is slightly more direct.

**Template 1 — Insight:**
```
Most Ugandan websites load in 8+ seconds on mobile.

Most Ugandan internet users are on mobile.

Most Ugandan websites are invisible to most Ugandan internet users.

This is fixable. [link]
```

**Template 2 — Question:**
```
Quick question for anyone who has hired an IT consultant in East Africa:

Did you ask whether they had vendor affiliations before you hired them?

If not — that is the first question to ask next time.
```

**Template 3 — Data point:**
```
ERP failure rate in Africa: ~60-70% of implementations don't deliver the promised outcome.

The consistent cause is not the software. It's the implementation process.

More: [link to article]
```

**Template 4 — Bilingual credibility:**
```
Conseil ICT bilingue anglais-français en Afrique : presque personne ne le fait correctement.

La traduction d'une interface n'est pas une formation. La formation unilingue pour une équipe bilingue n'est pas une adoption.

La différence se voit dans les résultats.
```

**Template 5 — Positioning:**
```
What "independent ICT consultant" means:

- No vendor commissions
- No incentive to recommend the expensive solution
- No affiliation that shapes the advice

What it produces:

- The recommendation that fits your situation
- Not the recommendation that benefits the consultant

This is rarer than it should be.
```

## Publication Schedule

**LinkedIn — 3x per week:**
- Monday: Insight post (Format 1) or Article amplification (Format 4)
- Wednesday: Case excerpt (Format 2) or Question post (Format 3)
- Friday: Credential post (Format 5) or Article amplification (Format 4)

**Twitter/X — daily:**
- Repurpose LinkedIn content in shorter form
- Tweet thread on article publication day (day of new blog post)
- Engage with replies within 24 hours
