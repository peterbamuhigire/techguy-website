## 🔴 Critical Issues (Fix Immediately)

### 1. All 12 blog posts are missing from the sitemap

The sitemap only contains 14 URLs (7 EN + 7 FR) — none of the blog posts appear in `sitemap-0.xml`. Google cannot reliably discover 12 pages of high-quality content. These pages are effectively invisible to search crawlers.

**Missing URLs:**
- `/en/blog/bankable-agricultural-business-plans-coffee-fish-goats-uganda/`
- `/en/blog/business-must-go-digital/`
- `/en/blog/choose-it-consultant/`
- `/en/blog/custom-software-africa/`
- `/en/blog/erp-implementation-mistakes/`
- `/en/blog/website-costs-uganda/`
- `/fr/blog/*` (all 6 FR equivalents)
- `/en/blog/` index and `/fr/blog/` index

**Fix:** In `astro.config.mjs`, the `@astrojs/sitemap` integration is filtering out dynamic routes or the blog pages are not being picked up. Check that all blog pages use `getStaticPaths()` and that the sitemap `filter` option (if set) is not excluding blog URLs. The blog `index.html` files exist in `dist/` — they just are not in the sitemap. This is almost certainly a `customPages` or `filter` configuration issue.

### 2. GA4 tracking ID is a placeholder (`G-102`)

The homepage contains `G-102`, which is not a valid Google Analytics 4 measurement ID (format: `G-XXXXXXXXXX`). No analytics data is being collected. You have zero visibility into traffic, bounce rates, conversions, or organic search performance.

**Fix:** Replace `G-102` with the real GA4 property ID from https://analytics.google.com. Search the source files:
```bash
grep -r "G-102" src/
```

### 3. No privacy policy page

The site collects data via a contact form and (once fixed) GA4. There is no privacy policy linked anywhere. This is a legal requirement in Uganda (Data Protection and Privacy Act 2019), the EU/EEA (GDPR), and standard expectation for any site using analytics or contact forms. Its absence also reduces trust signals Google uses for E-E-A-T scoring.

**Fix:** Create `/en/privacy-policy/` and `/fr/politique-de-confidentialite/` pages. Link from the footer.

## 🟠 High Priority (Fix This Week)

### 4. Seventeen title tags exceed 60 characters — Google will rewrite them

Google truncates titles at ~60 characters in search results and often rewrites them entirely when they are too long. Key offenders:

| Page | Length | Title |
|------|--------|-------|
| en/blog/bankable-agricultural… | 90 | Writing Bankable Business Plans for Agriculture: Coffee, Fish and Goats — Peter Bamuhigire |
| fr/blog/bankable-agricultural… | 104 | Plans d'Affaires Bancables pour l'Agriculture : Café, Pisciculture et Chèvres |
| fr/blog/website-costs-uganda | 103 | Ce Que Coûte Réellement un Site Web en Ouganda (Et Pourquoi les Prix Varient Autant) |
| en/business-planning… | 81 | Bankable Business Plan Consultant Uganda \| Pitch Deck & Presentation Training |
| fr/business-planning… | 90 | Plan d'Affaires Bancable — Consultant Afrique Francophone \| Pitch Deck & Formation |
| en/blog/erp-implementation… | 82 | ERP Implementation: What SMEs Get Wrong and How to Get It Right — Peter Bamuhigire |
| en/blog/custom-software-africa | 78 | Why African Businesses Need Custom-Built Software Solutions — Peter Bamuhigire |

The pattern is consistent: adding ` — Peter Bamuhigire` to every blog title pushes them over the limit. The brand suffix is worth keeping on core pages but hurts blog articles where the keyword-rich title matters most.

**Fix:** Shorten blog post titles to ≤58 characters, or remove the ` — Peter Bamuhigire` suffix from blog post titles only (the author is already in the Article schema).

### 5. Eleven meta descriptions exceed 160 characters — Google truncates them in results

### 6. Duplicate H3 headings on the Services page

The `/en/services/` and `/fr/services/` pages render the 5-step process headings twice (Discovery, Analysis, Proposal, Implementation, Support × 2). This looks like a dual-column/tab component that renders both in the DOM. Duplicate headings confuse crawlers about content structure and inflate the apparent keyword density of those terms.

**Fix:** Check the process steps component — if it uses tabs or columns, only one set should be in the DOM at a time (hide the other with `display: none` or use Alpine.js conditional rendering).

### 7. Hreflang locale codes are not region-specific

Current: `hreflang="en"` and `hreflang="fr"`
Should be: `hreflang="en-GB"` or `hreflang="en-UG"` and `hreflang="fr-FR"` (or `fr-CI`, `fr-SN` for Francophone Africa targeting)

Using bare language codes without region variants reduces hreflang signal strength. The sitemap uses `en-GB` and `fr-FR` but the in-page `<link rel="alternate">` tags use bare `en` and `fr`. These must match.

**Fix:** In the layout component, change hreflang values to match what is in the sitemap (`en-GB`, `fr-FR`).

### 8. Google Search Console not verified

No `google-site-verification` meta tag found. GSC is essential for:
- Submitting the sitemap manually
- Monitoring crawl errors
- Seeing which queries are driving impressions and clicks
- Receiving manual action alerts

**Fix:** Verify the property in Google Search Console and add the verification meta tag to the BaseLayout.

## 🟡 Medium Priority (Fix This Month)

### 9. Blog posts not in sitemap also affects the blog index and FR blog pages

The `/en/blog/` index page and `/fr/blog/` index page are also missing from the sitemap, not just individual posts.

### 10. Bing Webmaster Tools not set up

No `msvalidate.01` verification tag. As of 2024, Bing powers Copilot and DuckDuckGo searches. For an international business targeting Africa and the UK, Bing/Copilot visibility is material.

**Fix:** Sign up at https://www.bing.com/webmasters and add the verification meta tag.

### 11. Critical fonts are not preloaded

The site loads DM Sans and Playfair Display via `@font-face` with `font-display: swap` (correct), but there are no `<link rel="preload">` hints for the primary font files. This means the browser discovers them late, causing a flash of unstyled text (FOUT) and delaying Largest Contentful Paint (LCP).

**Fix:** Add preload hints in the `<head>` for the primary DM Sans and Playfair Display regular-weight woff2 files:
```html
<link rel="preload" href="/_astro/dm-sans-latin-400-normal.CW0RaeGs.woff2" as="font" type="font/woff2" crossorigin>
<link rel="preload" href="/_astro/playfair-display-latin-700-normal.CuDiGg7c.woff2" as="font" type="font/woff2" crossorigin>
```

### 12. Playfair Display loads unused Cyrillic and Vietnamese variants

18 `@font-face` declarations are loaded, including Cyrillic and Vietnamese subsets. These are bundled into the CSS and downloaded unnecessarily for a site serving East and West African users. The font directory shows these as separate woff2 files (7–23KB each) that inflate the CSS and font payload.

**Fix:** When installing Fontsource Playfair Display, specify subsets:
```
npm install @fontsource/playfair-display
```
Then only import the subsets needed:
```js
import '@fontsource/playfair-display/latin-400.css';
import '@fontsource/playfair-display/latin-700.css';
import '@fontsource/playfair-display/latin-900.css';
import '@fontsource/playfair-display/latin-ext-400.css';
```
Removing cyrillic and vietnamese saves ~55KB of woff2 downloads and reduces CSS by ~4KB.

### 13. GSAP bundle is 112KB — consider splitting or deferring

The GSAP scroll animation bundle is 112KB. For users on mobile connections in Africa, this is significant. If GSAP is used for decorative scroll animations only (not critical interactivity), it should be loaded with `defer` or dynamically imported only when needed.

**Fix:** Audit which GSAP features are actually used. If only ScrollTrigger + a few tweens, you may be able to reduce to GSAP core only (~30KB gzipped) or defer the entire script.

### 14. H1 tags on several pages are too generic

- `/en/services/` → H1: "Services"
- `/en/portfolio/` → H1: "Portfolio"
- `/en/blog/` → H1: "Insights"
- `/en/websites/` → H1: "Websites"

These single-word H1s do not contain the primary search keyword for each page. Google uses the H1 heavily for page relevance signals.

**Fix:**
- Services → "IT Consulting & Software Development Services in Uganda"
- Portfolio → "Software Development Portfolio — Uganda & East Africa"
- Blog → "Tech Insights for African Businesses"
- Websites → "Website Design Portfolio — Uganda & Africa"

### 15. No Service schema on individual service pages

The services page uses `ProfessionalService` (for the entire business) but does not use `Service` schemas for individual offerings. This misses rich snippet potential for specific services.

**Fix:** Add a `Service` schema for each service section on the services page:
```json
{
  "@type": "Service",
  "name": "Custom Software Development",
  "provider": { "@type": "Person", "name": "Peter Bamuhigire" },
  "areaServed": "Uganda",
  "serviceType": "Software Development"
}
```

### 16. Contact page keyword density is flagged at 2.07%

The word "contact" appears 5 times in 241 words on the contact page — 2.07% density. While not severe, combined with the page being thin (241 words, barely above the 200-word minimum), it looks low-effort to crawlers.

**Fix:** Add more useful content: a short paragraph about response time, typical enquiry types, or a "what to expect" section to bring the page to 350+ words.

## 🟢 Low Priority (Nice to Have)

### 17. No dedicated FAQ page

FAQPage schema exists on the business planning page with only 1 question-style heading. A dedicated `/en/faq/` page with 8–12 questions would:
- Capture voice search and conversational queries
- Increase FAQ rich snippet eligibility
- Create internal link targets from blog posts

### 18. Voice search keywords not present

No pages use question-format H2/H3 headings like "What does an IT consultant in Uganda cost?" or "How long does custom software development take?". These match how users speak to Google Assistant and Siri.

### 19. Missing `x-robots-tag` for PDF or printer-friendly assets

No issue currently, but as the site grows (reports, case studies), ensure non-HTML assets in `dist/` use `x-robots-tag: noindex` in Nginx config if they shouldn't be indexed.

### 20. `Article` schema should use `BlogPosting`

Blog posts correctly have Article schema, but `BlogPosting` is a more specific subtype that Google explicitly calls out in its developer documentation for blog content. Use `"@type": "BlogPosting"` instead of `"Article"` for richer categorisation.

### 21. No `sameAs` property in Organization schema

The structured data does not include `sameAs` links pointing to LinkedIn, GitHub, or other verified profiles. This strengthens the Knowledge Graph entity and helps Google confirm the author's identity.

**Fix:** Add to the ProfessionalService schema:
```json
"sameAs": [
  "https://www.linkedin.com/in/peterbamuhigire",
  "https://github.com/peterbamuhigire"
]
```
